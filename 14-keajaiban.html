It was on the evening, when February 14th showed 14 wonders to this disastrous world with a bouquet of exquisite flowers beside a charming bottle of almond water, resting on a beautifully adorned table, accompanied by a small candle that illuminated the day in a place where night always lingered. Without missing a beat, the waiter took up his violin and stood beside the table.

The first wonder, a captivating girl with glossy black eyes like the night sky, sitting at that table. She gazed ahead with a sweet smile that had long gone from her face. Her beautiful blue dress, has now nearly became impossible to find elsewhere. How could there be such a stunning girl in this world? This was the first wonder.

The second wonder, a handsome, muscular man seated across from the girl. His gaze was also fixed ahead, accompanied by a smile that revealed his shining white teeth. The dark black tuxedo he wore seemed to absorb the light around him. With both arms elegantly covered in soft white gloves, he held the girl‚Äôs hand. How could there be such a considerate man for a girl in this world? This was the second wonder.

The third wonder, a simple, ordinary restaurant waiter attending to the man and the girl. He greeted them with warmth and kindness that felt so familiar, as if reuniting with an old friend. He brought a sense of warmth, acting as a complement to the situation; a silent witness to their meeting that evening. How could there be someone so calm and gracious at serving others in this world? This was the third wonder.

The fourth wonder, a violin that produces romantic melodies with its four strings. A waiter held the bow and played with unwavering confidence. The notes released deep feelings of longing and love into the air that no man could resist. How could there still be a violin left in this world? This was the fourth wonder.

The fifth wonder, a bouquet of five stems with each crown adorned in a different hue. The flowers continued to blossom beautifully, capturing every gaze in the vicinity. These flowers could only be found in special places, so precious that even to behold them was a luxury. Their fragrant aroma shifted the air around them into a cool and calming breeze. How could flowers continue to thrive in this world? This was the fifth wonder.

The sixth wonder, a small candle that stands steadfast between the man and the girl. A tiny flame flickered on its wick, providing warmth in the air. The candle strove to resist its fate of fading away; it longed to witness their love story unfold to the very end and warming them both with its light. How could there still be a candle alive to witness the journey of a tale within this world? This was the sixth wonder.

The seventh wonder, the light emanating from the candle that allows them both to see each other's faces clearly. This glow was a testament to the brightness of their future, where together they would create a new light of their own. How could such illumination endure in this darkened world? This was the seventh wonder.

The eighth wonder, a bottle of almond water, tied with a delicate ribbon. Its contents complemented the warm meal, instantly dissolving any awkwardness in the air. The invigorating taste stung the tongue, banishing all hints of drowsiness. A truly remarkable creation of the restaurant that could someday become a menu item. How could almond water be so delightful? This was the eighth wonder.

The ninth wonder, a kalasan roasted chicken displayed before the man and the girl. Its skin gleamed seductively as it reflects the light around it. Devouring it would take a long time, perfectly suited for the long conversation that awaited them. How could such a beautiful roasted chicken still exist in this world? This was the ninth wonder.

The tenth wonder, a heart-shaped necklace emerging from the right pocket of the man's trousers. He held both sides of the necklace and began to move it toward the back of the girl's neck. A blush crept onto her sweet face. How could there still be a man who gifts a girl in this world? This was the tenth wonder.

The eleventh wonder was a bar of RKB chocolate presented to the girl by a waiter at the end of the evening. Taped to it was a heart-shaped note that read, "Will you marry me?" How could a proposal happen in this world? This was the eleventh wonder.

The twelfth wonder, the girl‚Äôs hands started covering the smile on her face, looking back at the man with a bashful expression. She nodded slowly, a gesture signaling her acceptance of the proposal. How could there still be love left in this world? This was the twelfth wonder.

The thirteenth wonder, the man started gently taking the girl‚Äôs hand, placing a ring he had crafted himself onto her right ring finger. A simple inscription adorned the band that read, "Forever." How could a ring find its place in this world? This was the thirteenth wonder.

The fourteenth wonder, today is the final miracle. A day so perfect for giving a gift to someone we love. A beautiful day painted in shades of pink, resonating with a sense of romance. Does such a day truly exist? This was the fourteenth wonder, the Valentine‚Äôs day.
