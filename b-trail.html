Entering hir password was more than just muscle memory these days. Hir hands felt purpose-built for clawing and clacking at the keys — ze imagined hirself pulling the keycaps right out and revealing the pure unadulterated data underneath. It was those types of thoughts that made hir feel like a drone. One with a remote control buried in the sofa.

Ze had tabbed over to the LOG IN button, but hir hands froze into marble before ze could press enter. Hir train of thought no-clipped and landed on the tangentially related memory of hir teenage life on the web back home (though home wasn't one of hir favourite words). Hir old username was "islandshelly" — and what a great username it was, it served hir quite well. From forum to forum ze used to bounce, ze remembered again those fond memories messaging from dawn to dusk, how they used to think hir real name was Shelly, and how much joy it brought hir to—! Ah. Ze was supposed to be working.

After that trip to heaven last month, this nostalgia business happened to hir at least once an hour. Ze already had some sort of undiagnosed executive function problem before entering the Backrooms, and this just added to hir problems getting work done. Truthfuly, ze felt like ripping hir memories out with a toothpick.

Ze changed hir username on the database from "islandshelly" to "the-a-river" the other day to stop hir teenage self screaming in hir microphone.

To hir, nostalgia was like… if you had a favourite program your motherboard used to run you, but you'd cut off your power supply with your manufacturer ID due to major incompatibilities of your SAMEORIGIN policies and now, though you still loved her, you could never really enjoy it again.

Ze pressed enter. Was everything normal again? Ze pretended it was.
