Welcome

Heyo! I'm BlueSignet. I've been writing for a long time! I have a Bachelors of Arts in Creative Writing, and I've had many, many writing-related jobs.

This page holds the pages I've written for the Backrooms. Take a look, leave a vote, and tell me what you think!

Here's my sandbox site if you want to take a look at what I'm working on: http://signetsandbox.wikidot.com/

  • Works
  • Recommendations

Entities

Entity 46 - "Lucky Cranes"

My first entity. I wrote it because there wasn't a mosquito-equivalent entity in the Backrooms. A mild nuisance that could quickly become deadly if gone unmanaged. Also, I'm pretty good at origami, so I wanted to show off my skillz by folding my own models.

Entity 398 - "The Wild Hunt"

My Classicon entry. I wrote it because I do like murder monsters, but I wanted to give them a more modern twist. Yes, they hunt down humans, but not because they're mindless machines of destruction. They're hunting the most dangerous game. Hee hee, and their page number is a reference to the call number of fairy tales and mythology being 398 in the Dewey Decimal System.

Entity 79 - "Wanderers"

My most controversial page, I really wanted to push at the boundaries of what "canon" is. When writing for the Backrooms, I'm always inspired by stories about the fey and humans being in places they shouldn't. In particular, you shouldn't eat food from the fey because then you belong to the fey. In this case, drinking Almond Water erodes your humanity and yet, is a necessity.

People didn't like this page lol

Guides

How to Critique

As part of the Guide and Critique Team, I noticed that there weren't a lot of guides and some people didn't critique because they didn't know how. I've tried to fix both problems with this guide, having experience being a writing tutor and a Greenlighter myself.

Levels

Level 131 - "The curtains are blue"

The curtains are blue.

Level 228 - "Maze of Mirrors"

My first level! I was very excited to finally get a crack at the most popular category on the site. It started with an idea of "The Man Who Died 1000 Deaths." Of course, I had to answer the question, "How did he get that way?" Mirrors that connect you with every possible version of yourself apparently.

Level 398 - "The Grove of Seasons"

For CharCon2025, I wanted to finally get out the home level of the Wild Hunt. I've been working on this on and off for two years, and so the fact that it's finally done lifts a huge weight off my chest.

Anyway, I was obviously inspired by tales of the fae being in court ruled by powerful kings and queens. If y'all haven't picked up on the fae inspirations yet… well here you go :)

I love Herne. He best boi.

Level 356.1 - "Writings on the Wall"

A level for Sublayer Con 2024. On the Discord, there was a bingo and since I'm somewhat infamous there for preferring to work on my own, there was a spot about me releasing a solo page. So being the mischievous person I am, I thought it would be fun to make the ultimate collab page. Also, Nico from SCP reached out and asked if I wanted to collab, and I though it would be fun and promote some interwiki unity :3

Phenomenon

Phenomenon 37 - "Generosity"

My first article ever. Posted during the Phenomena Contest. My idea behind this one was the idea that in video games, you get a suspicious amount of supplies before bosses or hard levels. So, this is that, but in the Backrooms! But obviously, since it's the Backrooms, there are fates worse than death, which is why there's a third option.

Phenomenon 16 - "Destabilizing Storms"

Second phenomenon. I based it off a book I read Storm Thief by Chris Wooding. I wanted to go into the more horror aspects of it, but it ended up being a little bit more subtle. It's solid in my eyes, and working with scutoid studios on the CSS was a blast!

Person of Interest

Herne the Huntmaster

Master of the Wild Hunt, I wanted to do a page for this guy. I wasn't sure if he could be a POI or perhaps Entity 398.1, but regardless, he intrigued me. Praetor pushed me a bit toward POI, and so he became one. I didn't like the typical format of a POI, so I attempted to do a monologue instead.

Tales

Letter from the One You Left Behind

This started as a level that looked like an internet café from the 2000s. It was supposed to be a place that could connect to the Frontrooms and cause all sorts of problems, but I ended up hating that concept. So, it became a tale from the perspective of someone who lost a loved one to the Backrooms. It was longer, but I felt like it was just droning on and on. So I cut it down to 100 words, and it became a drabble!

And then whoops! I fell in love with this character, and created a series of sequels about her entering the Backrooms and waiting for her love.

Letter from the One Who Followed You
Letter from the One Who Waits for You
Is it really cannibalism if they're not human?

Someone was goofing off in the Discord server, and asked the silly question, "What if someone tried to eat a Partygoer?

This page is the answer to that.

The Wretched Writer

Duelcon entry :) I'll be honest, this was mostly me using the wiki to vent out some feelings about my job. You don't realize how mundane your job can get, even if it's "important." So what happens when someone who documents levels has a crashout? Read this page, and mind the content warnings.

DON'T YOU WANT TO GO FUCKING APESHIT

A silly little thing about the Speednoclippers from CN. Not a lot of love from EN about them, but I like the idea of a bunch of adrenaline junkies trying to sprint through levels as fast as they can.

Read good shit, write good shit. Here are some books, short stories, and other pieces of writing I recommend for fun and to become a better writer.

Erm, watch and read at your own discretion. Some of these have sensitive topics.

Books & Short Stories

  • Color out of Space (movie or original short story) by H.P. Lovecraft
  • Horrorstör by Grady Hendrix
  • House of Leaves by Mark Z. Danielewski
  • Woe is I or Woe is I Jr. by Patricia T. O'Conner
  • Annihilation (the movie)
  • The Call by Peadar Ó Guilín

Levels

  • Level 284 - "Where I Want You". A good page that exemplifies having a human element within the page. While the tone itself is neutral, the supplemental documents add the personality and interest to the page.
