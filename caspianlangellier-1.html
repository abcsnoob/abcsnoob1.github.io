Caspian Langellier
rating: +15+–x

Name: Caspian Langellier

Aliases: "Backrooms Bard" (self-titled on occasion), "Casper" (informally)

Last known location(s): Level 1

Known Affiliations: None

An stylized portrait of Caspian; utilized here as he reportedly dislikes having his picture taken.

Caspian Langellier, typically referred to as "Caspian" or "Casper", is an 18-year-old male of Franco-American descent, recognizable by his generally androgynous appearance, with a pale complexion, blue eyes, and dark hair. Caspian wears a variety of clothing and carries a blue-colored backpack with him wherever he goes.

Much of Caspian's fame outside of M.E.G. expedition teams is owed to his prodigious musical talent, which he often utilizes during so-called "concerts". Typically, Caspian will abruptly decide to play a few pieces of music in a certain area, usually in what would generally be considered the central area of a settlement, before finding a comfortable position to lean or sit on. After this, he takes his flute out of his backpack, along with a portable digital speaker (typically in order to play along to a certain melody). Most frequently, a small crowd will gather to listen to melodies of many different kinds from the Frontrooms, as well as his own improvised songs. From observation, the general reaction to his music is a feeling of serenity, and Caspian has received high praise for his musical talent from those interviewed for this article.

He has been known to have played "concerts" in many populated areas of the Backrooms and is reported to have been recently seen making performances in M.E.G. bases several times, primarily the main bases on Level 1, Level 4, and Level 11.

There is another, more intriguing aspect to Caspian Langellier's musical talent—according to numerous sources, his music has a calming effect on conscious and semi-conscious lifeforms, especially those with violent or aggressive intentions or instincts. People listening to his performances have also reported feeling intensely negative emotions seemingly disappear in a manner that cannot be simply explained through Caspian's style of play.

Caspian's flute, a Yamaha FL-677.

Caspian was first brought to the attention of the M.E.G. when several eyewitnesses reported that while being chased by entities, they caught a glimpse of a brown-haired teenager matching Caspian's appearance playing an instrument nearby. As they came into the audible range of the music, they reported that the entities chasing them appeared to grow sluggish and become relaxed, often stopping in their tracks completely. The eyewitnesses themselves also reported feelings of numbness and relaxation during their ordeals, but all still remained aware enough to continue making their escape.

While Caspian's music has described by numerous sources as "intense", "enchanting", and "simply magical", examinations have shown nothing strange, altered, or otherwise anomalous about the qualities of his flute. Experiments have shown that recordings of Caspian's music fail to produce any kind of particular reaction from entities.

Due to this mysterious ability, Caspian, while residing or staying in the main bases of the M.E.G., has occasionally been asked to participate in expeditions into dangerous levels. He has typically tended to accept these requests, under 2 conditions;

1: Caspian is not officially a member of any kind of M.E.G. organization; he is an independent, unaligned figure, doing temporary volunteer work for the M.E.G. and is not beholden to any exclusivity agreement.

2: At minimum, the payment for Caspian's services is a large tin of shortbread, his favorite food. (He typically receives additional items.)

Several would-be entity attacks on scientific expeditions have been prevented as a result of Caspian's presence. Given Caspian's value to both the M.E.G. and wanderers as a whole, he has been designated a notable figure, and his safety has been made a priority of the group.

Personality

Caspian, outside of his performances, is often described as quiet, imaginative, and something of a romantic personality, in more ways than one. He also typically becomes much more avid and outgoing while discussing something in his direct interest, such as art, especially music. In public or at formal gatherings, he also appears much more polite and charismatic.

Within performances, Caspian displays a highly eager, passionate side of himself - many of his extroverted traits, well hidden in his typical shyness, exploding into life. He also appears to be very comfortable performing in front of a stage.

M.E.G. Interview with Caspian Langellier; recorded in order to gain extended firsthand information

Show Interview
Hide Interview

<Begin Log>

Time: 15:21
Date: 04/15/2022
Location: Level 1
Interviewer: M.E.G. Researcher David Clarent
Interviewee: Caspian Langellier

(Researcher Clarent turns on the camera. Immediately, we hear the sound of a flute seemingly playing some sort of Jazz ballad; analysis later reveals this to be a Flautist Cover of Ella Fitzgerald's Moonlight Serenade.)

(The camera pans; we see a group of at least 30 or so people visible in the background, surrounding a chair. On the chair stands a short, brown-haired boy in his late teenage years, wearing a beige shirt and dark pants, moving his fingers and blowing into a metal flute.)

(For about a minute; the camera simply focuses on the boy; so deeply immersed in his music that he does not even open his eyes. The audience simply watches; many of them appearing awestruck.)

(After approximately a minute, the boy finishes his song, lowers his flute, and makes a courteous bow to wild applause. After the applause has died down, he speaks.)

Caspian Langellier: Thank you, everyone, for coming to listen to me today! Erm, if any of you have some supplies or Almond Water, that would be greatly appreciated; just put it in the bucket next to me. Making this music is something of a living for me at this point, you know. No problem if you don't or can't, of course.

(Several people come forward, each one placing materials ranging from Almond Water to varying snacks in a small bucket located next to Caspian's chair. He gratefully thanks each of them verbally; all of them appear to receive a high five.)

Caspian: Again, everyone, thank you so much. If you enjoyed it, do come again!

(As the crowd disperses, Researcher Clarent approaches Caspian, who is packing the received supplies, along with his flute, into his backpack while humming a tune.)

David Clarent: Caspian Langellier, I presume?

Caspian Langellier: (Looks up for a moment and proceeds to look around Clarent before looking at him with a slightly nervous smile.) Uh… yep, that's me.

David Clarent: Ah, hello. I'm David Clarent, a researcher for the M.E.G.; I just want to interview you to learn a little more about you and your… ah, let's say "talents".

Caspian Langellier: Oh, you're here for an interview? Neat. I was a bit worried that I was in trouble for something.

David Clarent: Not at all.

Caspian Langellier: Alright. So what would you like to ask me about?

David Clarent: Well, a few things—if that would be possible.

Caspian Langellier: No problem, I've got a lot of time. Ask away!

David Clarent: Well, we'll start with the easy questions. I think we both know your name already, so I'll move on to the other ones. What would you say your age is?

Caspian Langellier: 18, as of now.

David Clarent: Alright. You're a musician—what would you say your favourite song is?

Caspian Langellier: Easy. Love Theme from Cinema Paradiso.

David Clarent: Alright… I'm finished with the simple ones, we'll move on to the more detailed questions, I suppose. First of all, when did you fall into the Backrooms?

Caspian Langellier: Oh… not too long ago, I think. Maybe about 2 years ago? I remember it was sometime in May. I remember tripping over the stairs in my apartment, and instead of faceplanting into the ground, I ended up here.

David Clarent: Huh… interesting, you've been in the backrooms for quite a short time compared to some of the other notable people we've documented. Would you say that you've gotten used to it?

Caspian: Langellier: I suppose so, yeah. My home life was never really great on Earth. In some way, I suppose getting here was a way to start something new.

David Clarent: So would you say your transition from the Frontrooms to the Backrooms was fairly easy?

Caspian Langellier: (Laughs slightly.) Well, I wouldn't say that. Like everyone else, probably, it was kinda tough to think about the fact that I wouldn't ever get to do, well, a lot of the things I enjoyed, a lot of the things I loved anymore. It's not like this place is any more exciting - it's way less so at times, and pretty much all the exciting levels can kill you. So… at the beginning, all I really had left was the belongings in my bag, which fortunately came with me when I noclipped; and one of those items was my flute.

David Clarent: Did you have a passion for the flute before you noclipped into the Backrooms?

Caspian Langellier: Absolutely. Actually, I really enjoyed playing a bunch of instruments, like keyboard, saxophone, and the like, but I always loved the flute most, and, well, I was really lucky that I had it. Otherwise, I'm not sure what I'd be doing right now.

David Clarent: Speaking of which.. when did you realize you had the ability to calm down, well, a lot of stuff, with your flute?

Caspian Langellier: Er.. it was a few days after I'd noclipped in. It was on this level, actually. I was running out of snacks in my bag, and it felt like I was going in circles.. And of course, the occasional entity was always keeping me on my toes. I was so bored that I sat down for a moment in an area that I thought was clear and opened up my flute case.

David Clarent: Did you not think that playing music here was a hazardous affair, given the hostile entities that appear on this level?

Caspian Langellier: Not really; I was so tired at that point I could barely register a conscious thought, and I wanted to keep my spirit a bit up, at the very least. So I started playing, and it was barely 30 seconds after I started, slowly pacing around the room I was in, that I saw one of those clump thingies behind a pillar. The thing that saved my life.. was that well, I realized that it wasn't moving towards me. The other one I'd seen chased after me immediately. I wasn't sure why; then as soon as I stopped playing to make a run for it, it began to shake itself off and start moving. I managed to get out of there, and a few hours later, I reached this place; Base Alpha.

David Clarent: So did you realize then that your music was calming at that point?

Caspian Langellier: No, it took me a week or so to realize after that; I was pretty tired at the time, after all. I was doing a practice performance, and then within my line of sight I saw like, four or five people having an argument. All of a sudden, they just stopped. Like, all of them just faltered, looked like they were wondering what they were mad about. Then, I stopped for a moment to change my music, and they were all at each other's throat's again. And then, when I started playing a different song, they all relaxed again.. and a few minutes later when I stopped playing that song, they had all forgiven each other. And then later, while I was still thinking about it, I made the connection to the clump I'd seen, and.. yeah, that was when I first had an inkling; I tried it a few more times; every time I played, people or entities seemed to mellow out. Then you guys found out about it, and.. yeah.

David Clarent: Yeah, indeed. So would you say you find reducing these kinds of hostilities, keeping people safe in general, with your music is something that you take contentment in?

Caspian Langellier: Well, of course. It's sometimes a bit tedious, though, to be honest. I prefer playing my music in front of audiences, like the one you just saw, you know?

David Clarent: Fair enough. So how did you start playing music in front of crowds like this?

Caspian Langellier: Well.. after I got to this base the first time around, I started by practicing on my own, just to keep myself from getting bored and to make sure I didn't forget everything. Then someone overheard me playing my flute, and he was the one who suggested that I play in front of an audience. So I tried a flute performance on the "street", or rather, what counts as a street here, and well, a bunch of people loved it. And well.. the rest is history, I suppose.

David Clarent: Nice. So.. I suppose that music has been one of the main reasons you've been able to keep yourself optimistic here.

Caspian Langellier: Yeah, I think so. I love music; if that wasn't obvious already, heh. I think it's a way to bring optimism to not just me, but people who might be struggling in such a cynical and unfair place, or even people who are just tired of such a mundane area - like every art, music gets people's spirits up. It gets them to dream again, to think of something unimaginably more wonderful.

David Clarent: Well… that's a quite beautiful way to put it.

Caspian Langellier: And, you know, the supplies I get from my performances don't hurt either.

(Both Researcher Clarent and Langellier let out a small laugh at this last remark.)

David Clarent: Well, that's all of my questions. Thank you for taking the time to answer these, Caspian.

Caspian Langellier: No problemo, Dave. I'll be heading to Level 11 soon, so if you have anything going on there, come see me play! I try to do different stuff every performance.

David Clarent: I'll check my schedule. And please, don't call me Dave.

Caspian Langellier: Oh. Erm, sorry.

<End Log, 15:42>

Additional Information

  • Outside of accompanying expeditions, Caspian is, as aforementioned, typically either found on safer levels that have a permanent population, or otherwise occasionally found on semi-dangerous levels, reportedly to "find inspiration".
  • Caspian's singing voice, while not as effective as his usage of a flute, also seems to have a calming, relaxing effect on life-forms, suggesting that the calming effect of his music comes from his person rather than his instrument.
  • Along with his musical talent, Caspian claims to have been a practitioner of fencing before he entered the Backrooms. While this obviously cannot be confirmed, he does appear to be highly dexterous and agile.
  • Caspian speaks both fluent English and French, and while his word choice and slang is consistent with American English, he appears to speak with a slight French accent. Whether this is natural or purposeful is unknown.

Addendum

Hey, Everyone! It's your Backrooms Bard here, Caspian.

The M.E.G. invited me to write this addendum - a message to any aspiring music artists, wherever you may be, within or without the backrooms. I wanted to rewrite the whole article (way too much seriousness up there), but they wouldn't let me, unfortunately.

Firstly, choose a instrument that really tickles your fancy. Do some research before you choose, preferably. There's nothing worse than getting an instrument only to realize it doesn't pique your interest, and that you'd rather have had a different one.

Secondly, practice, of course. But don't practice too much; from personal experience, practicing hours at a time over a few days quickly gets you burnt out. Just play consistently, for twenty to thirty minutes a day or so; quality over quantity is always best while practicing.

Thirdly, when you practice, sometimes it's cool to try and imagine one of the musical greats is watching you play, maybe for some sort of audition. I sometimes imagine that Louis Armstrong or Frank Sinatra is watching me while I play one of their songs; it makes it more exciting.

Finally, and most importantly, never lose hope. Even if you feel you're not good, or not improving, for as long as you can, keep going, and if you hold it in your heart, always, always, you'll realize that you're better than you know. Unless you truly know in your heart that it no longer brings you joy, don't give up on your instrument of choice.

Well, that all I have to say. Er, chase your dreams, no matter how impossible they may seem (unless they cause serious danger to yourself). Maybe one day we'll have a full orchestra of musicians in the Backrooms. How crazy would that be?

- Caspian

Is a wanderer's salvation.
