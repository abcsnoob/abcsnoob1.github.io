Against all odds.

Ariane Circle

Years ago, strong minds, true Backrooms survivors from a multitude of small groups joined forces. They understood what mattered most. Faced with a hostile and unknown world, it was necessary to guarantee security and the common good. Thus was born the Ariane Circle, from the desire of several initially insignificant groups to guarantee this security by all possible means. No technology too complex, no method too violent, to guarantee this objective.

Against all odds, despite the dangers, despite death itself, we will fulfill our mission.

We are the bulwark.


The Ariane Circle is a Francophonic group with numerous teams, whose common goal is to guarantee safety for all wanderers within the Backrooms. Thus, they simultaneously lead expeditions to explore and secure unknown levels, testing campaigns and eradication of entities, or war against factions representing a danger to the "common good".

The Circle sees its mission as a sort of “public service,” a vision which often distances it from other groups of interest such as the B.N.T.G. Nevertheless, although they do not seek to create alliances, their goal has led them to approach the M.E.G. Without being directly affiliated with it, the Circle collaborates with the M.E.G. in order to benefit from its database and advanced equipment. In return, the Circle agrees to help the M.E.G. in various operations thanks to its large strike force.

These form smaller teams, which, though all belonging to the Circle, enjoy total autonomy. At the center of the group's organization is the Inner Circle. The Inner Circle is made up of founding veterans as well as brilliant minds with advanced knowledge in many fields. The Circle, due to the difficulty of its mission, is a profoundly technocratic group, ready to develop and use all kinds of weapons and equipment to achieve its ends. This appreciation of technology grew as more promising new minds were hired. Today, each Ariane Circle team operates in their own preferred field, putting their formidable skills at the service, not for a group or an individual, but for the entire Backrooms.

However, not all is perfect in the Circle. Although its facade is seemingly indestructible, internally, there is much tension. The autonomy that teams enjoy is a double-edged sword. Many militias disapprove of each other's methods, while the Inner Circle, entrenched in its old beliefs, experiences difficulty adapting to the new problems of their environment, which is constantly evolving. During the Circle's turbulent history, teams, which only found the Circle to be a source of disillusionment, have entered into conflict and have left, sometimes even becoming the enemy of a cause they had once sworn to defend.

But if there is one quality that the Ariane Circle possesses, it is the ability to endure these trials as best it can. But for how long? If their professionalism no longer needs to be proven, the same is true of their elitism and stagnation. In recent years, the Circle seems to be losing its splendor, its former glory and power seeming to be fading, while some seem content to stay warm in their base, far from the dangers and from their mission.

History

The Ariane Circle was founded in June of 1998, when two small French-speaking groups decided to join forces in order to better fulfill their common vision: the creation of a powerful and expert faction capable of helping the most deprived wanderers in the face of the multiple dangers of the Backrooms. Although each used different methods, they commonly decided to ally themselves, pooling their abilities and resources to ensure their effectiveness. Gradually, several other groups merged with them in a desire to join their strength and their vision for this noble common cause. In three years, the Ariane Circle already included eight teams, led by eight veterans who worked together to guarantee the safety of wanderers.

Over the years, the group has grown and diversified in terms of focuses and action. It was also able to come into contact with other groups, strengthening its resources and its notoriety. However, from the beginning, the veterans were often in conflict over which methods to use to achieve their goals. It is because of these numerous disagreements that, on September 23, 2003, the Level 4 agreements (named after the place of ratification) were promulgated, where all the teams existing at that time committed to being self-sufficient. They kept to themselves, giving no orders to others and having no weight in the discussions of other teams. However, instead of improving relations between them, these agreements have only deepened the differences between the teams and the veterans themselves, although they were allies in a common struggle. However, this did not prevent the group from developing extremely efficiently, recruiting and training new individuals and introducing them to the customs of the Circle, but only accepting the elite of what the Backrooms could offer in terms of soldiers.

The history of the Ariane Circle is rich in highlights and key dates for the future of the group. Most events generally only include two to three teams and therefore have not had much effect on the future of the Circle over the years.

Structure

The Ariane Circle is not a singular institution like the M.E.G. or the B.N.T.G. On the contrary, it is made up of a multitude of subgroups called teams. Each team was created to fulfill a specific role, and is therefore master in a particular area. The Hermès team is, for example, a specialist in adapting in unfamiliar terrain and in improvisation, while the Thanatos team is a master in everything relating to combat against hostile entities and arming with rustic equipment, trying to replicate as best as possible the initial mission of the Circle.

Each team evolves independently for its own sake. This means that, in their nature, no team can force another to perform this or that action. This directive was initially decided in order to prevent them from slowing each other down and thus let them expand on what they do best. However, as the Ariane Circle developed, it became clear that this direction prevented any checks and balances or higher authority in the event of internal conflict between the teams.

The only general institution of the Circle is the Council of Veterans. This was originally a small group made up of the initial creators of the first Circle teams. However, as the number of teams increased, the Council expanded to include the leaders or representatives of each team. This Council decides on the future of the Circle as a whole—for example: deciding on the creation of new teams. It also happens that they implement sanctions against teams or individuals, but because of the differences in residential levels, the sentence can only be recognized by those who accept it. This brought its share of problems, leading to what could amount to a civil war in the worst cases, notably during the dissolution or de-officialization of a team that was judged to be too radical or too dangerous towards the Circle or its initial mission.

Strengths and Weaknesses

The main strength of the Ariane Circle is undoubtedly its professionalism. Even if the unique status of each team has over time reduced the capabilities of each of them in terms of pure combat, they nevertheless remain unconditional masters of their fields. This allows the Circle, to some extent, to be extremely versatile.

However, this versatility only works in theory. Indeed, without an institution of higher authority, no one on the board can force a team to engage in a fight that they refuse. Although, in most cases, teams execute on an as-needed basis. It happens often that a team refuses a mission entrusted to it for various reasons (need for rest, rivalries, or simply a belief that they are not capable of carrying it out). This remains rare, but tensions still occasionally arise following the refusal of one team to support another.

Additionally, due to the lack of higher authority, each team over time began to refocus on its own interests rather than those of the group as a whole. Indeed, although they keep the same overall mission of helping and protecting wanderers within the Backrooms in mind, the methods used for this are far from unanimous. Even worse so, it happens that two teams can come into conflict over equipment or an outpost, or following more vague reasons such as a "humiliation." The Council of Veterans tries as best it can to resolve these conflicts, but the fact that each team does not have to interfere in the work of the others makes any punishment or sanction impossible, in addition to exacerbating the conflicts between the teams.

Finally, it is important to mention that with all these conflicts, the Ariane Circle seems, for several years, to stagnate in terms of professionalism and capabilities. Indeed, if the group as a whole continues to be relatively respected, it is clear that its former image no longer suits the current Circle, which, due to a lack of collaboration and conflicts of vision, struggles to evolve at the same time in the Backrooms as a whole, which risks, in the long term, harming their mission and the group itself.

Team Apollo

Team Apollo Team is the scientific branch of the Ariane Circle, gathering experts in all fields of science for the common goal of unveiling the secrets of the Backrooms, especially for Objects and Entities. Despite the friendly atmosphere and their sense of camaraderie, they remain extremely efficient and always manage to find a solution, whatever the mystery or problem that presents before them. The team was founded in 1999, initially led by Regina Lancelot, before her promotion to the Council of Veterans. It was then that she appointed Dr. Julian Archange, a renowned scientist respected by all the Backrooms scientific circles, including the M.E.G. testers' division and even rival groups such as U.E.C.'s Circe research team, to take over leadership of the Team Apollo and carry out its various projects.

In addition, they generally supply the Ariane Circle with advanced technologies, items and equipment used in their various operations.

However, to join their team, an in-depth knowledge in one or several scientific fields is expected.

Team Hermes

Team Hermes is a subgroup of the Ariane Circle specialized in the exploration and mapping of unknown levels or those whose nature is poorly understood. It is made up of particularly trained scouts, capable of evolving and adapting to all types of environments and situations. We owe them in particular for the mapping of Level 64 and Level 94. The team was also the first to discover the Arms, as well as the cult attached to them. Their ability to explore and secure new levels is beyond compare to that of other groups, which leads to the M.E.G. sometimes asking them to support some of their incursions into little-known areas. Most of the relationships that the team maintains with other groups or teams of the Ariane Circle (such as with the Team Thanatos, which Hermes has supported on several occasions) are positive. However, this team is in moral conflict with Team Hippocrates for reasons long forgotten but whose repercussions are still visible when the two teams must fight side by side.

Team Hippocrates

Team Hippocrates is a team composed of doctors, surgeons and biology researchers. It aims to study the anomalous biology of the Backrooms and offer help to groups and wanderers in medicinal fields.

From the start, Team Hippocrates has been comprised of former medical students, lab technicians, surgeons, nurses or doctors, who all possess medical knowledge that comes in handy in a world as unforgiving as the Backrooms. While replicating the Frontrooms medical system was impossible, the team has managed to help many groups set up hospitals or treatment centers, saving many wanderers from infected wounds or the need for surgery.

In addition to this, Team Hippocrates has also realized many research projects, such as the studying of Staphyolococcus liminalis with the M.E.G. and The Doctors, Organiguns, or the determination of the Habitable Zone and Terror Hotel ecosystems. Thanks to their past and current work, entity understanding has heavily increased with time, allowing for developped tactics and measures against them.

For reasons lost to time, Team Hippocrates also holds a mutual deep grudge against Team Hermes. Despite the efforts of Team Pytheas to remove this hatred by operative exchanges between teams in 2016 (something that worked well at first), the tensions came back stronger than ever after the events of Level 8-FR started a chain reaction culminating with the open cobflict of the Contested Base.

Team Pytheas

Team Pytheas is a Circle team responsible for communication in its entirety. Its main missions are three in number.

  • The first is the collection of information from newly discovered levels, entities, etc. to populate the group's database and update it, which leads members to often come into contact with the Hermès team and other teams within the Circle.
  • The second mission is diplomacy. In the eyes of other groups, Team Pytheasis the voice of the Ariane Circle, which puts enormous pressure on its members, who are mainly responsible for maintaining good relations between the Circle and the M.E.G., but the team still does its best to maintain stable links with other groups. Indeed, the slightest mistake can potentially create strong tensions or deprive the group of valuable resources.
  • The third and final mission of the team is communication between the different teams of the Circle and the Council of Veterans, with the aim of maintaining the little cohesion still remaining between the different bodies of the Ariane Circle. Given the complexity of some of its missions, the team only accepts people who have worked in communication and diplomacy, which poses a problem when the team must go through numerous levels of the Backrooms to accomplish their mission.

Team Pytheas, despite its almost capital importance, has a very fragmented structure. In fact, the team is divided into three sections, each responsible for one of the three main missions and led by a section manager. During missions requiring the entire team, the three section leaders give their opinion on the procedure to follow, but it is always Pytheas who has the last word on decisions. Pytheas is the founder of the team, and he created it following the emergence of numerous groups possessing resources useful to the Ariane Circle. Over time, the team has gained importance, but this makes internal management more complex than in other Circle teams.

Team Thanatos

Team Thanatos is the armed wing of the Ariane Circle, specializing in armed conflicts, rescue and supply operations, the extermination of entities, and all situations that require a significant strike force. It is only possible to join Thanatos through the recommendation of an eminent member of the group or a high-ranking member of the Council. Thanatos is made up exclusively of people with a history of military conflicts, war zones, dangerous areas/districts or a violent past. The current members of Thanatos come from diverse and varied contexts which justify their presence; here are some of them:

  • Coming from a country at war.
  • Originally from a neighborhood considered a “hot zone”.
  • Veteran from the military.
  • Municipal police officer on duty.
  • Forest firefighter.

Thanatos' mission is to provide tactical support for the entire Circle. Initially, no team was considered the armed hand of the Circle, the members having all received training in the handling of bladed weapons and firearms; however, with the evolution of the group and the increasingly present dangers, the creation of Thanatos became a necessity.

This team was therefore created in order to resolve said problem. Each team has the possibility of summoning members of Thanatos in the exercise of a mission requiring armed force or protection, Thanatos has a right of refusal depending on the request, and the members are not subject to the hierarchy of other teams.

Thanatos is not governed by any hierarchy; each operator has a voice which must be heard by the others and vice versa, under penalty of heavy sanctions. It is possible that operators arbitrarily elect a team leader when the situation requires it. Thanatos operators have access to additional resources such as daily rationing, a communications system and access to almost all of the Circle's weaponry and equipment as well as all installations that may have military interest.

Operation Thanatos is the team's current priority: it allows other teams and all wanderers to have more or less reliable data on the resistance of the numerous entities dotting the Backrooms. This is a long-term mission and must not trample Thanatos' primary missions. It is common, in this context, to see collaboration with other teams or groups (M.E.G/U.E.C/B.N.T.G) or, more rarely, with PoIs.

The importance of the Ariane Circle has led it to interact with other groups through the Backrooms. While some of these meetings were fruitful, with both groups sharing a common cause or goal, other factions were quickly seen as potential dangers, with very strained relations. The levels of trust towards each group are as follows:

● — The group is friendly and works in conjunction with the Circle. Diplomacy with them is therefore important as long as it does not hinder the interests of the Circle.
● — The group maintains relations with the Circle, but these remain distant and strictly professional. No trust is placed in them and the Circle is ready to retaliate in the event of a problem.
● — The group does not interact with the Circle, but is seen as a potential risk. Any meetings should be done sparingly in order to avoid any conflict. The actions of these groups must be reported to higher authorities in the event of a problem.
● — The group is in open conflict with the Circle due to a great difference of opinion. Any meeting must be considered as a confrontation and must be escalated to higher authorities.
● — The group no longer exists or has not come into contact with the Circle. Any meeting must immediately be escalated to higher authorities.

● Backrooms Remodeling Co.

The Ariane Circle interacts little with the Remodeling Company, their professionalism regarding terraforming often being called into question. It has happened that the AC has called on their expertise, in particular their more or less effective mastery of the Blue Consumption, but their methods and their members are not sufficiently reliable for the group.

● Backrooms Robotics

The Backrooms Robotics firm pursues objectives that are far too suspicious for them to be considered allies of the Ariane Circle. It happens that exchanges between AC and BKR take place, but these are extremely rare and are only carried out with total distrust. The agents must at all costs be careful with their technology which, although attractive at first glance, can prove to be as dangerous for the Circle as for its enemies.

● The Black Knights

The Ariane Circle initially had no relationship with the Black Knights or their parent company, Heimwehmut Industries, and had only assisted the M.E.G. in their research of them for a short period of time. However, due to recent events, tensions among the Knights and the Circle have grown, and members of the Circle are now authorized to terminate Knights upon encounter.

● The B.N.T.G.

Despite the Circle's claimed autonomy, the phenomenal collection of material and resources possessed by the B.N.T.G. cannot leave it indifferent. Trade exchanges have in fact been established and help the AC to establish its power with other factions. Although relatively dependent on goods provided by the B.N.T.G., the Circle minimizes these interactions, because the lure of profit makes the B.N.T.G. a fairly dangerous group, even if they proclaim their neutrality.

● The Eternal Repository

The links between the Circle and the Repository go no further than trade. Just like the B.N.T.G., the Eternal Repository interacts with the AC only for commerce, but this time for the sale of information. Although they have never posed a problem, the lack of knowledge about them makes them a potential danger.

● The Eyes of Argos

Although relations between the Circle and the Eyes were cordial at first, they quickly deteriorated, as the Eyes of Argos, in their quest for justice, put many wanderers, some of whom are innocent of the crimes of which they are accused, in direct danger. Several Circle agents were assassinated by this group. It must be neutralized.

● The Followers of Jerry

The sectarian religion of the Followers of Jerry, as well as their fanaticism, has several times proven to be extremely problematic for the Ariane Circle during certain operations. Encounters are rare, but teams must be prepared to respond with force to their attacks. They are too deep in their proselytism to be saved, and their religion could very well pose a major problem to the population if it becomes widespread, subjecting humanity to this strange blue bird.

● The Kalag Institute

Few contacts exist with the Institute, because their work does not overlap with the Circle's missions. However, it happens that the two groups come into contact to exchange information on certain deaths within the AC.

● The Lost

Few members of The Lost's multiple ancient cultures have come into contact with the Ariane Circle. They are often ignored, as posing no real threat to the rest of the humans in the Backrooms, but their knowledge of these dimensions has already proven vital during certain operations.

● The M.E.G.

Although the ambitions of the M.E.G. differ from those of the AC, their way of acting as well as their common desire to protect have brought the two groups close together. Although they are only “partners”, each agrees to collaborate, and the M.E.G. makes its database and part of its equipment available, while certain Ariane Circle teams agree to lend them a hand if necessary.

● The Masked Maidens

Although the Maidens primarily fight the M.E.G., their perception of the groups could well cause problems for the Circle if they come to view the various teams or the Council as "corrupt". Their actions must be monitored, because the Circle's links with the M.E.G. could well make the Masked Maidens an enemy.

● The Pantheon

The Ariane Circle has no relationship with the Pantheon per se, but some ties exist with particular groups created to serve or destroy the avatars, a notable example being the Eyes of Argos.

● The U.E.C.

The military and extremist tendencies of the U.E.C. pose a major problem for the safety of all wanderers in the Backrooms. Due to odious and violent acts committed against other human beings and the threat they represent to public order and values ​​in total opposition to those of the Circle, the AC teams obtained authorization to neutralize U.E.C. members in case of contact.

While we refuse to gatekeep this group, we aim at a collaborative project for it, which means we would like people trying to use it to ask questions to the French branch if needed. We have an international channel on our discord, so come see us, and don’t worry, we don’t bite! We will gladly help anyone trying to use the group, as it is exactly the kind of collaboration spirit we wish to see.

The Ariane Circle wants to be very permissive in the creation of its universe. Therefore, you are free to create your teams as long as they do not conflict with already existing ideas or dates. If you want to use already created teams, it is strongly recommended to ask the creator in order to avoid contradictions.

The identity of the Circle is a professional and well-known organization, but is in decline due to its lack of overall cohesion. For this reason, it may be a good idea to involve multiple teams when presenting conflicts. It is also entirely possible to create renegade or refractory teams, which hardly, if at all, follow the objectives of the Council of Veterans for various reasons.

Apart from these generalities, you are free to write as you want, but certain things must be respected:

  • Try as much as possible to respect the work of others. The AC is intended to be a cohesive project, so it is recommended to do your research so as not to contradict other pages on the group's history.
  • Collaborative work is encouraged, especially if your page has a certain importance in terms of its universe. You can use the “community project” channel on the FR server to discuss your work.
  • No self-insertion within the group. A “self-insert” is the incorporation of oneself into the universe, often by creating a sublimated version of the author.
  • The use of the Ariane Circle theme is not obligatory, you are free to use other themes if you consider it more useful for the story. Just avoid going without a theme, for the sake of in-universe logic.
