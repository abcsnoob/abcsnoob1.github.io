Custodian Policy

What is a custodian?

A custodian is a person that holds all permissions for a few if not all the pages from a specific author. Those permission has been granted by the author themselves, and allow the custodian to basically edit, delete or rewrite any page they have custody upon to their liking. They basically manage everything about said pages (rewrite permissions, edition permissions and deletion permissions), until the original writer chooses to take them back if needed.

Why have a custodian?

If you are going away from the community for some time, or even forever, having a custodian on the wiki can be beneficial. Indeed, this allow people to contact someone easily if they have questions or if they need to for specific reasons (like expanding the lore of your pages, or asking permission for a rewrite). This also allows your work to be in the hand of someone that can be active enough to work on it if needed. On staff behalf, this also helps them when a change on the wiki requires contacting the owner of the page.

How to assign a custodian?

Simply tell the person, as well as staff! This allows both parties to make sure the custody is enforced. You can give custody of your pages to multiple people at once, to staff or even the greenlighter team.

Note that if you are banned from the wiki permanently, staff will ask you to assign an active custodian on the wiki, or to give custody of your pages to the greenlighter team as a whole. This doesn't apply to deleted pages. This doesn't apply for bans without appeal, as the permission will go to the GLMP.

WARNING - A custodian has a page limit. They can either manage 30 pages from different authors, or as many pages from one author. If the threshold is exceeded, the custodian will be able to choose what to keep to come back in the limit.

If the custodian is banned, the permissions will come back to the original owner, or GLMP in the case of when the original writer is banned without chance for appeal.

Can I change custodian?

You can! If you come back to the community, or if you feel like the custodian is abusing their rights, you can contact staff, who will modify the permissions to give them back to you. However, make sure to choose someone trustworthy in the first place!

Remember that you can still request to have some of your pages — or all of them — be on the no-rewrite list, even with a custodian. This means that the aforementioned custodian will still not be allowed to rewrite or give rewrite permissions to your pages. This doesn't apply to collab pages or rewritten pages.

Additional Rules Regarding Co-Ownership

In the case of co-written works, such as drafts or published articles, you need at least more than half of the custodians (whether they are the original writers or the custodians of said writers) to agree on posting, rewriting, or deleting the article in question. For example, in the case of a page co-written by two members, both need to agree in order for something to happen. In the case of three authors, at least two authors/custodians would have to be for a posting/rewrite/deletion. The only exception is for specific situations involving permabanned users, where staff can settle the issue if it heavily influences the site.

Specifically speaking, keep in mind that the custodian has "ownership" of the contribution the writer they have custody of made (so the parts they wrote). In total, each writer or custodian has an equal "share" of the overall article ownership, even if the parts aren't equivalent in terms of length (for example, in a two-person collab, each writer/custodian owns 1/2 of the overall permissions).

NOTE: If you have been permanently banned without any chance for appeal before the release of this policy, the permissions have been transfered to the GLMP. You cannot take the permissions back for yourself, but you can assign a custodian if you like.
