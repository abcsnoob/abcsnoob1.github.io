logout

Goodbye, Archivist Medio. Logging out…

Medio sighs to themselves, stretching their arms and cracking their knuckles as they watch the computer screen power off, leaving them in relative darkness once more. A necessity, given how much more… attentive the M.E.G. had gotten lately when it came to the whereabouts of their personnel.

maybe i've been too hasty.

The Archivist shakes their head and looks around. Straining their human eyes to look around in the dark hurts. They're not used to this level of weakness, this amount of restriction. They used to be limitless. They should've been limitless.

but my real body is in heaven, they think to themselves as they stand up. probably for the best. Grabbing their discarded coat, they throw it over themselves, feeling the fabric on the parts of their arms not covered by their shirt. Despite knowing what they're going to do today, they feel… apprehensive.

…if something goes wrong today, i won't have to bear the brunt of the consequences. The thought is reassuring to them. i'll be fine. the m.e.g. can't hurt me. they can't hurt this body or my real one back in heaven.

it's time to bring these wayward humans back to heaven, where they belonged this whole time. Medio Gaius slowly walks out of the dark room that she's been secretly doing her work in all this time, leaving behind the stolen things and equipment for the last time, as she steels herself for what is next. i'll make this quick.

It's a beautiful day outside. The sun is shining, the clouds cover portions of the park every now and then with their shade, and the M.E.G. personnel are as relaxed as ever.

Which is why they're totally unprepared when the sky begins to swirl in on itself and distort, before fragmenting into a hole in the fabric of the level itself and revealing the first few glimpses of heaven above.

The Archivist calmly walks outside, summoning a neon pink javelin in her waiting hand, as she watches the humans in the distance scatter at the sight of the skybox falling apart.

in heaven, everything is fine.

INCOMING VIDEO FEED FROM SECURE SECURITY OPERATIVE HANK S.

Operative Hank fumbles with the video camera, hurriedly pointing it at the sky as he takes a few steps back. "Fuck. Fuck, fuck fuck fuck fuck." His breathing is loud enough to overpower most other sounds, although the screaming and audible distortion of space still filters through. "This level just went to hell."

"Why weren't we told about any of this!?" Hank looks around, showing the distorting sky and the static slowly spilling through several rips in the cloud of the level. "We're barely armed as is, since this level's too damn quiet to warrant any heavy firepower. Or firepower of any kind, I guess. WHAT THE FUCK ARE A FEW PISTOLS SUPPOSED TO DO AGAINST THAT?!"

The camera focuses on a figure in the sky, the clouds looming overhead and casting the park in a dark blue glow as the figure slowly flies around, surveying the park. Rips in reality open and close around them as they do so.

A bullet seemingly strikes them in the chest. With an inhuman speed, they summon a neon pink javelin in their hand and throw it at the ground. The camera follows the javelin, and it continues to fly down for a split second before hitting a human armed with a pistol on the ground. The human screams as they get impaled, dropping the gun and going limp.

"…Shit…"

The camera points back up at the figure in the sky. The figure, now somewhat more visible, turns and slowly flies over to Operative Hank, a smile breaking out on their face as they raise their shaking arms mockingly.

"try it." They sneer, as their voice is slowly overtaken by a more feminine, more regal sounding one that echoes throughout the park.

Hank stumbles backwards, jostling the camera with every step taken as he continues to focus the camera on the figure.

you can't. : - ) but you'll try anyways.

"Fuck, fuck fuck fuck. Bastard! Come down here and face us like-"

The figure quickly summons another pink javelin in her hand and prepares to throw it at the cameraman. For a second, she seems to hesitate, holding it for a few seconds as her smile falters.

"Shit. Worth a shot-"

Then, in the next split second, she throws the javelin at the camera. As soon as its gleaming tip becomes visible, an armored figure suddenly runs into view and takes the hit, blocking the camera's line of sight with the unknown entity.

"Huh?" Operative Hank moves the camera a bit higher to get a good look at the figure. "Oh. Oh. No fucking way. It's you…"

The figure in the sky scowls as the armored man takes the javelin out of his chestplate and throws it to the ground. Without even addressing Hank, he looks up at the entity and takes out a shining red blade. "Gaius."

Operative Hank takes a few more steps back, not wanting to get caught in the cross-fire as the two stare each other down. "claudius. i didn't think you'd come to the rescue of these humans, though given your occupation, i should've expected you to interfere."

"You know, I thought you'd be using my new name by now." The knight twirls his sword in his hand as he paces around her.

Gaius follows his every move, no longer focusing on Hank as she summons a bright pink spear in her hand. "you don't deserve to hide behind the moniker that you've bestowed upon yourself."

"And you do?" He tilts his head at her. "Gudang?"

"bastard!" Gaius summons a volley of pink javelins behind her and rains them down on the Red Knight, who barely flinches as they impact the ground around him and nick his armor. He slashes the air his sword, deflecting a few as he slowly closes the distance. "don't you dare call me that! you caused her to die when you killed my husband!"

"It's only fair, isn't it?" The Red Knight jumps into the air, briefly hovering in front of Gaius before striking her with the pommel of his sword and causing her to slam into the ground. As he falls back down, he calls out to her.

"You won't call me by my chosen title, so I won't call you by the name that you've chosen to hide behind as you massacred these humans and tormented them for decades. We will both have to face the consequences of our actions as ourselves." He looks around morosely, briefly locking eyes with Hank before looking back at the mound of dirt that Gaius kicked up during her fall to earth.

"you hypocritical rat." Gaius slowly flies up, raising a trembling hand as she summons more pink javelins behind her from small portals in the sky.

"weren't you the one who caused all this? you attacked my husband! you stole my son's parents from him!" A few of the javelins fly at the Red Knight, who nonchalantly strikes them out of the sky with a swipe from his sword.

"you're the reason i'm such a bitter wreck after everything!"

"That may be." The Red Knight opens a portal beside him with a flick of his blade, before balling his hand into a fist and pushing into the hole in space-time as another one opens up beside Gaius.

"But unlike you, I don't go around terrorizing humans who've had nothing to do with our quarrels." His fist shoots out of the portal and strikes Gaius in the cheek, causing her to briefly stumble through the air before regaining her balance as his hand retreats and the portals close. "Nothing can justify this."

"oh, claudius. always so eager to please people. now that you can't please the two you've killed, you're resorting to pleasing these humans instead." Gaius shakes in rage as the sky breaks and distorts above her. "such… heroic… nonsense!"

The Red Knight deflects another javelin. "Let them go, Gaius."

She sneers as another barrage of javelins fly at the knight. "i won't. you'll have to free them yourself!" The entity laughs mockingly as she hovers in the air. "we both know you're too weak to kill me! you wouldn't dare murder your dear sister a second time."

"…You're right. I won't. I refuse to kill what remains of my family." He glares at her as the javelins impact the ground around him. "However, I won't hesitate to free the puppet that you've made out of their body." The Red Knight suddenly runs towards her, briefly disappearing before reappearing in front of the entity in the blink of an eye as he raises his sword. "I'm sorry."

Gaius gives a disbelieving laugh. "hah. you're sorry? i don't accept your-"

"I was talking to the human that you possessed." The sword begins to cut through her neck. "Goodbye, sister. Goodbye, human."

"no!" Gaius knocks the Red Knight to the ground with a swing of her fist, the air around them literally cracking with the force of the blow as fragments of the level's sky fall around them. "you can't-!"

Suddenly, she stills, her breath hitching as the anomalies around them slowly flicker out of existence. "Claudius?" The new voice is different softer. It is human.

The Red Knight stands. "River?" He leans on his blade for support. "How did she-?"

"I don't know, and that doesn't matter right now. I can't hold her back forever." The entity remains still in the air, looking around the park-turned-battlefield. "…You know what to do, Claudius. Free them."

Slowly, the Red Knight nods and launches himself back up into the air. "Thank you, River." He makes a quick cut with his sword, and the level seemingly holds its breath as he falls back down to earth with the body still above him.

Then, the body falls, and the level slowly returns to normal, with glitches slowly mending themselves and the clouds in the sky defragmenting and flying away. The sun shines down on the bloodstained field of the park.

"I hope you're free now." Without another word, the Red Knight opens a portal with his sword and walks through, leaving nothing behind as the portal slams shut.

"…Well. Shit." Operative Hank sighs as he slowly makes his way to the body, zooming the camera in on the M.E.G. uniform on it. "I guess we now know how that bastard managed to sneak past us…"

END OF TRANSMISSION








Gaius tiredly opens her eyes, seeing the fragmented skybox of paradise above her. The shoddy coding of what was supposed to be their shared home genuinely hurts for her to look at. With a sigh, she sits up, dusting herself off and straightening out the creases of her toga as she looks around.

"…river," the marble woman grumbles, narrowing her eyes as she imagines the damn human she's fused with standing before her. "i should've worked harder on breaking you."

The side of her face cracks, before falling away to reveal human skin underneath. "You kind of ran out of flaws to throw at me around the 5-year mark," ze snarks. "Face it, Gaius. We're stuck together, and I'm going to make sure you can never do anything like this ever again."

Gaius snorts despite herself, giving a small, wicked smile to her counterpart. "we'll see about that." Her smile slowly falls. "damn you, claudius…"

There's short, brief silence between them as the two stare off into different directions, not knowing what to say. Finally, River mumbles, "You could've just… left. You knew the odds were stacked against you, and he's your broth-"

"i don't need you lecturing me," she snaps, feeling her anger resurface as she abruptly stands up and starts walking into a nearby fragmented forest. The marble woman expertly avoids tripping over the glitched roots sticking out of the ground at odd angles, slightly marveling at the sight as she ponders. "besides, you and i both know he wouldn't let me run amok with my very own puppet to spy on your people with."

Her mouth slowly curls down as she stares at the static-filled ground. With a half-lidded eye and a twinge of grief at remembering what led her, her paradise, and her family to this point, she balls her hands into fists. "and… he's not my brother anymore. not after what he caused all three of us to go through."

Before River can dare preach to her again about hir human values, Gaius forcefully shuts hir up by pushing hir back into the deep recesses of herself, the marble creeping back over her face and restoring her visage once more. river. please. just leave me alone.

Mercifully, the former archivist does not attempt to speak hir mind again after that. With a sigh, Gaius leans against a nearby tree, and, after looking at the godrays peeking through the digital treetops, goes back to sleep.
