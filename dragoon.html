Red-eyes Dragoon
rating: +36+–x

SURVIVAL DIFFICULTY:

Class Dragoon-Author

  • Goofy Articles
  • Silly Shennanigans
  • Expanding Itself

This was a commission made by Star Saphire1!! They're too Awesome.

Well hi there, if you're here, it's either because you're reading all of my works at once, or you're randomly scrolling through the endless supply of author pages, looking for something interesting.

Anyway, welcome to my author page, I'm Red-eyes Dragoon, but discord members might recognize me as Monster9530; a 19 year old writer, admin, and greenlighter for the Backrooms Wikidot!

Anyway, I'm sure you're mostly here for my articles so I'll let you look through those! In-case you're wondering, my current favorite to least favorite are: E92, L297, Phenom80, Phenom 90, L182, E326, L402, L326, T-OFS, T-TSGTTA, L282, L401

My Articles2:

Entity 326: "Cuddlemares" +28

  • A weird entity that can possess toys- pretty interesting. This entity adapts depending on the toy it takes during its metamorphosis process.
Open thoughts
Close thoughts

It's kinda surreal that this was my first article. I originally intended for this to be my only article- like a one-and-done kind of thing. Of course, the longer I wrote trying to get this in, the more and more ambitious I grew. It helped that I was making friends on TS while writing.

Level 182: "Aromatherapy" +56

  • A large greenhouse with an infinite outdoor area- no monsters- although, that doesn't mean there isn't any danger.
Open thoughts
Close thoughts


It's funny to me how this is my most popular article. I originally intended it to be nothing more than a filler article. Honestly, at first, I didn't understand the general hype around it. I mean, yes it was well written for the most part, however, it really lacked too much interesting content in my opinion. Part of the way I tried to close this gap was with a mystery (the noises heard on the level.) Fun fact: Part of the original plan when developing the slithering was that I would design an enigmatic entity around it but I ended up abandoning that idea for just keeping it a mystery.

Entity 92: "Ferren" +52

  • Ferren the Ferret is a B.N.T.G. pet that is able to no-clip to any level from one level. For all most know, he could even no-clip into the frontrooms (although this is for the most part unknown.)
Open thoughts
Close thoughts

Probably my favorite article I've made thus far. It wasn't until this point that I developed my personal style of article-making. The reason I made him was simply, I like ferrets. I didn't want all of my articles to be too complex and the least complex idea that I had was a ferret. So uhm… yeah, ferrets for the win.

Level 282: "The Mariner" +24

  • Read either a logbook story explaining the depths of the submarine cave and the mysteries within or a collapsible with a typical M.E.G. article defiling the mysteries in the deep blue.
Open thoughts
Close thoughts

My first format screw article and what an article it is. For those who were here when I was setting my first iteration of this author's article will know that there was supposed to be a fragment. Thing is, I'm not currently comfortable enough to where I'm gonna actively try that so ye. Now, there will prolly be a lot of questions thrown at me regarding this article but for those reading this, the idea is you're supposed to come up with those conclusions on your own.

Level 401: "Choices" +15

  • The power of choice is the most valuable thing a person can have. But what happens when those choices are turned against you?
Open thoughts
Close thoughts

The most experimental article I've done thus far. This one is kinda odd I'll be honest. It's more of a thought experiment to see how many people would understand its meaning while reading it. For the most part, this article is built to question a ton of things. For starters, it questions the very nature of the backrooms. While most of it is up to interpretation, I give enough insinuation to where people should be able to draw their own conclusions. For instance, I asked if the level was sentient. I questioned if the MC may be dead or not and I questioned whether this level was a timeloop of some sort. These and many more are asked and I think it's kinda poetic to have the reader decide these things. I personally loved creating this level and it's inspired me to start writing a canon I call "Aurora Liminalis" in the near future, (This is on hold for now,).

Level 402: "Plastic City" +29

  • A city of plastic that's eerily abandoned, or was it? In Classicon, explore the depths of the city, find statues, explore the Belltower and find out what's really going on in this seemingly safe level.
Open thoughts
Close thoughts

Currently my most recent article. Despite the fact this was released on Classicon, this was actually an article voted in by everyone in a community vote. There isn't much to this article that's very unique. Like, it's probably one of my more mundane articles but I still think it's interesting enough to catch the attention of some.

Level 326: "Seared Frontier" +36

  • An eerily destroyed room taking place in the Decay canon. Your mission is simple, find out what happened.
Open thoughts
Close thoughts

My first work for a canon and gotta say, I should've spent more time on this one. My procrastination got the best of me here and Ratif and Limdoc carried my SPaG by fixing it so thank yall. I think the concept was at least pretty good overall so at least there's that. Fun-fact: This was originally a room article that was separated largely from other levels, but this was changed since it was posted right as rooms were getting removed.

Tale: "The sun giveth then taketh away" +16

  • Part one of a mini-series of articles documenting the dangers of natural light on Level 29.
Open thoughts
Close thoughts

I can't say I'll lie here, this article is rather mediocre compared to most of my other works. Though, this will mostly set up a lot of the context that happens in part two, where our main character is actually established. Avoiding the light through means—even planning on having a pretty little daybreak SCP reference because funni. This will be pretty fun. Last con got me into writing for Decay/Blackout so I'll see how it goes there.

Phenomenon 90: "Letters" +37

  • Someone you knew. Your commander, missing. So many questions, yet all you can do is drink, and read her final letter
Open thoughts
Close thoughts

As I said in the article, this was initially based on smile dog. After one of my longest breaks from the wiki, I wrote this. This was also, coincidentally, my first ever Greenlight. Originally there was gonna be a dog-like entity attached to this, but honestly, after a few reworks, figured out that it just wouldn't work out like I wanted, so I settled for letters that could sort of kill you. It's also an allegory for suicide, and, most either aren't too clear about that, or, are way too on the nose. I think this is a good balance.

Tale: "Out of season" +15

  • Headmaster Herna, hunting a hunter? Or is he being hunted. Meet the most terrifying beast haunting the grove.
Open thoughts
Close thoughts

Funny thing about this article, really—it's based on a pokemon-like. At least, the "mysterious monster" is. It's a bit on the nose, but it's slightly based on the Loomian "Grimyuline" inspired the actual design of the monster. Other than that, there's really not much to say. It's an okay article in my books, not really good. Not really bad. Just sort of okay.

Level 297: "Website 297" +32

  • Have you ever wanted an escape from the harsh realities of the Backrooms? an escape fromm the never-ending dangers, entities always wanting to kill you, rooms that put you through more anguish than your average saw trap? Well, what if I told you you could have that at the click of a cursor button?
Open thoughts
Close thoughts

Not my best article per se, but it's up there as one of my favorites I've made. Based off of the theme fruitger aero which is a popular theme for tech. It's supposed to inspire hope, but there's also something eerie about how perfect it is. This article is a reflection of that. Of the future we wanted, but never got and turns it into a dystopian work.

Phenomenon 80: "Please Don't Leave" +32

  • People are important. They're both the very foundation of our understanding of ourselves as well as who we interact with everyday. People are everything. But what happens when those you know of, those you care about, you become blind to?
Open thoughts
Close thoughts

Another contest entry. Honestly I'm impressed I was inspired enough to even start and or finish this one because the last two ones I had an idea for were scrapped or postponed. Either way, this one's not to bad. I followed a more Phenomenon 90 style of format for this one but without the image and I think it works—my favorite bit was the ending peice, which I changed last minute. Originally, it was that Finn was going to write "I love you," but because it didn't make the most sense and wasn't set up that well it was changed. The new one takes advantage of the MC's fear of abandonment which is nice. All in all, it's for sure in my top 5 articles I've made on this platform.
