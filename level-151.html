Level 151 - "The Dollhouse"
rating: +31+–x


Info

Written by ~ Alkrolyd (SplasherBlaster#3591)

Level 151 is the 152nd Level of the Backrooms. Come and play with us.

Description:

Level 151 is a large, human-sized dollhouse resembling a mansion from the late Tudor period of British history. The mansion appears well-maintained, although how the estate is kept clean is unknown.

Estimates place the size of Level 151 at around 7500 square feet, though it is believed that more areas exist, although they are currently inaccessible1.

Upon entering Level 151, wanderers will often find themselves in the centre of an open hall or, in some cases, the cellar. Most light sources resemble that of the time period (e.g. candles, fireplaces and torches), but these are in fact fake. Candles and torches are oversized, containing a plastic LED mimicking a flame instead of a true flame. A handful of anachronistic stage lights have also been spotted on Level 151. Upon further inspection of the aforementioned fireplaces, travellers will find that they are actually large image cards of burning coal, while the mantlepieces are constructed of plastic coated in metallic paint.

Many rooms contain windows, which can be smashed open. However, the windows are fake, leading to an empty white/black fog. Listen out for the screams. The slow, faint sounds of screams echo through the fog. Attempts to escape through this fog will deposit the would-be escapist back into the room they first arrived in.2

Level 151 exhibits a day/night cycle.

During the day, most of the lights in the mansion will be shut off. The aforementioned fog outside the windows will maintain a constant aura of bright white light, which functions as the main light source during the day. The bedrooms are inaccessible during the day3, walled off by bricks until night arrives.

During the night, the outside fog will turn completely black. Its darkness is comparable to that of Level 6, completely pitch-black. The candles and torches on walls will all synchronously activate, emitting dim ice-blue light. Let the cold stare shrivel your heart into darkness. Bedrooms will become accessible, as well as the cellar and attic. Multiple secret passages, trapdoors and tripwires will become operational during this time. Periodically, rooms and corridors will shift around, accompanied by a loud noise reminiscent of scraping wood.

However, multiple instances of entities designated "Dolls" will become active during this time.

Entities:

Only one entity has formally been identified on Level 151: the Dolls.

The Dolls:

Dolls are life-sized Dutch dolls, possessing a style reminiscent of Germanic culture. As they only appear on Level 151, it is possible they are trapped in the dollhouse mansion much like any wanderer.

During the artificial day period, which begins at 6:00 and ends at 18:00 precisely, Dolls are passive entities, and will not approach humans, or even acknowledge their existence. They are often found sitting at the dining table in the main hall, or standing idly in different rooms.

However, during the night, they will become highly aggressive. Initially, it would seem that the Dolls are immobile, however, this is false. The entities will only move when a human (who has been in their line of sight) moves, at identical speed regardless of the target's build. Once a Doll witnesses a traveller, they will "lock on" to them. Once this occurs, the Doll is able to detect when the individual moves, without needing to maintain visual contact with the target.

One touch from a Doll completely immobilises the target. If a Doll catches a wanderer, there are several courses of action they may take. Most commonly, the Doll will proclaim the wanderer to be their "toy" that must be stored away, as "playtime is over". They will then attempt to stuff the victim into a toy chest put the toys away, which will crush them to death make them very happy. In other cases, when the nearest toy chests are full, the Dolls opt to attack wanderers with toy melee weapons instead donate the toys to charity. Despite looking like nothing more than comically oversized toys, these weapons function exactly like their proper counterparts the normal toy versions.

On rare occasions, if enraged, the Dolls will instead simply snap the necks of their victims break the toys, because we don't want to play with them anymore.

The Dolls are capable of limited speech. They have been heard eliciting childlike laughter, as well as humming or chanting various nursery rhymes. Oranges and Lemons, Pop! Goes the Weasel and Ring Around the Rosie appear to be their preferred choices.

Addendum: The Dolls are highly intelligent entities, fully sentient and capable of free thought. The Dolls have gained access to the M.E.G. database through unconfirmed means (see Discovery Log) and have began editing this article. Attempts to correct these mistakes have only been met with further tampering by the Dolls.

Bases, Outposts and Communities

No known bases, communities or outposts are found on Level 151.

Except for the Dolls, who are happy for you to stay! Follow our instructions, be a good toy, and no harm will come to you.

Discovery Log

Foreword: The following voice log documents Operation Dollhouse, carried out by M.E.G. Team ██████. On 03/07/19, three operatives, Agent Valentine, Agent Jones and Agent Barrister, were sent on the first official exploratory mission to Level 151 to study the rumoured Doll entities. At the time of recording, the Dolls were simply known as "the Entities".

17:42
*Creaking sounds of a door opening*

17:43
Agent Barrister: This is it. Level 151. Watch your six, this is the uncanny valley.
*Loose floorboard creaking, followed by a loud thud*
Agent Valentine: Argh! You should watch your step, too!

17:46
*Echoes of loud wooden scraping*
Agent Valentine: What was that?
Agent Jones: Could be the Entity nearby. I'll check my tablet's radar.
*Device beeping*
Agent Jones: The radar isn't picking anything up, only three dots in close proximity - which would be us, I'm guessing.
Agent Barrister: Guesswork is not encouraging, Jones. The more we understand about this place, the better.

17:54
The Team arrive at the main lobby.
*Heavy steps echo down stairs, proceeded by the reverb of glass*
Agent Barrister: These candles look…comically oversized. Almost like a toy.
Agent Jones: Who would play with that? I think those giant toy chests we saw earlier were a greater indication.
Agent Valentine: But did you see those fireplaces? They're not even real! I know these levels are weird but…what's the point?
*Echoes of childlike giggling*
Agent Valentine: What the hell was that? The Entity?
Agent Barrister: It came from over there. We should take a look. Stay frosty.
Agent Jones: Still nothing on the radar. Are you sure it's one of the Entities, and not just a hallucination?
Agent Barrister: Only one way to find out.
Agent Valentine: For the record, I have a bad feeling about this. If I were just a wanderer, I'd be running in the opposite direction.

17:57
*Loud creaking of double doors opening*
Agent Barrister: It looks like a dining room.
Agent Valentine: Are those…peg dolls? Sitting at the table?
Agent Barrister: Oversized peg dolls. Most of the wanderers said the Entities were life-sized dolls, so this checks out.
Agent Jones: They don't pick up on the radar. What do we do, exactly? Watch?
Agent Barrister: Don't get too close!

17:59
*Camera clicking*
Agent Barrister: This is Agent Barrister of M.E.G. Team ██████. Begin report: we have encountered multiple instances of Unnamed Entity T-1514. Entities seem to be life-sized peg dolls, presumably Germanic. We are unsure whether instances are mobile or not, but they currently appear docile. Entities also seem to be communicating between each other through unknown means. We have enacted Security Protocol 637, meaning we are maintaining a distance from the Entities of no less than three feet. Further examination must be carried out to determine whether instances of T-151 are harmful to humans. End report.
*Camera clicking*
Agent Valentine: Do you think they know we're here?
Agent Barrister: I'm not sure I want to find out.
Agent Jones: I don't think pissing off something we don't understand is a good idea. Remember what happened when—

18:00
*Sounds of windows snapping shut, followed by shuffling of feet, accompanied by wooden scraping*
Agent Valentine: What the hell?! I can't see!
*Device beeping rapidly*
Agent Jones: I think my tablet is picking up something!
*Sounds of lights switching on*
Agent Valentine: What the-? They moved!
*Several rushed steps reverberate across the floor, followed by wooden scraping*
Agent Barrister: Stay still!
Agent Valentine: They moved again!
Agent Barrister: I can see that, Valentine.
Agent Barrister takes a slow, quiet step.
*An equally slow scrape of wood follows her*
Agent Jones: So…they only move when we move?
Agent Barrister: It would seem so. Everything's gone dark, too. Maybe it's like…night-time?
Agent Jones: So how do we…

UNKNOWN SOURCE: ~Oranges and lemons, say the bells of St Clement's.~

Agent Valentine: We have to get out of here.
Agent Barrister: Don't be a fool! We have to be smart here. If we just run for the door, they'll follow us. And I don't want to know what happens when they get us.
Agent Jones: How are we gonna get out? That door in the Hub was one-way.

UNKNOWN SOURCE: ~You owe me five farthings, say the bells of St Martin's.~

18:01
Agent Valentine: Di-Didn't that one group of survivors say they found a secret p-passageway that led to a trapdoor?
Agent Jones: Apparently they found one upstairs in the second bedroom. But how are we gonna find it?
Agent Barrister: It's the only chance we have. Maybe we can use those chairs to block them?
Agent Valentine: Al-Alright…we can do this.
Agent Barrister: On three…one…two…three!
A cacophony of noises ensues. Audible shuffling across the floor is immediately masked by the noise of the Entities moving. A distinct shrill of furniture moving is also audible, along with the doors opening.

18:04
Agent Valentine: Help, I think one of them touched me! I can't move!
Agent Barrister: Argh! Jones, keep the doors open!
Agent Jones pushes against the double doors as instructed.
Agent Barrister spots a cream porcelain hand gripping Agent Valentine's legs.

UNKNOWN SOURCE: ~When will you pay me? Say the bells of Old Bailey.~

Agent Barrister: They're holding onto him hard! How strong are these guys?

UNKNOWN SOURCE: ~When I grow rich, say the bells of Shoreditch!~ Playtime is over!

Several grunts and groans of exhaustion continue before a scream is heard, and the doors snap shut.

18:05
Agent Jones: Oh god, they got him! What do we do?
*Sounds of furniture moving, followed by loud banging*
Agent Barrister (panting): That should hold them for a little while. We'll have to head on without him. There's nothing we can do.
Agent Jones: It looks like when they're active, they show up on the radar.
*Device beeping*
Agent Jones: *Sigh*. Nothing in range.
Agent Barrister: C'mon, let's go.

18:17
Agents Jones and Barrister are wandering the hallways of Level 151. They have not heard the Dolls since Valentine went missing.
Agent Jones: I don't understand. Where the hell is this "second bedroom"?
Agent Barrister: I think the rooms have changed.
Agent Barrister rests against a wall.
*Camera clicking*
Agent Barrister: Reopen Log T-15. Addendum: Entities are hostile, Agent Valentine MIA assumed KIA, the "dolls" seem to have paralysed him and taken him away to parts unknown. It is to be noted that dolls only seem to move while another moves, matching the speed of said individual. I believe they were singing a nursery rhyme. Oranges and Lemons, I think. End log.
*Camera clicking*
Agent Jones: How are you so calm? Valentine is dead!
Agent Barrister: Presumed dead.
Agent Jones: Whatever! I'm losing my mind here! How do you stay so level-headed?
Agent Barrister: After I escaped Level 6, nothing scares me anymore.

18:35
The two agents are now traversing the third floor of the dollhouse mansion.
Agent Barrister: I think…when those dolls can't see us, they can't follow us.
Agent Jones: You might be right. I haven't heard any of that stupid kid's rhyme since…since Valentine was taken.
*Sound of a door opening*
Agent Jones: Finally, a bedroom!
The two step quietly into the room.
Agent Jones: Wait…how do we know if this is the second bedroom?
Agent Barrister: We don't. If the rooms change around…maybe "second bedroom" just means the second one we find?
Agent Jones: That's just guesswork. Like you said before…guesswork isn't promising.
Agent Barrister: Then look around. Maybe we can find the secret exit somewhere around here.
*Muted noises of movement about the room*

18:42
Agent Jones moves over to inspect a comically disproportioned wooden toy chest.
Agent Jones: Hmm…maybe it's in—
*Sound of a chest opening*
Agent Jones screams in fright, before muffling their voice with their hand.
Agent Barrister: Calm down, Jones!
Agent Jones: It's Valentine! They…they stuffed him in here! What the fuck?!
Agent Barrister reaches forward to inspect the body.
Agent Barrister: He's dead.
Agent Jones: As if you couldn't tell!
Agent Barrister: Jones, calm down and control yourself!
Jones stands up.
Agent Jones: Control myself?! Don't you think I'm trying?!
*Device beeping in the background*
Jones then shoves Barrister, causing her to stumble out the door.

UNKNOWN SOURCE: ~And when will that be? Say the bells of Stepney.~

A Doll arrives and grips Barrister's shoulder, causing her to freeze in place.
Agent Jones: Barrister!
Agent Barrister: Stop! Don't move!
Agent Jones: What do we do? We can't stay here forever!
Agent Barrister: You can't . Look…it's over for me, but you can still get out of here! They'll be focussing on me…so you can slip by!

UNKNOWN SOURCE: ~Oh I do not know, say the great bells of Bow!~

Agent Barrister: GO!

18:43
Agent Jones bolts out the door, dodging the Dolls that swarmed on the scene.
*Sounds of a device clattering to the ground*

UNKNOWN SOURCE: Aw, the toy chest is full! We'll donate this toy to charity…

Looking back, Agent Jones can see the Dolls grab a toy axe from the chest and move towards Agent Barrister.

UNKNOWN SOURCE: ~Here comes a candle to light you to bed. And here comes a chopper to chop off your head.~

Upon the final line of the rhyme, one of the Dolls throws down the toy axe on Agent Barrister's neck.
*Loud thud*

19:03
Agent Jones continues to wander the hallways and rooms of Level 151, alone. Jones takes out their camera and presses the button.
Agent Jones: Reopen Log T-15. Addendum: The Entities are dangerous. Avoid them…just avoid them. You hear the bells of St Clement's, then run! Just run! They stuffed Valentine into a toy chest…and they chopped Barrister's head off! Oh god…I have to get out of here…I have to get out…Please…E-End log.

19:22
*Door opening noise*
Agent Jones: The second bedroom! Please…please…let me out of here!
*Swift rifling through the room* Agent Jones searches through the room intensely, hoping to find any possible indication of an exit. After a few minutes, there is a loud stone rumbling sound.
Agent Jones: Oh thank God! The secret passage!
Agent Jones runs down the dark corridor.

Agent Jones was later found in Level 1 by M.E.G. Operatives, where they were taken to Base Alpha to record their findings. In the confusion, Agent Jones discovered they had lost their admin tablet in Level 151. The current whereabouts of this device is unknown, but it is believed to be in the hands of the Dolls. Extraction teams were deployed to search for the tablet, but they were unable to locate it. Following consistent fatalities, all subsequent requests to lead extraction teams to find this tablet have been denied.

A minor extraction team was later deployed to retrieve the corpse of Agent Valentine.

Agent Valentine

The corpse of Agent Valentine was discovered having been moved from the toy chest to a wooden seat at a comically large table, his limbs positioned in a manner as though about to drink from a mug of tea.

The following contains the autopsy report, carried out by Doctor ██████:

"Subject seems to have died from cervical fracture caused by high pressure applied at the base of the skull. Multiple legs fractures, cuts, bruises and one laceration on the lip, all suggest that the subject had been beaten repeatedly, before being stuffed into a small space. The victims arms must've been forcibly pushed down to fit into the space, before being crushed by an object pressed against their neck. In my medical opinion, this would have been excruciatingly painful."

Agent Barrister

The remains of Agent Barrister were later uncovered in the attic of the dollhouse. The intestinal tract of Barrister was found hanging on fishhooks attached to the ceiling. Barrister's skull and femur bones were found in a plastic linen basket adjacent to the fish hooks. A large wooden sign was positioned in front of Barrister's remains, marked "FOR CHARITEE :D" in crayon.

The whereabouts of the rest of Agent Barrister's body parts are unknown.

Entrances And Exits:

Entrances

Entrances can be found everywhere! Come and join the toys, the Dolls will be kind!

  • No-clipping through the trees in Level 150.
  • Level 151 can be found through a one-way door in the Hub.
  • Running through a fake wall in Level 178.
  • One of the windows in Level 881 can lead into Level 151 through a corresponding window.
  • Ordering a "Dollhouse Supreme" in the diner on level-98.

Exits

The only known exits to Level 151 are hidden trapdoors found at the end of secret passages found in the study, and the second and third bedrooms. These hidden exits lead to random levels.
You can't leave! Toys must be put away, not left to roam free. Back to the toy chest where you belong, come on now. It's nice and cozy in there!
