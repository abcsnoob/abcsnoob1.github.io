Scene 02.9

Generalities

One of the few known representations of Scene 02.9. As of now, no photos of the scene have been taken.

Scene 02.9 is a nightmarish plane of existence, described by the few people who entered it by accident as a flesh landscape, filled with rivers of blood and hills made out of bones. Although it can be entered through no-clipping, there is only one confirmed physical entrance to date, found within Scene 01.9. Due to the dangerous nature of the place, however, it was permanently sealed in 1980 by the Leaders, and has remained guarded ever since.

Scene 02.9 appears to be the source of many entity species commonly found across the DevRooms. Furthermore, the hypothetical existence of the Hive Queen, an entity supposedly personifying and overseeing the scene, has fueled backborn culture for centuries, although its existence hasn't been confirmed beyond rumors from wanderers of old.

Survival Advice

Due to the suspected risk of travelling to Scene 02.9, exploration of it has been prohibited by the Leaders, which means that the only information accessible takes the form of wanderer testimonies, some being centuries old.

10/12/2019

The Daily Rooms

M.E.G. Pioneer and Former Member Found Dead on Level 853

Olivia Bellerose, one of the earliest members of the Major Explorer Group, has been found dead on Level 853 by another M.E.G. operative. Although she left her position as Base Alpha researcher two years ago after the death of her partner, Tournesol, her body will nonetheless be buried similarly to other major M.E.G. pioneers, according to Overseer Justin River, who did not give any additional information for the moment.

These past few years haven't been forgiving to the Major Explorer Group. Since the loss of the senior archivist Asher River in 2016, it seems that every year since then has seen the death of yet another of their prominent members. 2020 is now no different, as the former botanical researcher and operative Olivia Bellerose's corpse has been found on Level 853 by an operative of Compass Point who asked to remain anonymous.

Olivia Bellerose had been a part of the M.E.G. from its very creation, putting her botanical skills to good use by working alongside Underseer Jana Cotyledon on many breakthroughs that proved vital to the M.E.G. as it grew. Ever since The Nomadic Coalition fused into what became the Major Explorer group in 2012, she has manifested the will to protect wanderers by exploring and researching this anomalous world. Her iron will notably led to the creation of Roserum, who is now commonly used to prevent Crawler contamination and proliferation.

Her time in the M.E.G. came to an end in 2018, however, when her partner, Tournesol, a former entity researcher of Base Alpha, died during an expedition. Although this event is known by the general public as the end of the M.E.G. Golden Era, it also affected Olivia, who left the group she considered to be too negligent, unable to help even its workers.

After taking an interest in Level 5 for a few months, where she wrote the now famous Terror Hotel Survival Guide, she wandered deeper into the Backrooms, with the goal to save as many helpless wanderers as possible. Information about her feats is scarce, due to her refusing to discuss with the M.E.G. during most interactions, but her name quickly became a symbol of hope inside backborn communities living away from the Main 12.

Level 853 proved to be her last sacrifice, however. While her cause of death hasn't been given to the public as of yet, the M.E.G. has confirmed that they retrieved her body back to Base Alpha, where she will be buried as a M.E.G. member and hero. "She was more than a coworker to us. Despite her having left us two years ago, this event marks the second death of someone we valued so deeply." announced Overseer Justin River during his last appearance.

With yet another pioneer of the M.E.G. gone, many M.E.G. workers have publicly expressed concern over the group's future and its capacity to move on from the loss of so many of their veterans. "The Oversight Council hasn't spoken since then, but it's clear the members are in distress. How many of their friends can they afford to lose before the whole group splinters again?" questions Ale Venrick, the Compass Point team captain.


The Oversight Council hasn't officially responded to the events as of now, and Overseer Justin River has also denied further explanation

{$caption}

Chimerae

Mosaic in Hoofstad, like many other forms of art, was deeply rooted in religion, and the Chimerae1 were no exception.

These creatures, like many others in the Backrooms, were the result of human legends taken form through what we now know to be Liminal Echo. These creations, whether friendly or aggressive, were believed to be created in Paradeos, a threshold between Nihil (the mind) and Daedos (the body). Said location is represented on the first piece of the aisle, depicting a man growing from the roots of the level.

The second and third pieces represent said entities2, whose portraits were often kept in houses. These representations of physical manifestations of Paradeos were believed to help strengthen the link between the soul and the body of the Hoofish living inside. Whether feared or loved, these creatures all originated from their own beliefs, taking form in unusual yet fascinating ways.

The last piece is believed to represent the hive mind of Paradeos, named Ohphúō3, believed to be the consciousness of Paradeos, and a child of Nirmaan, the Goddess of art and creation.

Il Nido si espande nel mondo

Painted by Leonio Vaccarello somewhere during the 16th century, Il Nido si espande nel mondo is one of the most famous representations of the Hive while the Backrooms were under the influence of Christianity and Christianity-related religions.

Despite the hellish imagery coming from monotheistic religions, the staples of the Hive representation since the fall of Hoofstad in 476 AC are also present here. These are the fleshy, dark floor (although more rocky than in other depictions) and, most importantly, the presence of the Hive Queen.

Interestingly enough, the depiction of the queen resembles an agonizing face partially merged into the floor. While the most obvious signification is how intertwined with its hive it is, many scholars at the time agreed upon the fact that this might also mean the regent is in everlasting pain, with the wounds directly coming from the level itself, a potential metaphor for a wound that reflected into the wider hell that is the Hive.

{$caption}

Voyages Au-Delà de la Raison

From 1872 to 1891, Gustave Vincent, a French artist from the at-the-time newly formed Amor Incrementum community, entered the Hive by unknown means.

The experience radically changed his painting style, going from his now famous colorful still life paintings to grim, fleshy messes of what he called "l'enfer de chair"4. Forever traumatized by the experience he went through, he spent the last few years of his life on this collection of paintings called "Voyages Au-Delà de la Raison"5.

In his memoirs, he describes "a mockery of life, an antithesis of what the world intended. Hatred […] reigning over the madness of what it created, just as much as it created itself." While the nature of said Hatred being a personification of the Hive Queen is still debated by scholars, the mentioned entrance being the Cave System, a threshold that has been confirmed to have existed for centuries, confirms the nature of the hell in question to be the Hive.

Out of the twenty-six paintings composing this collection, three can be found in the Interdimensional Museum of Backrooms History, five are held here, and the other eighteen are still kept by Amor Incrementum, who to this day are asking for the other nine to be brought back to their main sanctum in the Abandoned Office as a part of their culture.

The Regent of the Flesh Hive

While the author of the sketch is unknown, the remnants of the Poli's Unified Group records confirm that it was drawn in 1942 for the Poli's Bugle journal, and that it depicts "The Regent of the Flesh Hive".

This representation of the Hive Queen is most likely the most realistic one as of now, as it lacks the artistic liberties of madness or personification. Despite this, the creature is yet again portrayed as a grieved being, despite the anger and hatred it's been described to feature in the article's description.

"The Regent of the Flesh Hive" quickly became the representation of reference for the myth of the Hive Queen. Also named "The Grieving Spiders", it wasn't unusual for children at the time to be told tales about a boogeyman who would send monsters after them to eat their souls if they disobeyed their parents. The author of the sketch themself is believed to have drawn the creature according to the legends, as the journal extract does not mention them going in the actual Hive.

The Myth of the Hive Today

While the tales of the Hive Queen are now little more than creepypastas or scary stories to tell to kids, it is undeniable it has influenced backborn culture to a point where The Leaders decided to close up the (believed to be) main entrance to the Hive in 1980, guarding it to ensure nobody traversing the Cave Systems would enter it by mistake.

Despite their attempts, three testimonies from The Leaders' era exist, telling the stories of three distinct people who no-clipped in the level. All three managed to leave by exiting through the previously sealed main entrance, aided by the guards on the other side, allowing for their experience to be recorded and archived. While the existence of no-clipping thresholds from and to the Hive has always been confirmed to exist6, these unfortunate stories have only served to increase the popularity of the Hive and the Hive Queen as a boogeyman, whatever it is.
