Level 294 - "The Liminary Theatre"
rating: +42+–x

SURVIVAL DIFFICULTY:

Class 1

  • {$one}
  • {$two}
  • {$three}

A photo of the theatre, taken by Genova Charleston.

Description:

Level 294, the 295th level of the Backrooms, is an opera theatre thematically styled to be reminiscent of one of those which were built in the 60s. It was discovered by a wanderer named Genova Charleston on April 9th, 2020. Explorers who end up in the level will almost always emerge at an area close to the building's entrance within what appears to be a city that is devoid of any structure. Etchings in the concrete mark areas where buildings should be, and shadows are present where signs and cars would normally be. The city appears to extend infinitely and possesses no organic flora and fauna, which has made it so that the M.E.G. discourages wanderers from attempting any explorations to its far reaches.

The theatre itself is in pristine condition and is reminiscent of the Metropolitan Opera House in size. The structure is seemingly based on common architectural techniques of the 60s, and the outside geometry appears to be euclidean in nature. Many tinted windows and tall pillars line the walls of the theatre, with a few of the latter poking out at random angles; near the entrance is a large set of stairs that lead nowhere. The back of the theatre seems to have multiple back doors at first glance. However, when one opens them, one will realize that each doorway merely leads to an impenetrable and unbreakable wall. The main entrance is the only means by which one can enter the building. Above it, a sign that reads "The Liminary Opera Theatre - Desire for Glory" can be found, which is how it has attained its nickname: "The Liminary Theatre". Faint, somber jazz and chatter can be heard on the inside, but these sounds give way to complete silence when the theatre is entered. There are three main areas of the Liminary Theatre: the Main Hall, the Theatre, and the Dressing Rooms.

The Main Hall:

The Hallway—unlike the rest of the building—appears to be scaled-up, which is oftentimes described as eliciting feelings of minimality from wanderers. In regards to the hallway's design itself, wanderers who visit the level are always impressed by the splendor exhibited by its decorations. A staircase at the center of the room is adorned with velvety red carpets and intricately-designed golden railings is immediately made evident upon walking into the room. On its left, a large seating area can be found, which is composed of a handful of lush, red chairs. Wanderers who have sat on these chairs report that they occasionally hear voices come from around them, but no sources of this chatter have been identified thus far. On the other side of the hall, an empty reception desk lies abandoned, a single sign-in sheet lying on it. The sheet contains various illegible signatures scribbled on it, as well as a sign atop the counter which reads "Sorry we missed you! We'll be back soon :)". Behind the staircase are two restrooms, both of which are locked, and cannot be opened.

Multiple paintings are hung on the walls, all of which seemingly advertise upcoming operas and sets. Observes depictions include a woman at the edge of a crimson lake, a dark figure within the trees of a grand jungle, and a girl in what looks to be a large city1. Other pieces of decor include elaborate paintings, potted plants, and Wall Masks. Although multiple wanderers have entered the theatre, a generous coating of dust always covers the surfaces inside of it.

The Theatre:

Going up the stairs will reveal two more seating areas and a large balcony that looks out into a part of the glass dome ceiling. Wanderers have all mentioned feeling a profound sense of loneliness, even after leaving the level. Walking through the doors in between the seating areas will lead to the Theatre.

If the Main Hall had made wanderers feel small beforehand, the Theatre accentuates that feeling by making them feel as if they were ants. Nearly doubled in size, it houses an estimated 7,200 seats, which are all divided into stalls. All of these chairs are accessible, save for the balcony seats on the side of the room, which have no doorways leading to them whatsoever. The seats inside of the room, as velvety as they are, possess questionable qualities. Parts of their fabric are frayed, several seats rest lopsidedly on their platforms and feel unstable, and most curiously of all, they emit a faint, yet noxious smell of gasoline.

Two walkways inside of this area lead up to the stage, with one being blocked due to a considerably large amount of debris that has obstructed it. The stage itself is roughly the same size as the auditorium (if not larger). When one gets onto the auditorium, one will begin to hear whispers from the seats in the auditorium in a similar fashion to the seats in the seating rooms. Uneasiness and nervousness have been reported as well, which is postulated to be caused by the voices. On the stage, a dozen or so props are present, which change with every re-entrance to the Theatre. Said props range from large, golden thrones, drama masks, fake weapons, and cutouts of Backrooms-native entities such as Clumps and Facelings. The House Curtains that dress the sides of the stage are, while made of high-quality mohair velvet, are of poor condition—many rips and tears cover the curtains paired with dark stains. Behind the stage, a backstage can be found with a set of double doors on the right which lead to the Dressing Rooms.

The Dressing Rooms:

The Dressing Rooms are the final accessible section of the Liminary Theatre and is also the most unstable part of it. The area is comprised of a presumably infinite hallway, wide, low, and carpeted with a similar carpet to that of the staircase in The Main Hall. Ivory doors are evenly spaced out at intervals of 3 metres (around 10 feet), and each one of them leads to a unique room that makes up The Dressing Rooms. The hallway is consistent and unchanging, so the locations of the rooms never change.

The hallway itself is dirty and riddled with structural debris and rubble, which renders some of its rooms inaccessible. Those that are accessible are all identical, with each containing a large vanity, chair, clothes rack, and a few wardrobes, all of which are made of fine mahogany but are of notably poor quality.2 Other than the furniture, not much else can be found in The Dressing Rooms. Objects such as Almond Water and Level Keys can be occasionally found strewn about haphazardly, and some of the vanities have empty pamphlets and pens inside of them, but other than those, nothing else can be found in the rooms.

Emotional Displacement:

Whilst most levels physically and emotionally impact wanderers in their unique ways, The Liminary Theatre works oppositely to that, in that whatever the general wanderer populace inside of the level feels when they are inside of it affects the level itself. Dubbed as "Emotional Displacement" by the M.E.G., this peculiar phenomenon alters the Liminary Theatre's physical properties according to how strong the emotions of people in the level collectively are. Such changes include small tremors, noticeable temperature and weather changes, as well as shifts in the level's color. Several experiments have been conducted on this phenomenon, although none have concluded much about it. A handful of these tests have been attached to this article below:

DISCLAIMER:
Please note that all of the following teams except for Team "Joy" received proper treatment and therapy for all possible mental or physical damage caused by the experiment. No one has been left unattended.

Procedure: Each experiment group is to go through a specific routine and perform certain activities for a period of 1 month to elicit a specific emotion to their optimum. Upon doing so, each group is to enter Level 294 and remain outside of the theatre for a total of two hours. Once that time has passed, the team is to enter the rooms of Level 294 for another two hours and leave through one of its exits once the experiment is concluded.

Experiment Log #1
Date: 05/08/2020
Experiment Group 1: Team "Joy"

Pre-activities: For an entire month leading up to the experiment, "Team Joy's" members had their diets adjusted to increase serotonin and dopamine levels (using foods such as nuts, fish, etc to increase these levels naturally and organically), and several hours a day were allocated to emotional and relaxational therapy (such as meditation and massages). Finally, three days prior to entering Level 294, Team Joy was exposed to multiple forms of media and activity which are known to incite general positive feelings. This included motivational music, light-hearted media (I.e video games, television, etc), and hobbies specific to each member of the team.
Noted Effects: Changes were noted upon the first five minutes of the team being inside of the level. Average temperatures spiked to a pleasant 23°C (or 74°F), and all light inside of the level, both artificial and natural, was noticeably brighter. An unnatural over-saturation of all the colors within Level 294 was observed, which wanderers reported being pleasant at first, but straining on the eyes after long periods of exposure. The previously somber jazz music which was occasionally heard within the Liminary Theatre was replaced with a more soulful and upbeat track. Even after all the team members had left Level 294, the physical properties it had garnered as a byproduct of the experiment lingered for 2 weeks before returning to its previous state, which was the same period that Team Joy's members spent being reassessed before returning to their duties.

Experiment Log #2
Date: 05/29/2020
Experiment Group 1: Team "Desolation"

Pre-activities: Team "Desolation" underwent slight adjustment to their diets for 2 weeks prior to the experiment itself. This included an increased intake of alcohol, fat-heavy products, and generally bland foods, which were given to the wanderers in the near pitch-black rooms which they spent the preparatory days of the experiment inside. 3 days before entering Level 294 for the experiment, Team Desolation was also exposed to generally dark music and media to incite sadness.

Noted Effects: Changes were noted upon the first two minutes of the team being inside of the level. All light in the level was reported to have dimmed, despite the sun in the sky being present in the same fashion which it normally is in. Strong winds picked up shortly after entering, and the weather cooled down to a chill 15°C (or 59°F). Grey clouds soon set in, almost completely covering the sun up, at which point light droplets of rain began to fall, growing heavier as time passes. The colors of the level also took on a more desaturated appearance, almost greyscale in color—although it is unclear if this has anything to do with the clouds themselves or the general nature of the level.
Upon examining the theatre, Team Desolation reported that the exterior of the building seemed to be 'caved inward', even though the insides of it were reported to be identical to previous explorations. The music that played within the theatre seemed to have slowed down to a mind-numbing degree, and it was often interrupted by loud static or random decelerations in speed. The lights within the theatre were completely shut off and did not turn on for the duration of Team Desolation's venture into the level. Although Team Desolation's members recovered from the induced moods within 2 weeks of exiting, the level took a month to return to its original state.

Experiment Log #3
Date: 06/23/2020
Experiment Group 1: Team "Rage"

Pre-activities: "Team Rage" was originally notified that they were simply to enter Level 294 for a check-up on the level, and they were not informed of the ulterior motives of the experiment which they were to take part in. 2 weeks before the experiment, all familiars of the team were asked to treat them with general coldness and distance, dissimilar to what they would have been regularly subjected to. Furthermore, issues relating to their workplace were complicated, and a stressful environment was introduced to them, effectively making them more foul-tempered and moody. Members were also subjected to planned "encounters" with certain entities (some of which included Smilers and Hounds—though no one was injured during the encounters) to elicit tension and distress.3 By the time the day of the experiment came, Team Rage's mood had generally soured to the desired point.

Noted Effects: Immediately upon entering Level 294, Team Rage observed the sky immediately turning to a strong shade of pastel red, before clouding over entirely and submerging the level in darkness. At first, the members of Team Rage were instructed to stay outside for as long as they could, but when a violent storm began taking place and the level's general temperature reached a staggering (or 112°F), they were instructed to seek refuge inside of the Liminary Theatre in hopes of more forgiving temperatures being present inside of it.
Despite this, the temperatures in the theatre were not much better, and neither was its general condition. The structure of the theatre seemed to unnaturally distort in some areas, its pillars sticking further out than usual, and in some cases, changing their shape to resemble thorn bushes. The music heard inside of the theatre was replaced by faint screams, which could be heard coming from outside of the theatre rather than from the inside. The lights of the level flickered throughout the team's stay inside of the level, and they began to spontaneously shatter at some points during the team's stay. The colors of the level were overlaid by a faint red hue, which got more intense the more time that the team spent inside of the level. By the time the expedition was aborted, two of the team's members had fainted, and they had to be assisted by their fellow crewmates to exit the level.
Level 294 was declared a dead zone for the next month and a half which the effects took to subside. The members of Team Rage were all hospitalized for a plethora of different reasons associated with their venture into Level 294.

Ecoswiment Gr8xo: Her.

After being gone for a week, Evangeline finally came back to the Theatre. Slowly, all of the masked crept from their hiding spots beneath the seats and peeked out from the opening to the Dressing Rooms, watching her drag her injured body up on the stage. With everyone's eyes upon her, I saw my chance to leave—to go back to the M.E.G. to report my findings—and so I did, slipping through the walkways, using the abnormally large seats for a cover. Just as I was about to leave, I hear something fall. I turn, and see her, dropping her mask on the floor. With it, she collapses to her knees…

And screams.

A strong, hot gush of wind blew against the lot of us, the warmth of my blood seeping down my cheeks as her sobs seemed to echo throughout the theatre. I began to confuse the blood from my wounds with cold sweat: everyone, including me, it seemed, had tears falling from their face with no explanation. Ignoring everything around her, Evangeline screeched once again, the temperature rising at a drastic pace. In fear, I ran to one of the double doors that would lead back to the Main Hall, touching the metal knob and crying out at the feeling of my skin burning. Widening my eyes in horror, I see the doors begin to shake. Another scream comes, and the next thing I knew: the doors were blown off their hinges, nearly hitting me and revealing a fire brighter and hotter than anything I've ever felt before. The others cried out in fear, running as fast as they could to the Stage.

It took no time for the fire to spread. The flames flooded through the door and covered the walls of the theatre; light fixtures shattering, the wallpaper shriveling into blackness, and the curtains of the theatre were adorned with destructive flames. I retreated deeper into the theatre as the fire spread to the seats. Below us, the ground trembles, threatening to take the entire building down. My hands shook as I desperately look around, trying to find a way to escape, a pit forming in my stomach with the awareness that the chance of escaping lessened drastically.

I turn back to the stage to see some of the masked desperately shaking Evangeline, trying to get her to control her emotions as the fire surrounded us. It took one of them slapping her across the face to finally realize what she had done. In a moment, she fell back, fainting. Squinting, I see the others put her mask on; the tremor and the fire suddenly ceasing completely. Breathing heavily, I watch as they pick her up, bringing her into the Dressing Rooms. While everyone else huddled together in silence, a beam from outside of the doorless entrance cracked and fell, blocking it and causing some to scream. Some began to cry.

Legs shaking, I made my way to another door, turning the knob and sighing of relief at the feeling of cool metal. I shut the door and look down at the floor, a few tears dotting the carpet. I take off my mask, thinking all would finally be alright. I yelp and fall back on the carpet at the site of the fire ruining everything in its path. As the heat began to rise once more, I put my mask back on in a panic, sighing of relief at the sight of the flames dying again. I go to leave down the staircase and through the window… before stopping and looking back towards the entrance to the Theatre, a feeling of pity, hatred and anger igniting within me. I make my way back up and walk through the door, tightening the ribbon that held my mask up. Behind me, the sound of another beam falls to the floor.

In my times in the M.E.G., I've never heard despair like that until now. I've worked under you for so long, yet only now, away from you, were my eyes finally opened to the truth. You spoke the truth that day: you really would do whatever it takes to get what you want. A flame of hate ignites within me. I turn back to the entrance of the Theatre and walk in, with only one thought on my mind:

What have you people done to her?

Entities:

Entities are seldom seen roaming around Level 294. Nonetheless, Deathmoths, Curabiturs, and Evocationaries have all been sighted in various areas inside of the level, although their appearances are infrequent and apocryphal most of the time.

Bases, Communities, and Outposts:

As supplies are an extremely rare sight and entrances, in general, are difficult to come across, there are no known bases, communities, nor outposts.

Entrances And Exits:

Entrances

Only one working entrance to Level 294 has ever been reported, which is by entering a painting of an opera theatre in Level 57.

Exits

  • On the reception desk, there are a few flyers, some of which contain advertisements of a movie adaptation of an opera that may be recognized from the paintings in the Main Hall. Take one and leave through the front entrance to enter Level 68.
  • Entering the painting of a large city will lead to Level 11.
  • Performing a controlled no-clip on the bottom floor of the auditorium will lead to Level 74.
  • Performing a controlled no-clip into the walls of the Main Hall will lead one to either Level 1 or Level 4.
  • Some of the members of Team "Rage" have reported that one of the Dressing Rooms lead into a hallway of mirrors, but considering that reattempting to enter said room led to it being locked, this may not be true.4
