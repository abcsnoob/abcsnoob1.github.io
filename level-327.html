Level 327 - "Joint Operation"
rating: +21+–x

SURVIVAL DIFFICULTY:

Class 2

  • {$one}
  • {$two}
  • Devoid of Entities

Level 327 is the 328th level of The Backrooms. This level manifests itself as the second floor of a two-story house, with rooms that contain mostly beds, connected by hallways lit by morning light. The exact depth and size of which is still unknown. This level was discovered by a team dispatched by the M.E.G, and is currently inhabited by M.E.G personnel as of 10/22/2022.

Description

First ever reported picture of this level, taken on 28/08/2015 from an anonymous wanderer.

Level 327 takes the form of a contemporary home's second floor, containing multiple unending narrow hallways with rooms adjacent on each side. Additionally, stairways can also be found that may lead to different sections of the level with bright windows and wider hallways.

The design of the interior remains identical, maintaining consistency throughout the continuation of the level. The walls of both the rooms and hallways are made of cement and painted a shade of faded white that extends to the ceiling. In the hallways, the faded walls have a singular line of quartz running through them horizontally as part of their design. The floor is lined with standard-sized white ceramic tiles that reflect the natural light that penetrates through the walls.

The walls are made of solid concrete, but light from the exterior is able to permeate through them, giving them a distinct glow.

The ceiling has light fixtures, but it is undetermined whether they are functional, as there are no light switches to be found. It is also possible to encounter several security cameras attached to the ceiling in some of the hallways; majority of those encountered by wanderers appear to be deactivated.

Majority of the information from the brief synopsis above was provided by a group of wanderers who managed to have escaped the level. Due to the scarce information the database had on this level, the organization acted accordingly and decided to establish its existence. According to the group of wanderer's testimony, several of their companions had gone missing within the level.

After some deliberation, a compass point operative was assigned to lead a team of selected M.E.G. personnel to properly document and confirm the level’s existence. Encrypted here are the data entries of the team assigned to locate and secure the said level.

Date: 06/07/2019 ㅤLocation: Bedroom near Hallway #112
Time: 05:00 PMㅤㅤㅤㅤ Level: Level 327
John Willson, Senior Service Operative, First Response
Operational Data Entry 07

<Begin Log>


J.Willson: Operational Data Entry 07, the time is 5:00 pm, currently located in a bedroom, Level 327. When exploring a different section of the level, we ran into one of the other operatives of this mission, Kian Tanner, a track mapper specialist, who we haven't seen upon entry into this level.

We found him sat on the floor in a trancelike state, covered in blood, cooking what appeared to be two human fingers on a portable gas stove. It was quickly discovered that he had severed one of his own fingers on his right hand. He refused to respond when we asked where he got the second finger that he was cooking.

When we got him to come to his senses, he fainted due to exhaustion. And after he collapsed, Kim's left ear started bleeding. We were all filled with a sense of fear, so I carried Tanner on my back and we quickly retreated back where we came from, going back up the stairs into the normal sections of the level.

The strangest thing is, his bag was full of supplies. So whatever threat made him resort to eating his own fingers, must be psychological in nature.

Dhars was able to stop the bleeding, but until he can explain the other finger, we're tying him up.

We got something crazy going on here. At least we're not starving to death this week.

<End Log>

Date: 06/20/2019 ㅤLocation: Bed Room
Time: 08:12 AMㅤㅤㅤㅤ Level: Level 327
John Willson, Senior Service Operative, First Response
Operational Data Entry 11

<Begin Log>

Hello? Hello?




There's still some juice left… Let's do this.


This is Seltzer, First Response, Junior Service man reporting in. We……


I, uh…. the time…I think it's 8 am? I can't tell anymore, it always feels like 8 am here….


It's been approximately 13 days since I got separated from my group. And it's been officially a week since I ran out of food.


Anyway, I'm still alive. Running on my last flask of almond water. Well it's Tanner's almond water if you really think about it.


Something serious must have happened to jeopardize his mental state. I mean, he didn't even care that he was tied up. He didn't even say a word no matter how much Dhars tried to reason with him. And since we were starving, we all split his share amongst ourselves. Including him of course, but we still tied him up because we never figured out where he got that second finger.


For a while things were quiet. We never received any more transmissions from the main group since we found Tanner, no matter how much sir desperately tried calling out to them.


Thanks to Tanner's supplies, we were able to restock on blue tape. And so we continued to explore the level, running our trail of adhesives all over the floors of the hallways. Dhars was drawing up a map, but he eventually ran out of paper.


We didn't let the fear of starvation control us like Tanner did. We continued exploring the level, rationing the best we could, so that we last a little longer.


We tried to find exits to the level, but knowing that our entry point into this place wasn't in a fixed position, it was likely that the exits are the same.


One night we camped out in a room with a window. I kept watch as I watched over my fellow operatives as they fell asleep. Dhars was huddled up in the corner of the room, using his map as a blanket. Next to him Kim slept with with his back to the wall, still clutching his knife with a forceful grip. Sir Willson was laying on the floor beside him, he always looked more at peace asleep than awake.

Tanner was on the opposite side of the room. Still tied up, shifting sides every now and then. Probably distraught from what he'd seen.


That night, I stood infront of the window, to see the vast neighborhood of houses and streets. It could almost pass for a normal neighborhood in the real world. Minus the lack of people and deafening silence, of course. That alone reminded me that it this was all an illusion. An empty reflection of what it was supposed to be. A mistake.


And then suddenly Tanner got up. He was panicking. Screaming gibberish as he started banging his head on the wall. This was the first time he had spoken since we found him, and now it was as if he was speaking a language we couldn't understand.


Everyone woke up, and long story short, he charged at me head on, pushing me out the window. I…. probably could have avoided him, but I was honestly too tired to move. Fatigue had set in on my body more than my mind, rendering all my actions sluggish.


Both of us went flying out the window that day. He fell through as soon as I was halfway to the ground. I could hear the depth of the sound from the shattering window pane echoing through the room where I once stood.


Then I blacked out.



Next thing I know, I'm in a different room, surrounded by shards of glass and broken pieces of ceramic mugs. My eyes searched around the room for Tanner, but he was nowhere to be found. It appears that the damage from the impact of the fall was mitigated greatly, as it had left me with a few cuts on my right arm and a sprained ankle, instead of a bone fracture.



I limped out of the room, only to find myself in a completely uncharted area of the level. No trail of tape, no way back. Tanner's satchel was with me when I fell, so at least I still had some source of nourishment.


And that leads me here. I'm still not dead. Although it's going to look that way real soon if I give up. I'm faced with two options. Try to make it back to the others …or find a way to leave this level.


If you listen to this recording, know that I'm not dead, and I don't plan to be.


<End Log>

Date: 09/03/2019 ㅤLocation: M.E.G. Base Camp, Sector 25
Time: 2:00 PMㅤㅤㅤㅤ Level: Level 327
John Willson, Senior Service Operative, First Response
Operational Data Entry

<Begin Log>


Operation data entry 11, John Willson, Senior Service Operator, First Response. The time is 02:00 pm. Our current location is sector 25, the site of our base of operations.


Just when we almost ran out of food, we found a different tape line. Eventually it led us to another set of stairs. We were all still suspicious of following it considered what happened last time. But we were running out of food, so we decided to risk it. It led us to an area similar to the last room we were in, but this time, this time there was nothing strange going on, no gust of wind, no sound of sizzling oil on a frying pan. That's when we found the rest of the main team.


Two weeks of desperate searching, we finally found the main team's trail, leading us directly to them. They had set up a base and settled in some of the rooms. And as it turns out, one of their guys went missing too. Said he got violent and jumped out a window. Sounds familiar, doesn't it?



Turns out the entire team had been sitting here this whole time. Pomerantz came to greet us with relief, and someone explained that they were able to solve the resource problem in this level by creating their own source of almond water. How they were able to do that, I don't know.


Pomerantz said that until we get new instructions, that information is classified. But honestly, we didn't care. We were thirsty.


When we replenished my strength, we joined several search parties with the other map trackers, exploring more and more of this place, trying to find the missing we've lost, and there was still no sign of them.


After a month of living off nothing but almond water, someone found an a fixed exit point, to everyone's delight. Since then more frontliners would show up to check in every now and then. And then according to the higher-ups, majority of of the members from the joint operation were to remain on this level, not really explaining why. It was later revealed to us by Pomerantz the true objective of this special operation.


Turns out the plan was to set up an outpost here after all. "Increase and widen the efficiency of supply routes between outposts." More like just another power play by the M.E.G. to expand its territory. Honestly, I'm not surprised.


So far the Major Exploration Group is the biggest and most influential organization in this surreal dreamscape. And they'll probably want to expand their influence more, due to the rise of hostile groups.



So anyway, we followed the new orders and set up an outpost. And after a while, most of the operatives from entity removal were given new assignments, including Kim, who wasn't too happy about it. Apparently Dhars, Kim and Tanner were together when it happened, so she was obviously enraged at the loss of his friend. That's why me and Dhars made a promise to find him…. or his body at the very least.



A month passed, and one of them mappers stumbled upon an audio recorder. It was the one I gave to Seltzer before he disappeared. And after listening to the last entry, I feel his resolve to stay alive.



I know that he's still out there, somewhere.



And I'm going to do what I can to find him.


<End Log>

Entities

There is no presence of entities on Level 327, but it remained unsafe due to the lack of consumable resources required for sustaining basic physiological needs, which are essential for survivability. Food, water and Almond Water are absent in this level, which results in a majority of unconfirmed casualties of wanderers in this level being due to starvation or terminal dehydration.

The exact estimate of people that have died in this level is unconfirmed as no bodies have been discovered. Several personal effects of individuals were found within the rooms, some of them covered in blood.

Additional Information:

"More mugs."

"Hey Kim, dragons are a good omen, right?"

There are some anomalies that exist within this level, most notably its deafening silent ambience. The time of day does not change. The level does not have a day/night cycle and is stuck at approximately somewhere between 06:30 to 08:00 AM. Despite the thickness of the cement walls light is able to penetrate through them providing every room and hallway this early morning brightness. Occasionally, a light breeze of air may be felt passing through the same thick cement walls where ventilation is impossible. The breeze only occurs in the hallways, so it is unlikely to come from the windows from some of the rooms. The level is lacking of other integral aspects, such as sound. It is mostly eerily quiet, with the only sounds being made from the footsteps of other wanderers, and the occasional gust of the sea breeze. The exact cause for this is unknown.

Additionally, light filters in through some of the windows that can be found in some of the rooms. The exterior of these windows are visible; it could be described as being positioned on the second floor of a house in a normal neighborhood that cannot be interacted with, as the ground at the bottom is unstable.

According to the recovered data logs, two operatives fell out the window. It was later discovered that doing so will send any object or living entity back into a random room on Level 327. In this instance, the operative noclipped midair, entering a room through the ceiling, causing him to experience the force of gravity, leaving the operative to have sustained mild injuries. It is advised to be careful around rooms with these windows.

Bases, Outposts and Communities:

M.E.G Outpost: "Breakfast Club"

  • This outpost consist of 20 M.E.G. personnel currently stationed, all of them from different divisions. The expedition into Level 327 was classified as a joint-special operation. This is done after Compass Point Operatives discover levels that are safe and habitable, or have potential to become so.
  • The operatives registered their outpost as "breakfast club" because every meal taken in this level is consumed in the presence of perpetual rays of light.
  • The outpost is located in the 25th sector of level, which can be accessed by following the trails of green tape, which indicate safe routes to get there.
  • A supply depot that connects other outposts.
  • Friendly to wanderers and other allied groups.
  • Frequently visited by M.E.G. operatives passing through.
  • Trades exclusively with M.E.G. operatives and non-affiliated wanderers.

Entrances and Exits

The entrances are difficult to find, but they may be found by crawling under beds that have light radiating from underneath them. Level 162 is the most common route, although it is frequently used by M.E.G. personnel. Although prior to the joint-special operation, there have also been some reports of the entrances showing up in level 64.


Leaving level 327 has proven to be difficult, as the exit points tend to shift every few hours. Although it is still possible to encounter a shifting exit point, one would need to leave the same way they entered the level, which is by crawling underneath a bed.

The only fixed exit point has been discovered and is currently controlled by the M.E.G., its location is far from the outpost, thus escort runs led by the breakfast club are a common occurrence.
