Level 330 - "A Stroke of Colour"
rating: +46+–x

SURVIVAL DIFFICULTY:

Class P

  • Unsecure
  • Hazardous Transformation
  • NO HUMANS ALLOWED!!!

A photo of one of the entryways to Level 330, located in Level 57.

Level 330 is the 331st Level of the Backrooms. Visually, it takes the form of an animated child's painting.

Description

The level itself takes on a unique effect that makes the surrounding area look as if it was hand-painted, and as if 2D objects are standees within a 3D space, which also affects entities that one may come across while traversing this level. However, the wanderer will see their own body and other wanderers' bodies as normal. Wanderers are only able to view the world around them to a certain distance, as the colour fades out into a blank canvas past a certain point. Due to this, it is impossible to theorise or measure the scale of this level.

The longer one stays within the level, the chances of them finding a way out will increase. However, it is recommended one searches for an exit at all times. While the level is relatively safe, containing only Facelings and Wretches, wanderers are advised not to stay for longer than two hours. The longer they stay, the more they risk irreversibly transforming through a process known as the 'Inking Process'.

Inking Process Transformation Phases

Phase Hours Passed Effect
Phase One One Hour Wanderer begins to leave footprints made out of paint behind them, usually the colour of the underside of their shoe.
Phase Two Two Hours Wanderer will begin to take on the visual effect of the level, with their body being stylised by brush strokes.
Phase Three Three Hours Skin and clothes of wanderer will begin to drip paint. Escape is impossible at this point, as attempting to exit or no-clip will result in the wanderer's body splattering into paint on the surface they were attempting to no-clip through, killing them.
Phase Four Four Hours Wanderer will become locked in place, unable to move along the 3D plane. The wanderer can still move the rest of their body in the 2D plane in this phase, however their body will begin to undergo drastic changes, rapidly morphing into a stylised version of themselves. How a wanderer looks will depend on their own artistic ability, and whether they continue to live past this point.
Phase Five Five Hours The body of the wanderer will become permanently frozen in place. If their artsyle is not realistic enough for internal organs to fit, the wanderer will have died by the time this process is complete, but if the wanderer's style has realistic proportions, they maintain full sentience.

Due to the final stage of the inking process, wanderers consider it a kindness to die in this process, rather than be frozen in place until the end of time. The Inking Process can be reverted should the wanderer find an exit before the third hour begins, and their body will turn back to normal after a few hours.

Discovery Logs

+ First Discovery Log
- Hide Log

The following log was acquired from the first wanderer to successfully make it out of the level, and back to a M.E.G. base safely:

13:28: Okay… starting this recording now! I think I've found a new level? I was wandering through Level 57 when I came across this weird painting that… seemed to be moving all on it's own. Unlike the paintings in the level usually, this one seemed to have the opposite effect, like it wanted me to to touch it, and when I did, I found myself in this new area! I'm going to look around, as it seems relatively safe right now.

13:42: The way this level looks is kinda like… oh, what was that one- Paper Mario? It's been years since I last played one of those games. Anyways, it's like there's cardboard cutouts everywhere… bushes, trees, you name it! Haven't found much else yet, not even any entities or wanderers…

13:45: Oh nevermind, spotted a faceling. I can't really tell what kind it is from here, and I am definitely not about to find out. I think there was a wretch further back, thinking about it now. I thought that was just a weird looking rock…

14:01: I see… a human standee? It… weirdly doesn't look painted. There's a couple of them actually! I know it's a bad idea but… I need to go check.

14:03: Oh god- they're wanderers! All of them are wanderers!

14:06: Some had died long ago, the ones that were still alive, they- they screamed at me, told me to run, leave, before it's too late, before I become like them. Paint's started appearing under my shoes, this level… it does things to wanderers. Bad things.

14:48: Everywhere I run, I keep finding more and more people. I think I've noticed the difference in who stays alive and who dies here. It's always the stick figures and most disfigured that are dead, and those who's bodies look normal stay alive.

15:18: My body is starting to look weird now. it's taking on the same texture of this level… Fuck, I hope I can find my way out soon. Still haven't spotted much I could easily no-clip through, and I've tried a lot of the bushes so far.

15:25: There's a lake! I'm going to try jumping in, please for the love of god don't have some weird entity in here.

After the final log entry, the wanderer found themselves on Level 43, covered in blue paint, presumably from the lake they had jumped into. From there, they were able to navigate their way back to a safe level to report their findings.

MEG EXPLORATION LOG


Time Start: April 15th, 2022, 11:20

Recording Device: Handheld Transceiver

Entry Point: Level 57

M.E.G. Team Leader: Officer Eric Blane

PREFACE: A group of three M.E.G. Task Force members were sent to investigate the transformation effect that occurs in Level 330, as reported by a wanderer who escaped said level. The following text below is a transcript of their exploration.

<BEGIN LOG>


Blane: Alright squad, we do not have long until we have to leave, be on the look out for any human-like standees, and any close-by exits. We need answers, and we need a way out.

Virta: I… already do not like this place, looks like a five-year-old made it.

Osvin: And what, you can paint better than this? You can't even draw stick figures, man.

Virta: Shut the fuck up you-

Blane: Enough, you two, we do not have time to sit and argue about artistic ability. We will not find what we are looking for if you two keep messing around. It's time to focus.

Virta: Ugh, fine.

Osvin: Yes, sir.

[Time - 11:50]


[SOUNDS OF THE SQUAD WALKING ARE ABLE TO BE HEARD.]

Osvin: Sir, I found one!

Blane: Do they look alive?

Osvin: Seems to be!

Blane: Good. Virta, continue to search for an exit point.

Virta: Really? Me?

Blane: That is an order, Virta.

Virta: Ugh, fine… fuck's sake…

Blane: Osvin, start setting everything up. How long have we been in here for so far?

Osvin: About half an hour, sir. We have plenty of time before we have to go.

Blane: Good. Keep an eye on that.

[SOUNDS OF FOOTSTEPS AND EQUIPMENT BEING SET UP CAN BE HEARD.]

(Note: The following standee responses are only a rough translation1.)

Standee: Leave! Run! Leave!

Blane: No. We're here to talk to you. We want to know what caused… this.

Osvin: Please? We want to try and help if we can.

Standee: Can't! Splattered if we try! Lost friends!

Osvin: Oh… do you know how long it took for them to get to that point?

Standee: I think… 3 hours… paint drips… from their body… does not like paintless…

Blane: How are you understanding what they're saying?

Osvin: Odd hobby of mine, but I learnt how to get good at ventriloquism when I was younger. It's amazing what boredom and strict parents with paranoia over technology can do to a kid.

Blane: What… the fuck?

Osvin: I'll show you some time, if we get out of here. Now then…

Osvin: How do you guys stay alive?

Standee: Lost… feelings… hunger… thirst… no more.

Osvin: How do only some of you survive?

Standee: Only some… survive… good artists… only survive… sticks die… incorrect die… must have it all…

Osvin: Well… guess Virta wouldn't live, heh. Hey, do you know what's causing this to happen?

Standee: This level… it lives… does not like… people… hear its voice… screams… constantly angry…

Osvin: That's… horrifying. How long did it take then to make you like this?

Standee: I think… fifth hour… it was done… fourth hour… so much pain… so much pain… so much pain so much pain so much pain so much pain- (Standee begins to scream) LEAVE!

Osvin: That's- that's not the same voice!

Standee: PAINTLESS! TALENTLESS! LEAVE! MY WORLD! NO HUMANS ALLOWED!

Osvin: Why do you hate humans?

Standee: TRAMPLE! MESS! TEAR! MEAN! HUMANS ARE MEAN!

Virta (through walkie-talkie): Blane, sir! Found a possible exit! A building of sorts, the door seems to lead back to… I think it's Level 1. North-East of where you guys are currently.

Blane: Good, we'll be with you momentarily.

Virta: The hell is with all that screaming? Something go wrong over there?

Blane: No, I believe we have all the information we need. We will explain once we get back to base. Osvin, we're leaving soon.

Osvin: But sir! We could learn so much right now-

Blane: This being is clearly agitated by our presence. We need to leave. I'm getting paint under my shoe.

Osvin: … alright then. Give me a few minutes to pack up.

Standee: GO! LEAVE! MY WORLD! NOT YOURS!

[THE NEW VOICE CONTINUES TO SHOUT AT BLANE AND OSVIN UNTIL THEY LEAVE TO LOCATE VIRTA, AT WHICH POINT THE RECORDING ENDS.]

<END LOG>

Bases, Outposts and Communities

Due to the nature of this level, no bases or outposts have been made on this level.

Entrances And Exits

Entrances

The only way to enter this level is through a painting with a moving image on the canvas, usually depicting a house on green grass, with a blue sky and yellow sun. These paintings can usually be found in any levels that have an art gallery or museum involved, such as Level 57 or level-321. There have been reports of the painting appearing in Level 5, but this has yet to be confirmed.

Exits

Jumping into a lake can lead you to one of the following:

  • Level 7
  • Level 43
  • Level 130
  • Level 173

You will enter the new level covered in blue paint if you choose this method.

Entering any door connected to a building can lead you to one of the following:

  • Level 1
  • Level 4
  • Level 9
  • Level 11

Doors not connected to a building will lead you to a random level, including negative levels.

There's a good girl.

The little girl returned to her painting room hours later. She pouted at her painting, one that was once again tainted by humans, and sat down in front of it once more. What could she do? There was no way she could simply paint a message for them to leave, it would ruin her art!

Her thoughts cut-off as the bucket next to the canvas jingled. She peeked inside, seeing a new popstick puppet— another human who succumbed to her painting's will —appear within the small collection that had formed within.

With that, she had an idea.

Taking to some crayon and paper, she created a small card. She knew the rules of the canvas: anything placed upon them will display in all of the clones, no matter the material. She docked the card into the bottom right corner, and smiled with joy, thinking that finally, the humans would leave her work alone.

She couldn't wait to show the caretaker her solution.

PLEASE STOP GOING IN HERE
