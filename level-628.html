Level 628 - "The Island"
rating: +17+–x

This is a land of research. Without knowledge, we have nothing.
~The Changer

A rough concept sketch of The Island, featuring A Lonely Girl. Signed "Trusty".

Level 628 is the 629th Level of The Backrooms. Level 628 is a Level which is split into three different sectors, labeled as "The Desert", "The Ocean", and "The Island." Each of these sections are connected to each other, and all vary drastically through all mediums. The most notable feature of all these sectors it their periwinkle sand, as well as the time period always being night.

Description:

Level 628 is a multi-sectored Level, which has a varying level of danger throughout. Every section of the Level is interconnected to each other, and can all be accessed in their own unique way, as well as left. Every section of the Level has been secured in a different way, and are all habitable to some extent. There are exceptions to the habitability in certain sections of the Level, however, these areas are currently kept under M.E.G. control to prevent wanderers from entering. Every section of this Level is undergoing research in order to explain its strange properties.

Level 628 has been noted to have a few consistent features throughout each section. The most eye-catching feature would have to be the periwinkle sand which is present throughout the Level. This sand has been tested for any different minerals which may be present, and none have been found. It is currently unknown how this sand is able to support any life, as it doesn't appear to have any water in it. Despite this, all types of flora can be seen growing from this strange sand. The sand appears to not have always been periwinkle, which may suggest that outside forces were involved in changing the color of the sand.

Level 628 is constantly in a "night time" cycle, despite their being evidence of daytime being present throughout the Level within the flora. Stars can be seen throughout the sky, though they don't appear to be in any way connected to a sun. When viewed through any magnifying device, they appear as specks of light, suggesting that they may be under The Backrooms properties. The biggest feature within this night sky is an apparent moon being visible throughout. This moon is noted to be very large, and it is unknown if this is due to a bigger size or due to the moon being closer than it appears to be. Despite their being no apparent "sun" within this Level, the Level is quite luminescent, and darkness has never been a worry.

Level 628's three sectors are labeled after what they generally represent as a whole. The Desert is a vast expanse of periwinkle sand, filled with different areas that you would typically find within a desert. The Ocean is a vast expanse of water, filled to the brim with many mysterious aquatic Entities. Alongside the Entities, many peculiar features can be found within the life, as well as the not so living things within this area. The Island is the most mysterious part of Level 628, and is a single piece of land jolting out from The Ocean, covered in periwinkle sand and a single tree. Each of the sections are connected through various means, and all stand out wildly from each other.

Many flora and fauna on Level 628 have been noted to be vibrantly contrasting from the standard flora and fauna. For an example, many bushes within the area glow a deep shade of purple, and blend in well to the environment. The Ocean is filled with many species of coral, which all seem to follow the theme of cooler colors. The only exception to this is found on The Island, which despite having periwinkle sand, and being within the same bounds of experiencing this effect, has a single tree, which is shaded a standard color for palm trees. The reasons for this tree being this way are largely unknown, and all current attempts to learn about this have resulted in failure, due to the unique properties of The Island.

The current size of Level 628 is unknown, which is largely due to the nature of the Level itself. A common theme throughout all sectors of this Level is losing something once you lose sight of it. Whenever you leave The Island, then attempt to turn around in order to find it again, you will find yourself incapable of locating The Island once more. This theme is consistent throughout every section of this Level, and is to be considered a given.

The Desert

The Desert is a vast expanse of land, covered completely with the aforementioned periwinkle sand. This sand extends very throughout the entirety of The Desert, and appears to affect the fauna and flora in this section. The flora found throughout The Desert varies wildly, and has been noted to sometimes feature fauna and flora which are not typically found within a standard desert. This has been theorized to be due to the strange properties of the periwinkle sand located on this Level. The most common piece of fauna which can be found is a Carnegiea gigantea1, which is noted to be nova purple.

The Desert is filled with an abundance of Entities, all of which are unique to this Level exclusively. Many Entities within The Desert have yet to be documented, due largely to The Desert featuring a large amount of Entities, making documentation difficult. A common theme throughout many Entities throughout The Desert has been the coloration, which is noted to be largely either blue, purple, or periwinkle. The biology of many Entities throughout The Desert has varied, though they all appear to be mammals to some extent.

Areas within The Desert

Currently, within The Desert, there are 4 unique and documented areas. However, The Desert has been known to host many more unique types of areas. For this reason, this data is not concrete in a literal sense, and is rather what is currently understood of The Desert itself. The Desert has been known to be the most varying of the three sectors, with each section being drastically different from the last.

The Desert is largely unexplored due to the unique nature of it twisting around you. When wandering through The Desert, key landmarks are lost quite quickly. For example, walking away from The Oasis until it is out of sight, then turning around to return to The Oasis may result in you losing The Oasis altogether. This phenomena is quite common within The Desert, similar to the way it functions within The Depths of The Ocean.

Areas of The Deserts
...
The Sands

The Sands are the most common part of The Desert, and make up a vast majority of The Desert itself. The Sands host many Entities, including Synotics and Blys, as well as numerous other undocumented Entities. The Sands have been noted to be much more devoid of flora and fauna than the other areas within this section, however, this does not mean that there is no living plant life. The Sands have been noted to contain the most Carnegiea gigantea, alongside many other variations of cactus, all shaded nova purple. Whenever you enter this Level through The Desert, this is the most common area you'll end up in.

There are many shacks and buildings around The Sands, however, they are rather difficult to find. This is largely due to the randomness featured within The Desert itself, and the way it conforms to the idea of random changes and turns in environment. However, whenever you know what a specific building within The Sands, and seek out to find it, you will find yourself at a much higher chance of encountering it compared to before. Many bases are set up within The Sands, due to the amount of space available.

Dehydration is rather rapid within The Sands, and occurs faster here over any other area within this section. Water is uncommon within The Sands. However, Almond Water can be found bottled randomly throughout The Desert occasionally, for no specified reason. It is suspected that these Almond Water bottles are left by wanderers, though their motivations, as well as their intent, are unclear. Currently, The Sands is the most secured section of The Desert.

The Dunes

The Dune are considered to be the most dangerous area within The Desert. It can be found uncommonly whenever wandering throughout The Desert. The Dunes houses multiple Entities, nearly all of which are dangerous, including species of The Grand Periwinkle Worm. Periwinkles can be seen being blown around within the wind. It is advised that you avoid this area.

The Dunes are one of the few areas within The Sands which experiences any wind cycle at all, which is the biggest defining factor which separates it from The Sands. Other then the wind picking up large amounts of periwinkle sand throughout the air, there is very little that differentiates The Dunes from The Sands. There has been notes of unique flora within The Dunes, however, these are unconfirmed as of now. The flora rumored to be within The Dunes has been noted to be incredibly potent in making highly addictive drugs, so many seek out The Dunes for this reason alone.

The Dunes have a M.E.G. outpost set up around the perimeter in as many locations as possible. For this reason, The Dunes are one of the only places within The Desert that has a confirmed size from an outside point of view, which is estimated to be 50 kilometers squared. However, it is suspected, due both to the fact that when wandering within, the size appears much bigger, alongside the fact that the size fluctuates whenever a M.E.G. base begins to disperse, the size is much bigger then it appears to be externally. The exact size of The Dunes cannot be calculated, due to the danger presented by wandering within The Dunes, as well as the nature of The Desert itself.

The Dunes itself has to main exits, which are both somewhat rare. When wandering, at random, you'll suddenly emerge outside of The Dunes, into The Sands. The reasons for this are unknown, and you won't be able to return to The Dunes once you leave, as you'll be in a random location in The Sands, until you can relocate The Dunes. The other exit is through The Sand Bunker, which can be seen occasionally in the distance. Should you enter, you will be in The Sand Bunker, and will once more be incapable of going back out to The Dunes once you should lose sight off The Dunes.

The Oasis

The Oasis is another area which can be found rarely whenever you wander throughout The Desert. The Oasis has the unique capability of diverting nearly all Entities within, excluding the occasional periwinkle which wanders astray, alongside an occasional Aquawink which sometimes finds its way to The Desert, and seeks refuge. The Oasis is exactly its namesake; a body of freshwater within The Desert, which can be found uncommonly. It is currently unknown if there is only a single instance of The Oasis, or if multiple copies of The Oasis exist across The Desert. It is likely at least two instances of The Oasis exist, though this is impossible to prove, and there may be multiple, or a single one.

The Oasis itself has a very unique look surrounding it. A dip within the periwinkle sand can be observed, which is filled with standard freshwater. Almond Water has been rumored to occasionally appear within The Oasis, typically at time periods where The Island was inaccessible, however, these rumors are impossible to confirm, and for this reason are meant to be considered lies. Surrounding The Oasis, multiple bushes and fauna can be observed, which all appear to gain nutrients from the water within The Oasis. These fauna are the only fauna observed to require water, as attempts to make them lose water have proven to kill the plant. Reasonings for this are largely unknown. A single palm like tree can be observed near the water, though its position changes often, and it is colored a deep maroon shade, making it stand out much more.

The Oasis has multiple distinct features, which each hold their own unique properties. Near one end of The Oasis, a Well can be spotted if you look through the bushes enough. This Well appears to draw people closer to it, for reasons unknown. This effect only occurs should you be aware of the Well itself, including its position. Should you approach the Well, you will inevitably fall into the Well itself. This effect causes you to fall for an extended period of time, before eventually re-emerging from the Well, through reasons unknown. The effect is known to be disorienting, and very difficult to describe, which is the reasons for the vagueness in this description, as very few can describe it in an understandable way.

The Sand Bunker

The Sand Bunker can occasionally be found whenever you choose to wander within The Desert, though it has been more commonly reported within The Dunes. The Sand Bunker is a vast cave system made up of various sub-sections. Within The Sand Bunker, the majority of the space is sandstone, colored in a periwinkle fashion, though it is a deeper shade. The Sand Bunker is made up of multiple widely varying components, each of which will be described as follows:

The Channels

The Channels are the largest section of The Sand Bunker, and make up a majority of The Sand Bunker. The Channels are a set of vast caves, each varying drastically to each other in shape and size. Auroras have been reported within The Channels, however, very few Entities wander astray into The Channels. The reasoning for this is presumed to be the lack of things needed to survive within The Desert, such as Almond Water and food.

The Channels have been noted to vary drastically within themselves. The most common appearance of The Channels is a thin hallway, surrounded with no visible life, though Dens can be seen within the subsection. Occasionally, you may encounter a vast open cave, filled with only stalagmites2. The rarest section of The Channels are The Clusters, which appear as small openings within the thin hallways. Within The Clusters, Almond Water is common, and an exit back into The Sands can be found, most commonly appearing as a gap within the roof.

The Bunker

The Bunker is a rare section which can be found within The Sand Bunker, most commonly whilst wandering throughout The Channels. The Bunker is small in comparison to the other sections of this area, especially considering The Channels. The only Entity which can be located within The Bunker are Periwinkles, which have been noted to appear in very high quantities. However, the Periwinkles within The Bunker have evolved to be highly acidic, and similar in flavor too sandstone. For these reasons, it is highly dis-advised to attempt to consume any Periwinkles within The Bunker.

The Bunker itself is a mini array of sandstone, which many can confuse with The Clusters within The Channels. For this reason, Periwinkles are the only callsign of The Bunker itself. Within The Bunker, many "shelves" can be found, which are really indents of sandstone within The Bunker. On these shelves, bottles of Almond Water appear uncommonly, however, Cashew Water has been spotted on these shelves. For this reason, it is highly advised against to attempt to consume any liquids in this section. The only other notable feature is the "tables", which are indents of sandstone from within the ground. It is noteworthy to point out that no chairs are in this section, except for the ones previous wanderers have left behind within The Bunker.

When leaving The Bunker, you commonly appear back within The Channels. However, it has been noted on rare occasions that when exiting The Bunker, you will slowly no-clip your way into The Ocean, more specifically, The Depths. As The Depths pose a minor threat to wanderers, it is advised to be prepared when exiting The Bunker, in order to avoid any potential threat of The Depths themself. The effect of the no-clip is noticeably different from a standard no-clip, being much more smooth. The transition of no-clip has been noted to feel like slowly changing positions, as you can feel yourself slowly emerging more and more into The Depths themself.

Selzas Spire

Selzas Spire can be encountered randomly when wandering throughout The Channels, and is regarded for being quite rare. Selzas Spire can appear as virtually anything, though it is found most commonly as a tower, similar to the stereotypical Wizard's Tower. Selzas Spire has 3 layers, each of which vary wildly.

  • Layer 1 is a largely empty space, and serves as a welcoming chamber to Selzas Spire. It has many unique books, none of which are written in any understandable language. The exception is a dusty old book which is always found hidden away. The book is largely illegible, with the exception of one word in the corner of page 12, which states "Selza".
  • Layer 2 is much smaller, but still is notable. The layer is filled to the brim with many different liquids, and you'll have to swim through the entirety of the layer. The exception to this is the entryway, which holds back all the liquids with a bubble-like encasing. The current colors each have a different effect on climbing, and a riddle can be found floating near the entryway. This riddle changes up every time you enter, however, each riddle has three notable occurrences. Pink liquid is always harmful, and has a high chance of killing you. Cyan liquid (if present) is breathable, and mends wounds slowly overtime. Finally, gray liquid always puts you to sleep, awakening and hour later back in the original bubble encasing. When swimming through, bodies can be seen floating amuck in the water.
  • Layer 3 is the most confusing of each of the layers, as it is a floating state of mind. The layer holds one strong regret in your mind, and shows you it deeply. This can include a person from your past, an object from your past, etc. If you should confront this Entity, an Aurora will slither in, and bite you immediately.

This layer is still largely misunderstood, due to its rare chance of appearing. It is largely regarded as an exit to Level 628.

Stairway to the 629

At random, no matter the location you are at (with the exception of Selzas Spire), you have a small chance of encountering a stairway. This stairway appears to lead back into The Desert, however, it is a stairway which leads directly to Level 629. This stairway typically causes you to emerge near any of the other entryways to Level 629, so be wary. This is largely regarded as an exit to Level 628 as a whole, as it is rather common, and can be found nearly anywhere within The Sand Bunker.

The staircase itself isn't made of sandstone, but rather, falling periwinkle sand. This effect has been noted to cause a haze within wanderers, as the sand is able to walked on, however, the sand also falls beneath you, typically in a mesmerizing way. This effect seems to carry on as long as you watch the Stairway to 629. The sand itself is the only example of sand which isn't periwinkle, instead being a shade of color commonly found within sand.

The Den

The Den is a collective name for a set of dens which can be found when roaming The Channels. The Dens can easily be unnoticed, as they are hidden in with the environment. These dens hold a few minor things, such as patches of grass and logs of water. However, the biggest feature of these dens are the amount of Auroras present. Typically, each den holds 1-3 Auroras, and as such, they are easy access to leaving Level 628. The Den is noted for being the easiest way to leave Level 628.

The Exit

The Exit can be found rarely when wandering through The Sand Bunker for a prolonged period of time. It exist as an arch, which, when entered, causes you to appear within a random point of The Desert. The arch is made out of sandstone, and it is unknown how it lets you exit The Sand Bunker so seamlessly. The Exit is one of the only two ways to exit The Sand Bunker, and is largely regarded as safe to enter. However, there have been reports of re-emerging in The Dunes, so be wary of exiting.

Entities

Throughout The Desert, a wide variety of Entities have been spotted, however, only a few have been documented to the point of being noteworthy inclusions within the Entities listed below. Many insects can be seen, however, they tend to behave similarly to insects from The Frontrooms, with the exception of the coloration, always falling under the cooler end of the spectrum. For this reason, only Entities which have been seen as noteworthy to have a section under the Entities list are included below. The Entities of The Desert are as follows:

Entities of The Desert
...
Periwinkles

These Entities are small puffball-like Entities which can be found throughout the desert. They show no harm to others, and are known to be a delicacy among wanderers. They can be eaten at any point, as they repel germs and dirt when rolling through The Desert.

Periwinkles themselves resemble a small ball, around 4 inches in diameter, colored in the shade of periwinkle. They have no internal organs, and exist purely as flesh, with fur surrounding itself. They have legs, though these are quite small, and any wind can cause them to lose balance, They spend their time wandering around, doing little to nothing. They are considered to many wanderers as "wandering morsels".

Synotics

Synotics are similar to illusions or mirages. Due to the periwinkle sand in The Desert, mirages are incapable of being produced. However, in cases of severe dehydration, Synotics shall appear as distant, purple-colored humanoid like Entity. They shamble in the distance, and are incapable of being interacted with. They can appear alone, or in groups of two or three.

The Grand Periwinkle Worm

The main Entity in The Desert which poses a threat is The Grand Periwinkle Worm. The Grand Periwinkle Worm are small worms, similar in size to Memory Worms. The Grand Periwinkle Worm seeks out malnourished Wanderers and attempts to sneak into their body whenever they can. This includes ways such as ears, eyes, pores, acne, open wounds, etc.

The Grand Periwinkle Worm has been speculated to have connections to The Water Hydra, both due to it being similar in shape, simply smaller and a different color, as well as due to the theories related to The Island, however, this is impossible to prove. The Grand Periwinkle Worm is colored a shade of periwinkle, which is also speculated to be connected to The Water Hydra. Occasionally, a person can see The Grand Periwinkle Worm mating with another of its kind, and it has been described to be similar to wrapping two dough strings together.

Blys

Blys are a minor Entity which resemble mini birds, similar in size too flies. They are able to be domesticated, and are much like pets. They will show no harm too Wanderers, unless you attempt to splash them with liquids. From that point, they will go very hostile and will swarm around you. However, due to their size, they are only capable of causing minor scratches to the skin. Interestingly, any cuts which appear due to Blys will be healed quite quickly, with periwinkle skin tone appearing instead of normal skin tone.

Auroras

Auroras are a unique type of snake found in The Desert. They appear as illusory images, similar in look to a purple nova snake. They appear rather rarely in the distance of The Desert, and can be glimpsed. Should you choose to walk towards and Aurora, they will show behaviors similar to that of a snake. If you get close enough, they will then bite you, typically on the leg. Once they bite you, you will pass out nearly immediately, awakening on level-18, commonly beside Entity 198.

The Ocean

The Ocean can be found on the outskirts of The Desert, as well as surrounding The Island. The Ocean is the biggest section of Level 628, and it is currently unconfirmed to have an end. The Ocean is divided into multiple sections, both over water an under water. Within The Ocean, three separate boats can be found, named The Wooden Boat, The Periwinkle Boat, and The Presidents Boat. All water in The Ocean can be breathed in for unknown reasons, and the water seems to have no effect on the eyes.

Areas within The Ocean

Areas of The Ocean
...
The Shallows

The Shallows are the easiest section to access within The Ocean. They are accessible through either The Desert or The Island, and are the section closest to land in The Ocean. The Shallows are mostly devoid of Entities, beyond the occasional stray Periwinkle from The Desert which winds up sunk at the bottom. The Shallows themselves are largely similar to a standard Shallows in The Frontrooms, complete with coral, rocks, fauna, etc.

The water within The Shallows is shaded a very light bright of aqua, suggesting that any dirty remnants which enter the water do not pollute the water in any way.

Swimming under the water in The Shallows is largely harmless, other than then need for water, as there are no Entities within The Shallows. The ground is visible from The Shallows, and can be touched easily. It is impossible to enter The Depths underwater through The Shallows, as attempting to will cause you to turn around, oftentimes without you realizing you turned around.

The Depths

The Depths are one of the more dangerous areas in The Ocean, and are directly outside The Shallows. They can be most visibly recognized by a difference in wave speeds, in which The Depths have slightly more harsh waves. The Wooden Boat can occasionally be found throughout this area, and is recommended to be found and ridden by all wanderers. The surface of The Depths are mostly harmless, though if any rocks are seen above water, they should be avoided at all cost. Sirens are commonly found on these rocks, and may cause harm to you if approached.

Going underwater in The Depths is highly unrecommended, as numerous Entities lurk about, many of which are incredibly hostile. Once you swim down to the point where you can't see the surface of the water, you will be trapped underneath The Depths, unless a Wizard helps you out. There is currently no confirmed bottom to The Depths, however, if you are lucky, you may see a bottom. If you see a bottom, it is highly recommended you swim towards it, as it is always Atlantis. The biggest threat in The Depths would be The Water Hydra, which should be avoided at all cost.

The Vortex

This section of The Ocean is rather rare to encounter, however, if you wander in the opposite direction of The Shallows for a few days, you'll likely end up here. The Vortex is a small swirling vortex, which has the capability to draw you down. It is not dangerous, and effectively serves as an entrance to Atlantis. There are no Entities present in The Vortex.

Atlantis

Atlantis is directly underneath The Vortex, though it can rarely be accessed from The Depths. Atlantis is a harmless location, and all Entities present are helpful towards you. The only Entities present are mutated Periwinkles and Merman. Atlantis itself is a beautiful kingdom which has numerous areas, both outside an in. Theres no supplies present, however, there is an underwater garden, a chapel, a bank filled with Drachmas, and a central court. There is housing, though the housing is largely unused, as all Merman typically work.

The Boats:

Boats of The Ocean
...
The Wooden Boat

The Wooden Boat is found on the surface of The Depths, and there appears to be multiple of The Wooden Boat. The Wooden Boat is sailable, and helps you to avoid the occasional lurking Siren, as well as navigate through The Depths much quicker. The Wooden Boat will also appear should you begin to swim up away from Atlantis, and will take you back up to The Vortex. The Wooden Boat itself cannot be sucked into The Vortex, and cannot leave The Depths, though it can linger near the edge. If you attempt to take The Wooden Boat into The Shallows, you will find that the boat simply sinks beneath you.

The Periwinkle Boat

When walking along the outskirts of The Desert, you can sometimes find the occasional boat shaded periwinkle. Once you board this boat, it will begin to gently carry you through The Shallows, until you reach The Depths. The Periwinkle Boat will then immediately suck you down The Depths with it, taking you directly through The Depths quite quickly. However, in a short period of time, you will find yourself at Atlantis. Once you exit The Periwinkle Boat, it will gently float back up to the surface.

The Presidents Boat

This boat can only be found in The Shallows near The Island. The Presidents Boat serves as a way to leave The Island, and will emerge should you walk away from the tree on The Island. The boat can be ignored, and if it is, will simply sink back down to the water below, disappearing once out of sight. However, when boarded, you will begin to sail away gently, until you inevitably fall asleep. You never seem to leave The Shallows, though The Island is no longer visible. Once you inevitably fall asleep, you will reawaken on Level 777, specifically, in Morgana Casinos. Should you leave The Presidents Boat before sleeping, you'll immediately begin sinking without being able to stop, emerging once more into Atlantis.

Entities

Entities of The Ocean
...
Sirens

Sirens are found within The Depths, only on the surface. They can be seen on rocks which poke out from above The Depths, and will typically beckon you to come closer. Once you approach a Siren, they will begin to attempt to puncture you with their razor-sharp scales. They will never kill you, and will leave once they confirm you cannot swim. You will sink for a period of time, eventually either being eaten by an Entity in The Depths, or else end up in Atlantis. Sirens themselves are rather attractive in nature, looking similar to a stereotypical mermaid3. They do not sing, but they are capable of speech.

Largris

Largris are a piranha like Entity which lurks throughout The Depths. Largris at first glance appear to be normal fish, which are simply tinted a deep shade of blue-green. However, they have been noted to occasionally swim at speeds up to 120mph. They tend to swim faster the more hungry they are, however, they are strictly herbivore, and only attack if threatened. Largris are typically found in schools, and can easily shred you to pieces in these schools should you attack them.

Microspecks

Microspecks are an advance form of bacteria which can be found throughout The Depths. They float around, and are impossible to avoid. When breathed in, ingested, or otherwise are put into your body, they will begin to cause you to excrete gulps of periwinkle-colored gas. This appears to be harmless, however, it does cause anything it touches to be colored periwinkle for 5-7 days. The excrement comes out of any pores within your body, albeit your nose, your eyes, acne, etc.

Lagoona

Lagoona are a sentient form of coral, which can be seen floating around The Depths. They seem to wander aimlessly, however, when approached, they will begin to rapidly beat any wanderers who come into close contact with it. Lagoonas will use any part of their body to beat you, including their stems, their roots, and their branches. Lagoonas are quite powerful, and can only be neutralized through deception. This comes most commonly in the form of periwinkle excrement from Microspecks.

Wizards

Wizards are a humanoid type Entity which can be seen swimming around The Depths. They show no reaction to any Entities, and are never targeted by Lagoona. Whenever approached, they are quite friendly. If you ask one to submerge, they will swim up with you through The Depths. After a random period of time, you will emerge at the surface of The Depths. This is currently the only know way to resurface in The Depths.

The Water Hydra

The Water Hydra is a highly intelligent type of serpent similar in size to a boa constrictor. There is currently only one in The Depths, and it should be avoided at all cost. The Water Hydra will actively seek out any wanderers it sees, and once close enough, it will begin to slowly wrap itself around said wanderer. Once the wrapping process begins, your life is effectively over. After wrapping around you completely, The Water Hydra will then push ~100 spikes into your body, which pierce all over you. This process is rather slow, and causes immense pain to the wanderer. Once done, The Water Hydra will begin ingesting the wanderer, which takes around 1 hour.

Aquawinks

Aquawinks are a mutated form of Periwinkles which are adapted to living underwater. Similar to Periwinkles, they are rather delectable, and are tasty to eat. They are considered a delicacy among Merman, and are numerous in size. Aquawinks themselves are periwinkle puffskiens with evolved legs made for swimming. It is assumed they have non-visible lungs.

Merman

Merman are a highly intelligent Entity similar to humans. They are similar to the stereotypical mermaids, however, they are all male. They have a highly evolved democracy, as well as an economy. They also have an entire society set up amongst themselves, with no leader present. Each one appears to have a job which they do. Merman tend to have low regards for humans, and see themselves as above humans. However, they show no violent tendencies unless attacked.

The Island

The Island is the smallest part of Level 628, and is the hardest spot to reach within Level 628. There are no Entities on The Island, however, there has been noted to be one person on The Island occasionally. It is impossible to have more than one person on The Island, as any attempts to lead people to simply to not want to go on The Island. Any attempts to force someone onto The Island whilst one person is on The Island have been met with extreme violence from the recipient, leading them to kill others or themselves to prevent going onto The Island. There are rumors about The Islands existence, however, as it stands, none of these are confirmed. It is currently thought that no outside sources can be seen from The Island, with only The Ocean being visible.

Theories of The Island

The Island Theories
...
Theory 1: Center of The Void

The Void is a largely devoid Entity, who once existed as the sole Entity on The Island. One day, The Ocean got infected with a great disease, leading to The Oceans life nearly dying off. However, The Void decided to provide a noble response to these actions, and selflessly absorbed the majority of this disease singlehandedly. The Void nearly died off due to this action, however, The Great Water Hydra gave up the majority of its size in order to save The Void. The Void then became The Island, and devoted its new existence to help other wanderers. The Great Water Hydra itself became The Water Hydra, and spends its time enacting harm to all, as a way of coping with the loss of its only friend to a disease it couldn't control.

This theory is supported by various drawings within The Sand Bunker.

Theory 2: A Lonely Girl

A Lonely Girl revolves around an unnamed Person of Interest. A Lonely Girl is suspected to have made The Island, as she is noted to be there often. She is also suspected to have made the sands of Level 628 periwinkle, though this is largely unconfirmed, and is speculation. Very little is known about A Lonely Girl's intent, as she shows no visible reasoning for visiting The Island. However, it is likely A Lonely Girl made The Island.

The identity of A Lonely Girl is unknown. An illustration is attached on file. She has been glimpsed.

Groups

The Desert

Groups of The Desert
...

The Blyers

The Blyers are a group of wanderers who reside in The Desert. They focus their efforts on capturing Bly's for cosmetic purposes. They reside near the center of The Desert, and spend their time largely focusing on helping others dye their skin. The process is noted to be none painful, and they are open to others. They take payment in the form of things such as Almond Water and food.

Wasters

This group spends their time within The Dunes, and serve as helpers out of The Dunes themselves. They are highly experienced in combat, and will be more than willing to transport you out of The Dunes. They have been seen standing near the edge of The Dunes, typically urging wanderers to not enter. Their leader, Ramirez, has been noted to have experienced major loss to The Dunes, and simply wants to help others avoid the same fate.

Aurors

Aurors are a highly inept group who focus their efforts on the capture and containment of Auroras. They have been devoted to this project to an extended period of time, as a way of sending others to level-18 when necessary. These efforts are highly dangerous, and these wanderers are no strangers to Level 18. Their numbers vary widely over time, as they oftentimes lose members to Auroras.

Periwinkle Collectors

These wanderers devote themselves completely to the capture of Periwinkles. They capture any Periwinkles within sight in order to feed others on Level 628, and to an extent, other Levels as well. There Periwinkle numbers are very high, and they have been noted to breed Periwinkles often. They experiment with Periwinkle cooking frequently, however, these have resulted somewhat bad on many occasions, with only a few notable dishes coming from Periwinkles.

Selza's Devotees

Selza's Devotees are a largely mysterious group who's intentions are unclear. They seem to be highly interested in Selzas Spire, and go to extremes to research it whenever possible. They have high interest in the mysterious language of the books within Selzas Spire, and have been noted to perform "rituals" for Selza to decipher these. They are highly dangerous, and should be avoided whenever possible. The majority of their group spends their time wandering The Sand Bunker in The Desert.

Bunkered Down

This group of wanderers can be seen wandering throughout The Sand Bunker. They seem to be researching The Sand Bunker in attempts to explore unexplored areas. They can be seen grouping up within The Bunker often, and have established a map of The Sand Bunker. This map fluctuates often, similar to The Sand Bunker itself. They are open to trading, and are friendly to others.

The Ocean

Groups of The Ocean
...

The Hydra's Killers

These people are incredibly devoted to The Island, and are currently unknown in size. They spend their time hunting The Water Hydra in attempt to reach The Island, and often lose numbers in this process. They enjoy spending their time on The Island, and sometimes die on The Island, typically due to starvation. Where their corpses are remains a mystery. Their most commonplace tactic of murder is strangling, though they have been noted to go towards desperate methods.

Atlantians

This group of wanderers spend nearly all their time in Atlantis, and have merged into the Merman's society. They have been noted to be friendly, yet similar to Merman, have been noted to think of themselves as higher than the average wanderer. Their size is estimated to be around 100 wanderers, fluctuating at a steady incline.

The Magicians

The Magicians are a rather small group, consisting of 10 members, who each devote themselves to performing "magic" within The Depths. This magic is most notably done by covering themselves with Microspeck's, then proceed to claim to be Wizards. They are largely harmless, and are rarely encountered.

Siren's Lyre

Siren's Lyre is a highly occult-like group who largely focus their efforts on sacrificing themselves to Sirens. Very little is known about this group, their numbers, or their intent. However, they should be regarded as highly dangerous, as they have been seen to capture other wanderers for the purpose of sacrificing them to Sirens. They regard the Siren as "their goddess" and will do anything to sacrifice to it, including sacrificing other members of their mysterious occult.

The Island

Due to the nature of The Island, there are no Groups on The Island.

Entrances and Exits

The Desert

Entrances and Exits to The Desert
...

Entrances

The Desert is the easiest section of this Level to enter. The most common method of entry is through dehydration. Whenever a wanderer is dehydrated, they will begin to see glimpses of The Desert around them. If they focus on these glimpses until they pass out, they will awaken in The Desert with their dehydration being sated. This entryway causes you to emerge near the middle of The Desert. The only other known way to enter The Desert is by swimming in from The Ocean, or else sailing in from The Ocean. You will emerge on the outskirts of The Desert.

Exits

The Desert has a few unique exits, each of which are somewhat difficult to find. Near the center of The Desert, you’ll find an abandoned shack. Should you enter this shack, you’ll find numerous desert related items for sale, such as Periwinkle shells. As it is unstaffed, all items are able to be taken, and the shop restocks itself when no one is present in the shop. Due to the shop having no useful items for wanderers, you are free to take as you please. Once you exit the shop, you will emerge in The Hub, and will be unable to return to the shop unless you end up back in the desert.

The second exit is much different. Near the barrier of The Ocean and The Desert, the occasional Periwinkle quicksand pile can be found. If you enter the periwinkle quicksand pile and sink in it completely, you will pass out, and then reawaken on Level 628. Periwinkle quicksand piles are rather rare, so this method should only be used in specific scenarios.

The final known exit can be found at random throughout The Desert. As stated above, being bitten by an Aurora can cause you to re-awaken on Level 18.

The Ocean

Entrances and Exits of The Ocean
...

Entrances

The most secure entryway into The Ocean is through Level 7. Whenever you enter Level 7, an occasional room can be found, which is notable for its periwinkle exterior wallpaper and periwinkle sand surrounding the rooms entryways. When you enter this room, you’ll end up in Atlantis. Once you exit this room, you’ll be near the bottom of The Ocean. Swimming up will cause you to emerge at the surface much faster than normal, and you’ll be near the center of The Ocean. From there, swimming in any direction will most commonly lead you to The Desert, however, it can lead you to The Island under specific circumstances.

The only other entryway to The Ocean is through Level 4. Occasionally, a bottle of Almond Water will be laying down in a periwinkle container. Drinking from this Almond Water bottle will result in you feeling much more refreshed than normal. While wandering, you will begin to see glimpses of water, which will fade after looking at them. If you wander with others, or stay still, you will stay on Level 4. However, if you follow these water like mirages, you will eventually end up in Atlantis. Exiting once more will cause you to emerge at the bottom of The Ocean. If you swim up, you’ll be picked up by The Wooden Boat from the bottom of The Ocean. The boat will take you to the surface, then gently lead you to The Desert.

Exits

Should you swim down anywhere other than The Vortex, you will find that there are numerous air gaps within the water. Should you enter these gaps of air within The Ocean, you will be able to breathe a moment, then reawaken on Level 7

The Island

Entrances and Exits of The Island
...

Entrances

The Island is the most difficult area to access, and has only three confirmed methods of entering. On levels featuring staircases going down, there has been noted to be an entryway revolving around Cici. If you should be Cici in a gamble with the stake of seeing true beauty, he will be able to show you a staircase. This staircase winds for a while, however, after a while, a step will break below you. You will fall for an invariable amount of time, until you eventually land softly onto The Island.

The only other known entryway is through The Water Hydra, specifically, "killing" The Water Hydra. Should you manage to "kill" The Water Hydra, its carcass will reanimate and take you to The Island, once arrived, it will slither away into The Depths.

Exits

The Island has numerous exits, all of which are easy to encounter. At any point, you can simply walk off The Island into The Ocean. Bear in mind that once you exit The Shallows, you will be incapable of turning around back to The Island, more commonly appearing at The Shallows near The Desert. Should you take The Presidents Boat off The Island, you will re-awaken on Level 777. The final exit is through sleeping, which has a random chance of transporting you to The Hub.
