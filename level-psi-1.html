Unnumbered Level - "Ψ"
Duelcon2025 » Unnumbered Level - "Ψ"

Maybe it's always been like this, wandering alone, caught between endless corridors that never say your name, but always know it. Maybe you can only ever be alone. Alone, with that ache twisting on itself, forever walking, forever forgetting, forever remembering that you never really left at all.

+ CONTENT WARNING:
- CONTENT WARNING:

Author Note:
This article contains themes of mental deterioration, dissociation, depression, trauma, self-hatred, and self-harm. Reader discretion is advised.


Ψ




I told them not to look at it.

I told them… over and over.

But they always want to see things for themselves. Typical. Always touching the hot stove and then crying about the burn.

They said Ψ was just a letter. Another classification, another symbol. They said I was overreacting, that I was "fixated." But they didn't feel it—the split between the eyes when you really see it. Not look. See. It vibrates, doesn't it? It crawls in sideways, like a whisper you swallowed too fast.

Ψ is the crack in the knowing. Ψ is the name for the things you forgot to unlearn. Ψ doesn't mean "mind." Ψ is the mind. Flayed.

I tried to explain it. I screamed it. But… the M.E.G. still called this a "phenomenon." Idiots. All of them. Filing away godhood like a medical report.

They don't get it.

I get it.

I've always gotten it.

And sometimes, you think the only cure is clarity.

And sometimes, the thoughts get too loud to whisper over.

And sometimes…













Ψ


"Level Psi"

...

And sometimes, you wander through hallways much too purple and much too bright—white rings etched into your vision, spaces where the world forgets to exist the way you remember it. The floor stops caring about gravity; you walk because that's the only logic left, lost somewhere between a sharp migraine and a slow leak of sanity. You twist, you curve, you bend, but there are no dead ends—a mercy, or a curse.

You hear screams in your periphery; a suggestion of self screamed until the echoes go hoarse. Each step lodges something deeper in the skull: a pulsing ache, impossible to explain, impossible to ignore. But what reason is there to stop? Pain neither means guilt nor absolution here. The walls don't listen, and neither do you—not anymore.

Maybe it's always been like this, wandering alone, caught between endless corridors that never say your name, but always know it. Maybe you can only ever be alone. Alone, with that ache twisting on itself, forever walking, forever forgetting, forever remembering that you never really left at all.

.


.



.






You forget how many steps it's been.

You forget how to count them.

You forget why you ever started.

And sometimes, the purple burns. Not just the color—you don't even like purple—but the brightness of it, the wrongness of it. Like looking directly into the thought of a scream. And it moves, doesn't it? No, it's the hallway. The hallway moves. It knows your spine curves when you're tired. It bends like you; you bend like it.

There's something breathing between the white lights. Or maybe it's just you, again, breathing too loud. Again. Too loud. Always too loud. Everything is loud now. Everything thinks in screaming. You can't tell if you're walking or falling, and the difference feels increasingly irrelevant.

There's no door.

There's never a door.

Only turns and twists. Folds like a paper brain.

You fold too easily.

My knees don't feel like mine anymore. They pulse.


One foot. Another. A third? You keep walking, even if you're not sure you still are. You forgot where your legs begin. You forgot if your legs belong to you anymore. The headache doesn't pound—that implies a rhythm. This is something else. More like the headache seeps, spreads, like ink through fabric. Almost like thoughts you didn't want, sinking into thoughts you didn't choose.

I think I left part of myself behind four turns ago, and I don't miss it.


The walls breathe with you. Or you breathe with them. It doesn't matter anyway. You keep moving. You don't know why. It's not hope nor fear; rather, compulsion. There's a difference.

You pause, or twitch. Hard to tell. Your fingers fumble for the Almond Water at your side. That's what it is. Lifeline, they all called it. You unscrew the cap with shaking hands and raise it as ritual. You don't remember where you found this bottle—maybe back at the Abandoned Office, maybe you stole it—but it was full then. It must still be full. You tell yourself that.

You tip it back.

Something touches your tongue. Or doesn't. You swallow anyway. You always swallow anyway.

It's supposed to help. It's supposed to stop the spinning, the purple, the pulsing. The parts of you that feel like they're thinking without you. You close your eyes and wait for it to kick in. For the silence. For the stillness.

…You forgot what it tastes like. You forgot if it ever had a taste. You forgot if you already drank it. Maybe you drank it yesterday. Maybe you drank it four turns ago. Maybe you just forgot how to feel it go down.

But it worked. It must've worked. That's why everything still hurts exactly the same.

And the lights above—too bright, too circular, like halos for a god that forgot how to die. They never flicker. That's the part that hurts. The consistency. The mockery of stability. The world doesn't strobe, doesn't warn you. It just… is. And maybe that's worse.

I miss blinking… I think.


You scrape your hand against the wall, just to feel. Yet, all it gives you is more purple; no texture or comfort, only color and reflection. And the migraine behind your eyes screams that you deserve it.

You try to stop walking, but your feet keep going.

You try to turn around, but you only loop.

You try to scream, but the hallway has already done so—louder, longer.

There's something inside me that doesn't want to be alone, and now it doesn't know when to shut up.


There's no up. There's no down. Just forward, purple, thought.

And thoughts rot here.

Just like you.

If I just let go a little more, maybe I can hear what I really think.


The migraine becomes memory, and the memory becomes someone else's. You don't remember the last time your mind was quiet… or if it ever was. Maybe it never worked right. Maybe the hallway's just showing you the truth.

I think I remember being made fun of… I think I remember laughing with them.

You only say sorry when you're caught.


Laughter's a hollow thing here; it echoes as a cough. You smile and your jaw aches. You cry and your chest doesn't rise. You start to forget how expressions work. You start to forget why they mattered.

No one's watching… except you.

And even you don't like what you see.


Why did I say that back then?

Why do I keep saying that?


The purple keeps curving. You keep hurting. That's the deal, isn't it? You walk. You hurt. You think. You suffer. Repeat until…

...

…until?

I was a mistake, but I tried to be a good one.

They were right not to pick you.

They were right not to stay.


You press your forehead to the wall. The wall presses back. Not with force—just presence. Like it knows where you are. Like it's always known. Like it's been waiting.

Do you remember the way the teacher looked at you when you stuttered? I do.

If you're the problem, then everything else makes sense.


The pain becomes familiar at this point. You name it. Not out loud, of course. Names are dangerous, and there are already too many things in here with power.

You didn't mean it like that, but that doesn't matter now.

You took something beautiful and made it bleed.


The ceiling lowers, or maybe you grow taller. No, you're shrinking, or the hallway's remembering you too fondly. That happens… sometimes. Doesn't it?

I wonder if I was ever real, or just a story someone else told themselves to cope, too.


You try to stop thinking. It doesn't work. The hallway loves when you think. It feeds on thinking. It twists thinking. Every new realization you have is another corridor. Every suppressed emotion becomes a bend.

I should've apologized sooner. I should've screamed louder.

You can't keep blaming the world.

You were always the common factor.


Everything starts to go soft. Not in comfort, however—in clarity, as if your brain is butter under a heatlamp. You can still feel it melt.

I hurt myself a little. You know—to feel something that didn't lie.

You were given chances.

And yet, you wasted them.


There's no air here. Not really. You breathe because your lungs remember how. That's all.

They said they loved me.

I said I believed them.

We both lied.

...

This is why they all left.


The hallways don't even echo anymore. You've been alone too long for sound to make sense. Maybe you never had a voice, or maybe you left it with the rest of yourself. But… maybe it's better that way.

I wish I hadn't said what I said, but I meant it.

Every time you say you'll try harder, someone else has to pay for it.


Your thoughts keep circling. You don't stop them. You let them rot in you. You let them curl into themselves like dying spiders.

You only get so many second chances…

And I used all of mine on people who left anyway.

You made it worse.


Your mouth is dry. You don't remember thirst. You don't remember much, actually. Just enough to stay dangerous. Just enough to keep hurting.

Every time I remember their voice, I get sick. I think that means I still care.

But you're not meant to be remembered…

You're meant to be endured.


The hallway curves again. This time it feels like guilt.

You hate guilt.

I didn't stop it.

I watched.

I let it happen.

And then I cried like the victim.

But you liked the pain.

You liked how it excused you.

Did you not?


There's a flash. Not of light. Of memory. Too fast to hold, but enough to burn.

They screamed my name.

I looked away.

I still don't know if they lived.

This is what you wanted, no?

This is what silence sounds like.


Everything gets quieter.

Which is worse…

Quiet makes space for thinking.

You're back in your skull again—just you.

And the rot.

And the purple.

Always the purple.

They said I changed, but I was always like this.

How could they see through the difference?


…They just stopped forgiving me for it.

You only exist because someone else tolerated you.

You talk too much.

You never say anything.


You wonder if you're walking anymore, or if you ever even were.

You wonder if you exist, or if this is the thought that finally snapped.

You wonder if you're dead.

You wonder if this is the consequence.

You wonder—

No.

No, don't.

Not again.

If I stop walking, maybe the guilt will catch up.

Maybe I'll finally deserve it.


And that's the worst part.

You need the hallway now.

It's the only thing that bends just enough to fit perfectly around you.

You don't want to leave.

You don't deserve to.

They told me to seek help.

I laughed.

I didn't know how to cry yet.

Stop pretending.

You know you like the attention pain gives you.

You begged for this.


You keep walking.

The lights burn into your vision.

The purple stretches into forever.

And for a moment—

Just… for a moment—

You think you've reached the end.

But… there's no end.

Just another turn.

Another thought you didn't want.

Another version of you that lied better.

I think I've passed this spot before…

I was worse then…


You blink—maybe.

You breathe—barely

You exist—unfortunately.

And the hallway still curves, like it's laughing.

I want to stop walking…

What's there left to do?


You reach out to steady yourself.

The wall is warm.

It shouldn't be.

It beats.

A heart?

Like yours?

I think it's learning from me…


You let your hand fall.

It follows.

You don't look back.

You know what's there.

You've always known.

And it never stops thinking.

Just like you.

Please don't leave me here forever…
