Lucy Chabar

⚠️Warning⚠️

This article is included in a canon in preparation and it will be published soon.


rating: +3+–x

Full name: Lucy Chabar

Nicknames: Top Leader, Supreme Leader, The Machine, Chokbar1

Known affiliations: Nomads, Ariane Circle

Last known location: on Level 4-FR

"Photography of Lucy Chabar on a mission with a community in migration to Level 4-FR"

Lucy Chabar is a 29-year-old2 Caucasian woman measuring 1.79m, with blonde hair of medium length and green eyes. She is also in good shape. When she goes on a mission, she is always dressed in a tactical outfit or a trellis wrapped in a sort of thick brown leather coat, with a bag on the back. The rest of the time, she can be seen dressed in simple t-shirts and pants.

Chabar is the leader of the Troop Dream-Hunter, which itself is the most versatile troop and has the most experienced members of the Nomads. This status makes her wield both great responsibilities and grants her many distinctions, which have made her known throughout the Backrooms. Former private secretary of a business director known to be 'boring and frustrated by his profession', she integrated into the M.E.G.. Then, by climbing the hierarchy thanks to her growing initiative, she ended up founding the Nomads with 8 other members in order to no longer be limited by the regulations and the repetitive missions of the M.E.G. This first group, named Ariane Thread (wich still exists today), aimed to lead large-scale expeditions to explore and map out places far from inhabited levels in order to make them safe as well as to find new passages to other levels. The security of the group was then provided by Toreen Stoël, who was trained in the handling of firearms and taught it to other members, including Chabar.

At the founding of this independent group, the first months of its existence can be summed up as exploring and surviving as much as possible. It then made itself quite noticed, by the M.E.G. on one hand, including the top of the hierarchy, who authorized their independence3, but also of another French-speaking group, which saw in them an opportunity for collaboration, the Ariane Circle. Thanks to the growing will of Lucy Chabar, they gradually transformed their simple mapping mission into a utopian dream of prosperity within the Backrooms. The Nomads then soon took contact with other groups, now in their own name, in order to provide for their needs in exchange for service. It is thanks to these newly formed relationships that the Nomads were able to grow in number and importance, recovering members from the different groups and residents of the Backrooms.

All of this was made possible by the bravery of Chabar, who already had a strong personality in the Frontrooms, making her workplace stick together.4


Despite her leadership personality, she remains smiling and pleasant with the other wanderers, pulling the others forward with her. It was notably this trait that made it possible to consolidate the group so quickly. Her social ease is largely due to her job which was about being around and reaching out to people. It has been about 4 years since she left the M.E.G., and thanks to her efforts combined, with those of other leaders and founding members, the Nomads were set up.

Nevertheless, her lithe silhouette does not hide her natural charisma, bolstered by her stare, which is qualified as piercing and intimidating, as well as her physique, previously maintained by her gym routine in the Frontrooms and then her repeated missions in the Backrooms. In addition, apart from harmless conversations, she can be authoritarian and stubborn, and will not hesitate to stand up to anyone who would not agree with her on important subjects. Being often relevant, this characteristic has also made her reputation and the respect that most people show her. Her personality is also the source of most of her nicknames.

Interview with Lucy Chabar for the Ariane Circle register
Close

Start of the recording: 12/03/2021, 14:00.
Interviewer: Archivist Ninon Diner
Interviewed: Top Leader Lucy Chabar

The Ac Dinner starts the recording and checks that the equipment works before sitting on a chair in front of Leader Chabar, a notebook in hand.

Dinner: Well, we can start. You kind of already know what I'm going to ask you for this interview?

Chabar: Smiling Essentially. I'll introduce myself; Lucy Chabar, 29, founding member of the Ariane Thread and now part of the Dreams Hunter of which I am currently "at the top of". She mimics quotes with her fingers, formerly a member of M.E.G as a course cartographer and, even more formerly, a private secretary in an electronics sales company, murmur, I do not recommend working there.

Dinner: Very good! Straight to the point, embarrassed laugh, why did you leave the M.E.G.?

Chabar: We decided to take our lives in our hands, a third time, after being limited when we were at the M.E.G. I mean, there are several reasons that justify it, but it is mainly because we were limited in our actions.

Dinner: What do you mean?

Chabar: I mean… First off, after arriving at the M.E.G., we were bored, despite the fact that we were from the "upper crust", Mimes once again quotes with her fingers. Most of us only did repetitive tasks similar to an average job at home.5 The landscape was interesting, but the missions themselves were spaced, with lonnnng pauses in between where we stayed at The City and without the right to move in order to minimize the risks.

Dinner: Then, how did they let you go?

Chabar: Hmhmmm, I mean, after a while, our team was starting to be talked about, then I was promoted to its top, and, from there, I started to negotiate with the upper ones. I had permission to make more varied missions, more often and with more resources. After a while, I even received the greenlight to set up my own expeditions on the condition that they would be useful to the group.

Dinner: Which is normal.

Chabar: Which is normal, yes, but after a while it kept me back. We wanted to continue these missions, but without having to report to the same people, with the others, we wanted to provide to more people, and clearly the M.E.G. was not delighted. So, one day, we had a public meeting with the others, and we asked them for our independence, explaining that it would be a good thing, how we we going to do it etc., etc.. Surprisingly, they didn't complain that much… Well, anyway, we told them that we would leave in all cases, so either it was in the cold or it was on good terms.

Dinner: And they just agreed to let go of a competent team without asking for anything in return?

Chabar: Hm no, we obviously told them that we would provide support to the communities derived from the M.E.G. and that we will continue to carry out some missions on orders. Also, we have implemented "commercial" exchanges to the point that we have become partners today. Our number largely permits it, and it allows us to keep very good relations with them, even if our privileged partner, is you, of course.

Dinner: laughs gently Yes, it's true. Clears her throat and today?

Chabar: Today, hm, we keep it up, we concretize our initial vision, secure the Backrooms, and we do well. I think we will soon be discussing perhaps enlarging the size of the newbies' troop; we have motivated volunteers. Otherwise, we continue exploration, we often make a joint walk with your teams, more and more often, and it is quite pleasant. Then, if it continues like that, we will continue to enlarge the ranks and see what we can do. Perhaps, founding our own base in the long term if we are numerous enough; and they won't want to. Laughs audibly

Dinner: Smiling That's not wrong. Well, it should be enough. Everything is noted, everything is recorded; I'll send it this afternoon. I will get back to you if there is a need for new information. It was great thank you very much, I won't hold you any longer.

Chabar: Our troop is resting at the QG today; we can continue to chat after your workday if you want. You can take your notebook.

Dinner: I- uh, where, uh why not but where could we talk?

Chabar: I saw that they had installed a snack bar on floor 22; we could have a drink.

Dinner: turns red Uhh, yes… yes, okay, yes let's do that. Okay, well I cut the recording.


Notable achievements made by Lucy Chabar and/or her troop:

  • Has secured Level 11 within a radius of 10 kilometers around Base Beta.
  • Has served as a mediator between the M.E.G. and the AC to appease relations, in particular thanks to the establishment of commercial routes for which they themselves ensured deliveries initially.
  • Has allowed a community of 60 people to migrate to Level 4 from Level 2. Unfortunately, this operation has cost the lives of 11 members of the Dreams-Hunter.6
  • Has such an impressive physique for an ex-secretary. This note is not relevant Dinner … -Michaël.k
  • Has fitted out a safe road connecting Levels 4-FR, 14-FR, 1, 4, and 522 to facilitate the exchanges of goods and civil movements.
