DESCRIPTION

An image of Object 82

Object 82, commonly known as Sage Crystals, are crystals that bear a strong resemblance to quartz in both appearance and physical properties. The object has only been found at former ritual sites of the Church of the Veiled1. Sage Crystals, similarly to quartz, although brittle, are fairly hard, clocking in at a 7 on Mohs hardness scale, which is higher than jade and lower than topaz. They are also incredibly heat resistant, with a melting point of 1670C2, and are poor at conducting electricity.

One major difference that set Sage Crystals apart from quartz is their considerably lower mass. For scale, quartz has a mass of 265 g/m compared to Sage Crystals with a mass of 140 g/m. Another unique property of the object is its unusually high resistance towards corrosion, similar to Iridium. These differences are seemingly inexplicable considering the identical molecular structures of quartz and Sage Crystals.

Sage Crystals are commonly used in jewelry, being sold by vendors as good luck charms in the form of rings or necklaces. They are also advertised as being capable of curing diseases or reviving the dead, but these claims remain entirely unproven. Due to their association with the Church of the Veiled, the crystals could also have been bewitched; reports of the disappearance of individuals who were in possession of the crystals have piled up, but the causation is entirely speculative. Regardless, caution is advised.

Due to their similar properties to quartz, Sage Crystals are also used by wanderers in a similar manner as quartz. These include glassmaking, casting metals, creating refractory bricks, and sharpening tools.

Out of all the major groups of interest, the Backrooms Robotics uses Sage crystals the most, using them mainly in the production of semiconductor chips3. There have also been several unconfirmed projects regarding the construction of drones that can withstand extreme conditions, which intend on using the crystals for certain vital components due to their resistance towards corrosion as well as their low mass.

Notably, the Lost have also found their own uses for the crystals; some members of the Lost Legion and the Lost Suns use the material to craft tools or cut certain gems, while other members of the group tend to use it for worship or decoration. Either way, most members of the group are very receptive towards wanderers that carry them. This also applies to normal quartz as well. The reverence the Lost has towards the crystals are thought to be due to their quartz-like properties, and not because of their anomalous properties as they possess no knowledge on the crystals' composition.

It is widely speculated that Sage Crystals are creations of the Veiled, given the fact that they appear exclusively at former Church ritual sites. However, these theories struggle to create a motive as to why the Church would bother with creating these crystals.[SEE CREATION SECTION FOR MORE INFO].

PROJECT ROSETTA

DO NOT SHARE OR REPRODUCE THIS DOCUMENT WITHOUT EXPRESSED PERMISSION FROM THE ETERNAL REPOSITORY

Project Rosetta is a project headed by Honorius of the Eternal Repository. The project's aim is to collect all texts from the Church of the Veiled, particularly those from the Third Grand Parish in Level 8. The knowledge from these scrolls will grant us valuable information that will leave the Veiled vulnerable to attacks.

However, several of the texts proved illegible despite confirmation that the Veiled only spoke English and Latin. Initially, these texts were thought to written in another language. Upon further inspection, however, these texts were discovered to have been enchanted, preventing non-Veiled members from reading it.

Thus, the goal of Project Rosetta has shifted towards accessing to a Veiled prisoner. During the war with the Veiled, knowledge of a particular spell that Veiled priests use to force prisoners to answer questions truthfully was successfully gained through undisclosed means. If the Repository got access to a Veiled prisoner, the illegible texts could be translated accurately without fear of misinformation.

While the Repository did not capture any Veiled prisoners due to not participating in the conflict against the Veiled, other groups, like the M.E.G., and Eyes of Argos did capture prisoners. Plans were initially made to trade with the EoA for one of them. However, they immediately attempted to kill the envoy upon hearing of the Repository's interrogation tactics. Thankfully, no casualties occurred as a peace treaty was later brokered and signed, by Alasdair Morgan preventing conflict from occurring.

After the failure with the Eyes of Argos, another offer was made, this time to the M.E.G. However, similarly to the EoA, they turned down on the offer upon hearing about the interrogation tactics4.

Fortunately, these groups weren't the only ones that held Veiled prisoners, as the Ariane Circle also contained a Veiled prisoner5. However, they did not accept the offer without compromises, as they refused to give custodianship of the prisoner to the Repository. Instead, the Circle would allow the Repository access to the prisoner in exchange for Teams Apollo and Thanatos taking part in Project Rosetta.

While much speculation has been given to the object regarding its potential to be cursed, there is still no solid indication that these crystals are cursed. Regardless, it is still cautioned against wearing or touching them especially in the wild or from unidentified street vendors.

DESCRIPTION

Frvyo Quartz is a sub-type of Sage Crystals. The object is identical to its generic counterpart aside from its ability to interact with Frvyo8. This is odd given that quartz is unable to interact with Frvyo safely.

The distinguishing feature of this object is similar to that of Frvyo Jade; while Frvyo Jades illuminate when an entity is detected, Frvyo Quartz release flashes of white light along with a small amount of heat at varying frequencies depending on the entity's danger or threat level9. Known frequencies are listed below in descending order:

Frequency of flashes Danger of Entity Advice
4 flashes per second High Danger/Extremely Hostile Avoid this threat at all costs
2 flashes per second Moderately High Danger/Hostile Highly encouraged to avoid the threat
1 flash per second Moderate Danger/Minimally Hostile Avoid this threat if possible
1 flash for every 2 seconds Moderately Low Danger/Unsafe Threat is unsafe but approachable
1 flash for every 4 seconds Low Danger/Safe Threat can be safely approached


OVERHEATING


A picture of overheated Frvyo Quartz

If left activated for too long10, Frvyo Quartz will overheat. An overheated crystal is blood-red in color. If left in this state for an hour, it will explode, sending hundreds of shards flying though the air at speeds enough to pierce through the human body. Not only that, a large amount of the neurotoxin11 Frvyo gas is also released, causing everyone in the vicinity to be poisoned through inhalation. It is thus highly advised to not bring Frvyo Quartz to levels with high entity count.



RELIABILITY

Frvyo Quartz occupies a similar niche in exploration to Frvyo Jades. They are a lifesaver in isolated environments and regions thick with cover such as heavily forested or overgrown areas as well as places where the entity population is low. While in high entity population areas, such as metropolitan cities and entity nests, the crystal is very unreliable. Frvyo Quartz is also rather risky to use for long-term exploration. They are also a risk in levels where illumination is low as the brightness of the crystal can be detected easily, especially by light sensitive entities like Deathmoths and Smilers.

The discovery of Frvyo Quartz created a lot of speculation regarding the connection between the Church of the Veiled and Frvyo. Multiple theories have been put out that the Veiled could produce the anomalous compound. However, these theories still lack concrete evidence.
