Alone

SLAM

"WAKE UP, DAMNIT!" Your boss yells.

Crumbs fly. One clings to your lapel, another buries itself in the wrinkle of your shirt, like it’s trying to nest. Half-asleep, you watch them fall like snow. One settles in the crease of your palm, crushed by your finger as it twitches to life.

You lift your head slowly. Time passes hazily, as if you're drowning the ocean. You’re still at your desk. You’re still here. Of course you are.

Joints are stiff like rusted hinges. Your mouth is half open, breath stale. You don't even remember having fallen asleep, only staring at your screen. Level reports. Again. And again. And again. There’s no end to the monotony. Just different names for the same emptiness.

"You’ve worked here for how many months? And your desk is still this dirty?" your boss mutters, not even bothering to sound angry anymore. "Get back to the reports and get some coffee or something."

You suppose you’re no longer worth the breath. He walks off, already scanning for someone else to bark at.

You push with your meek arms, barely able to stay awake as you stumble to the newly developed coffee room. You boil the almond water and pour in one of the packets. It smells like burnt almonds and plastic. You take out a small pill bottle with anxiety medication inside. You've had to start using them more sparingly since you wound up here, not that they truly help.

Popping the pill in, you take slow sips, mumbling about the work you need to do.

Tap

"Handsome!" It doesn't take you longer than a moment to recognize the voice. Finn. He always surprised you, but you kind of appreciated that. A bit of a chatterbox compared to your other peers, grinning, shifting on his heels, eyes wide with that ridiculous, stubborn optimism.

Finn keeps talking. Something cheerful. You were too spaced out to tell what he was really rambling on about. He’s waving his hands like always, filling the sterile space with his noise, his warmth. You manage something of a smile. A flicker of movement at the corners of your mouth.

He beams at it like it’s a gift.

You always respected that about him. His ridiculousness. His joy. His hope. It made the place feel less like a prison. Less like a grave. He never seemed touched by the rot that seeped into everyone else. Maybe he made it feel like some form of twisted home.

As he finishes his ramblings, he makes it a point to tell his latest assignment, "Oh, and before I forget, I have some great news! I think." He grins. "We're going to get some lab samples for one of the researchers in Level 282! It's got to be better than rotting in this place, right?"

You nod; your coffee's gone cold, yet somehow you feel warm.

"Yup! We'll be off in a few hours actually, so you should probably pack, huh? Well, either way, see ya there; it's a date!"

He giggles happily as he walks out of the room.

You go to your work station, piled with research work to fill out for later. You pull out the desk drawers, grabbing the few essential supplies you have to take with you.

You reach for a pen, but it stalls midway, fingers frozen in place. Something in your chest caves in, soft and slow, like wet paper. You glance toward the drawer. Still locked.

You never open it. Not anymore. Not unless something inside you cracks just enough to bleed through the seams. Maybe it’s the fatigue today. Maybe it’s the cold. Maybe it’s the quiet that presses too tightly on your ribs. You reach for the key. The drawer creaks open like a wound unsealing.

A stack of old field notes. Weathered, curling at the edges. On top, one page. His name scrawled in thick, fading marker: Elias. Everything else on the page is violently crossed out. Not edited. Erased. Slashed in the way you only do when you're trying to carve someone out of yourself.

You stare at it, just long enough to feel heat swell behind your eyes.

You were just a trainee back then. Green, eager, stupid. He was your mentor and someone you looked up to. Elias had promised to show you the ropes. He promised to stay. He’d made it feel like a kindness, like someone finally saw you, not as a burden or background noise, but as someone worth holding on to. It only took two weeks for him to break it. You take a look at the back again; something he'd written and gave to you on his last mission. It never gets any easier to read.

"Don’t wait up. If I don't come back, assume I did it on purpose."

The paper stares back. You don’t know if it was a warning. Or a mercy. Or just another excuse to leave without looking back. Like every person who’s ever gotten close enough to touch you. Like everyone who made you believe, even for a second, that you were worth the trouble of staying.

Mid thought, your boss strolls on over, handling a backpack precariously. "Don't fuck this up."

He escorts you to the noclipping point. You can hear him muttering under his breath behind you, some kind of sarcastic quip you can barely hear. "Why is it always the useless ones who come back…"

You stare at the entry point for a moment. Would anyone notice if you didn't come back like the others? Would you be replaced? Would people…forget? Would Finn forget? Eventually…Probably. Horror stories go far in the M.E.G. You don't have time to ponder as Finn approaches.

"You ready for our first ever real mission?" he says, bouncing in place, his voice high-pitched and full of something you can barely recognize anymore. Excitement? Life?

"Just us, alone and the rooms," he continues, grinning. "Or, submarine cave. Huh. Anyway, you reaaady?" He giggles at his own words, looking up into your eyes as if expecting excitement.

You try to answer, but your mouth doesn’t cooperate at first. Your throat feels dry and hollow, scratchy almost. You nod, eventually. It's not a lie. It's not the truth either.

Finn just smiles wider and pats your shoulder; you nod again, walking through the noclip point together.

The nice breeze of the levels air brushes against your face, a stark difference from the melancholy damp office space you're used to. Finn's clearly enjoying himself, laying down on the soft blades of grass. He stares into the sky for a moment before beginning to speak, "You know, now that we're alone. I just wanted to say… I really care about you."

The last time someone said that, they vanished into the fog of a forgotten level, their last note filled with cold contempt.

You're quiet for a moment, unsure really what to say. Unsure if you should speak at all. He continues, "I honestly didn’t even accept this mission at first," he says, fiddling with a loose thread on his sleeve. "But then I heard you were going… and I guess that kind of decided it for me.

He glances down, voice quieting. "I hope that doesn't sound weird, I'm…not the best with words you know."

You blink slowly. It takes a second to register what he’s saying. Another second to realize he means it. Part of you wants truly trust what he's saying, but it gets strangled by the louder voice, the one shaped by years of leaving, of silence, of cold shoulders.

So you say nothing. Because saying anything that didn't crush him would mean believing, and belief is all you really have left.

Finn continues looking at you. Still smiling giddily.

And all you can think is, please don’t leave. But the words stay buried. Where it’s safer.

Cough

You loudly cough, switching the subject to the mission at hand. He frowns a bit, taking a second of silence before getting up as his usual happy self. "Oh yeah," he says. He lightly chuckles, almost forcibly, "Guess we were actually here for something, huh?" His smile rebounds, "So we’re doing basic recon for researchers. There’ve been some weird reports about the Siren on this level. Like… no one’s seeing her anymore. She's just… gone. We’re here to confirm it for them, basically."

It sounds simple. Easy. But nothing ever is. You ponder for a moment, spaced out of everything else. What if she’s just learned how to kill quietly? What if no one sees her anymore because she doesn’t leave anything behind? You're not sure.

The thought doesn't scare you. Not really. It just settles in your chest like everything else: heavy and inevitable.

Finn laughs, cutting through your spiraling. "I think this will be pretty chill, and here's hoping she's dead!" His tone is playful, like he’s already solved the mystery. Already found her.

You two head towards the cave, handling a lighting bug from the level softly to serve as light in the dark. The soft waves crash against stone somewhere nearby, rhythmic and slow. It might be comforting, in another life. But here, it’s just background noise for thoughts you can’t stop having.

Finn talks as you walk. About missions. About how excited he is. About you. You don’t answer. You let him fill the space so you don’t have to.

THWACK

Finn faceplants onto the hard concrete floor, tripping over the entrance of one of the large, rusted submarines. The safehavens of the levels. You instinctively flinch for a moment; one that seemingly lasts forever while your brain assumes the worst.

He's alright. He always is.

When ready, he leaps up off the floor; if the lights were better, you’d probably see stars in his pupils. "Let’s go in here!" he says, eyes locked on the hulking rusted submarine half-sunken in the cavern wall. "I mean, what else are we gonna do? Plussss, if the Siren shows up, we’ll be safe in here, right?"

He doesn’t wait for a response. Just grabs your hand and pulls you forward. His skin is warm. Soft even. Inside, he gasps in awe at everything, like a kid at toy store for the first time. He talks with his whole body, pointing and gesturing and bouncing up and down.

"I’m gonna check the rest out," he says suddenly. "You stay here. Guard the entrance."

Before you can respond, before you can ask him not to leave, he's already off into the dimly-lit corridors; his voice echoes behind him like the ghost of joy.

"THIS STUFF LOOKS REALLY OLD, YOU KNOW, IT’S A WONDE—"

Silence.

Your heart drops. Your heart fills the void of silence. One beat, then two, then three.

You tear through the submarine’s narrow corridors, the dim submarine light's glow barely cutting through the dark. Your thoughts race, swelling your head over and over. Did he noclip? Did he fall through? Is he dead? Did he leave you?

The thought chokes you. Not again. Not again.

You call his name, but it comes out thin and hollow, like your voice is already too far away to matter. Your breathing turns ragged. The world around you narrows. The walls feel tighter. You check room after room after room. Nothing. Just rot and ruin.

Your legs start to shake. Your chest seizes.

He left. He left just like the rest of them. Just like Elias. One minute by your side, the next, vapor in the air. No goodbye, no body. Just the long, slow learning that they’re never coming back.


You stumble into the final chamber, your pulse like a war drum in your throat.

Empty.

Tap

You jolt back, body locking up as your hands fly to your face. What's going on? Another tap. What the hell is happening?

Your breath catches in your throat as you back away, fists clenched, pulse screaming.

The room is still empty.

But something is here.

Something you can’t see.

A soft, warm sensation fills your hand. A human touch. Is it him? He's here, yet, not here somehow.

You speak, voice cracking as you reach out

"Please," you whisper, voice splintering around the word. "Just… tap me on the shoulder if you're here."

Tap

It is him. Somehow. Someway. It's him. Tears stream down you eyes,the terms of your situation being revealed to you. You gasp, as tears continue running down your cheeks. A soft warm sensation fills your body. A hug.

Your breathing slows down; you can't feel this way. You're not supposed to. You try to stop the tears, only holding them back as you step away. Your voice is crackly as you speak.

"Could we… just hold hands? Just for a little while." You pause for a moment.

"I… I know it’s stupid, I just"

You feel warm fingers interlock with yours. You shakily breath in and out, trying to hold back mountains of tears.

If you can only feel each other, it's the only way. You're unsure if he can hear or see you, but you know you can't see him. You can't listen to his over-joyous tone.

You slowly walk out of the submarine together. Into the dark. You ramble a bit; tears being filled with soft, perhaps forced smiles on your end. "Sometimes I think everyone hates me… Even when they smile, I can’t tell if it’s real or if they’re just… waiting to leave. Maybe they do. Maybe I just…make it too hard to stay," you frown softly before continuing. "So I thought… if I just shut up, you know? Be quiet. Be easy. They won't go." You look down, going silent once more.

Despite Finn's absence of breath, of steps, of sound, you feel closer somehow. Maybe this is all you needed. Maybe this is what you always needed. Not words. Not a face. Just someone. Someone who won’t let go. Someone who stays.

Your hand tightens around his. Or, you think it’s his. It’s warm. It squeezes back.

A thought drifts in like rot in water: Maybe this is all you get. Not love. Not peace. Just a phantom in the dark to keep you from drowning. And it’s still more than you ever thought you’d have.

You want to speak to him, to say something. What would you even say? You open your mouth, uttering the only words that come to mind. Not because you want to. But because you need to. "Don't leave…Please."

He’s not leaving. Not yet. But you know how this ends.

The lightning bug glows faintly ahead of you, casting long, shifting shadows on the walls. You walk slowly, as if breaking the silence might shatter everything.

Hummm

The sound creeps in low and wet, something vibrating through every muscle of your body. The air thickens, like you’re breathing through cloth. You stop walking. So does the warmth at your side.

You’d almost forgotten. The mission. The Siren. The reason you came here.

A voice whispers, seemingly everywhere at once. A voice. Gentle. Floating. Coming from nowhere and everywhere at once. "Hello? …Is someone there?"

Your lungs tighten. No. No no no. You don’t move. You don’t breathe. Your heart hammers loud enough to betray you. If you speak…No, if you even blink, you're dead. You know this. You feel this.

You squeeze Finn’s hand tighter. And for just a second, just a breath of time, you almost believe you're not alone.

But there’s no pressure against your palm. No returning squeeze. Just fingers. Soft. Cooling. Just skin, limp as memory. You whisper, "…Finn? Are…Are you there? I-I'm sorry."

Nothing answers. You tug once. Then again. Harder. Tighter than you held Elias's coat the day he noclipped and never came back. Tighter than you held your own breath the first time you were left behind by all your lonesome by your parents.

Because this…He was the only thing in this world that hadn’t let go of you.

It doesn’t squeeze back. It doesn’t move. It just hangs there in yours doing nothing.

He’s just still…He's gotta be. Maybe he’s scared too. Maybe if you stay calm, he’ll laugh and hold tightly. Maybe if you whisper his name again, softer this time, he’ll answer.

"Finn?" It's barely a sound. Barely a plea.

Nothing. It wraps around your throat like a hand that presses gently seeing how long you can last.

You pull at his arm. It slips. You tug harder. His fingers slide from yours like everything else ever has.

There's no pulse.

You open your mouth to scream, but the sound catches somewhere between your teeth.

Tears don’t fall all at once. They crawl, tracing your face. Familiar paths carved from every inevitable goodbye you’ve ever swallowed.

"Please," you whisper, voice trembling like a wire stretched too thin. "Please don’t leave me. I can’t… I can’t lose you too."

But you already have.

You can’t move. You can't breathe. He's gone. And you didn't even get a chance to tell him you cared. You didn’t see him. You didn’t get to hold him. You only had a hand. And now, you don’t even have that.

It’s always the same. They leave. And you’re still here. You sit down, legs folding beneath you.

The hum returns. It’s closer now.

The Siren.

You don't run. You don't hide. You don't even blink. Let her come. Let her take what’s left. Let her find a person made entirely of silence and regrets.

For the first time in your life, you finally understand: You were never waiting to be saved or loved. You were just waiting to disappear.

And now, at last…you will.
