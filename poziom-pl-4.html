Level PL-4 - "Marble Maze"
Translations Hub » Polish Hub » Level PL-4 - "Marble Maze"

{$title}Languages:
















rating: +12+–x

Level PL-4 is the fourth Polish level of the Backrooms. It was discovered on April 13th, 1970.

Description:

Photo of Level PL-4.

Level PL-4 is a system of interconnected corridors and rooms made of concrete, varying in size. Most of the floors and walls are covered in identical tiles made of white marble, with a surface area of 20 cm². Most of the objects found on this level, similar to the walls and floors, show signs of damage. The exact causes of this remain unknown. The areas of Level PL-4 share a total surface area of 600,000 km².

The Bathrooms are a complex of several dozen adjacent rooms, covering an area of nearly 1 km². In them, one can find 93 toilets, 188 sinks, and 27 showers booths. Some sinks have running water, but most often it is cold. The same applies to the showers. None of the discovered toilets have a functioning flush, despite the water in the toilet bowl. In the Bathrooms, one can often encounter other wanderers, as they are one of the few landmarks in the area of the level. The Wells are rooms measuring 12 m² that occur in some places in Level PL-4. In the center of these rooms stand circular concrete wells covered with marble tiles, with a diameter of 2 meters. The individual wells vary in depth and the amount of water stored. In each Well room, there is a metal bucket and a rope corresponding to the well's depth. The water here is always clean and suitable for consumption, despite the pervasive dirt. This leads to these types of rooms being often sought out by wanderers. Unfortunately, they cannot be treated as an inexhaustible source of water, as the supply in the Wells is limited.

Photo of the Bathrooms.

So far, no entities have been confirmed to exist in Level PL-4. However, from time to time, various travelers report stories to the Committee's employees, speaking of a constant feeling of being watched, movements of shadows noticed out of the corner of the eye, or difficult-to-describe sounds coming from an unknown source.1 Many also report experiencing debilitating headaches, frequent sensory disorientation, and an overall sense of dislocation, further amplified by irrational fear.

Bases, Outposts and Communities:

11th. Outpost of the Polish Discovery Committee

  • It is located in a room 300 meters from the exit to Level PL-3, next to one of the Wells.
  • Between 2 to 5 Committee employees are stationed there.
  • Its tasks include providing protection to wanderers arriving from Level PL-3 and communication with the 23 th. Outpost.

4th. Outpost of the Polish Discovery Committee

  • It is located near the Baths, between two of the Wells.
  • Between 10 to 20 Committee employees are stationed there.
  • Its main tasks include further exploration of Level PL-4, assisting wanderers, and managing the water supplies remaining in one of the wells.
  • It is one of the first outposts of the Committee in the Backrooms.
  • Its operation was resumed in 1990, after a nearly 20-year break.2

Entrances and Exits

Entrances

  • Upon entering one of the caves on Level PL-2, you will be sent here.
  • You can also access this level by no-clipping.

Exits

  • If you fall asleep in this level, there is a chance you will wake up at Level PL-7.
  • Going through the wooden hatch in the floor of one of the rooms will take you to Level PL-3.
  • No-clipping through some of the walls will transport you to Level PL-5.
  • Entering one of the booths will take you to the showers on Level PL-9.

Additional Documentation

The recording below was found in the archives of the 4th Outpost after its operation was resumed in April 1990. It is suspected that it was authored by one of the employees of the Polish-Soviet Interdimensional Cooperation Committee, the parent organization of the current Committee. According to official reports, the 4th Outpost was closed in mid-1972 after its team was found dead. After examining the wounds inflicted on the explorers, a single gunshot to the head at close range was determined to be the cause of death for all of them. One more body carring the same injuries, was found several hundred meters from the Outpost at the edge of the Bathrooms.

+ Acquired Recording
- Acquired Recording

[Begin Recording]

Ahem, my name is Jerzy Madej; I am a senior explorer, currently serving as the manager of, 4th Outpost. I didn’t know how to start this recording, this… confession, so I thought I’d introduce myself. Although, it doesn’t really matter anymore. The Fourth will probably be shut down soon. However, before that happens, I would like to convey something.

They sent us here in January '72. None of us really knew what we were getting into. To be honest, I don't know what we expected. A sunny meadow full of flowers and nice animals? Nonsense. At least they told us they were sending us beyond reality. So, we could have any suspicions about what awaited us.

Well, no matter what we were expecting, it definitely wasn't a labyrinth of marble corridors and bathrooms covering millions of square kilometers. The first few days weren't easy. We had the equipment, we had a mission, but we lacked confidence and motivation. Many wanted to go back. Sometimes I thought about it myself, heh. However, I didn't want to abandon the mission. I was young and naïve. I believed we would succeed.

What Goździk did could have ended everything. And, for God’s sake, I regret that it didn’t happen. Of course, we informed the higher-ups, but they only offered their condolences and a lot of unfulfilled promises, after which they ordered us to continue the mission. Some of the team were shaken; after all, we all saw how far one of us went in an act of desperation. Some were afraid they would be next. I must admit that my mental state wasn’t good at that time either. I blamed myself for Goździk’s death, and perhaps I was right.

Later, that difficult period somehow… passed. It was still hard, but at least no one else shot half their head off, right? A few months had passed since we got to this level. It wasn't bad, it was… even good. We were managing. We had regular supply deliveries, and it was clear that the Committee was growing. We were exploring larger parts of this labyrinth. It was stable, until it wasn't.

It all began, I believe, in August '72. Wierzyński's team reported a strange structure found… just a few hundred meters from the Four. They were babbling something about Bathrooms, dozens of sinks lined up next to each other. Two years of work on this Level should have prepared me for anything. Still, when Wierzyński first took me to those messed-up Bathrooms… It just seemed unnatural to me, regardles of the fact that I was in an endless marble labyrinth beyond reality.

For the next few weeks, Wierzyński and Michnik walked through those damn Bathrooms, uncovering more square kilometers of shower stalls, sinks, and non-functioning toilets. Gradually, the whole crew of 4th. Outpost began to feel a slight paranoia again. How could we have missed such a huge structure right under our noses? Although on the other hand, it's just a few hundred old toilets… not that scary, right? Just imagine that THE DAMN TOILETS CAME TO LIFE AND ATE MICHNIK.

Boss? Why are you shouting? Is something wrong?

Everything is fine, Wierzyński. Really, nothing happened, I just spilled coffee on myself. Damn hot.

Did you mention the explorer Michnik?

I didn't mention anyone, you must have misheard.

If you think so.

Damn. Getting back to the story, Michnik was consumed by those damn toilets right before our eyes. I don’t remember much from that day. Wierzyński shot at those bastards a few times and I think he killed some of them. Thank God. When was that? Two, three days ago? Since Michnik’s death, I haven’t been able to sleep a wink. They're always lurking somewhere. Recently, they even tried to force their way through the main entrance of the 4th Outpost! And the management? The management isn’t responding. You see, in such a situation, THE DAMN MANAGEMENT DOESN’T RESPOND.

Boss!

What now, Wierzyński?

Those bastards blew up the western entrance! We need to barricade ourselves here and wait for our boss to send reinforcements.

You can't tell me what to do, Wierzyński. I’m not going to hide from any bloodthirsty toilets. Grab the shotgun and make use of it, damn it.

Yes, boss. Just… boss are you recording something?

It's no longer important, Wierzyński. Marysia, remember that I love you. It's time to smash some toilets.

[Ending Recording]

The report published below was prepared a few months after the resumption of the activities of the 4th. Outpost. The mentioned deficiencies in the documentation regarding the Level PL-4 could not be supplemented due to the almost complete wiping of the PRKWM archives before the conversion of the organization into the contemporary Committee.

+ Research Report
- Research Report

Research Report: June 1990

I've got no idea what the previous crew of this facility was doing, but they certainly did not fulfill tehir duties as the explorers and researchers of this place. The documentation received by our team regarding Level PL-4 is incomplete and full of gaps and ambiguities. I don't know if it's a matter of the current administration's actions or the incompetence of our predecessors who worked here before us, but they should be clarified immediately, and all documents and reports regarding to the Level PL-4 should be reviewed.

However, moving on to the main section of the report, a few hours ago, a team of technicians tasked with examining the composition of the local air made an interesting, but worrying, discovery. Most parameters match those from the Frontrooms, but along with the oxygen and nitrogen, trace amounts of an unknown gas are present in the air in Level PL-4. We do not yet know its exact impact on organisms or other entities; in this regard, the gas is currently being studied in the 4th. Laboratory. The technicians have indicated that it could potentially contain hallucinogenic properties.

Please urgently fulfill my request regarding the organization of documentation, and I assure you that the 4th. Outpost will keep the management informed about the progress in the investigation of the discovered substance.

— Senior Explorer Ryszard Czarnecki
