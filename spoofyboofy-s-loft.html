Oh, don't worry, you didn't startle me too much. I'm just super flattered that you decided to take the time to pay my author page a little visit…so yeah, thanks!

Anyhoo, I'm Spoofyboofy! But you can also call me Spoofy, Boofy, or Spiffy. I'm a 23-year-old Canadian who loves hiking, tea, doodling, board games, card games, and chemistry! I also go by she/her as well.

I initially joined this site in April 2021 to write stuff in hopes that other people may enjoy it. However, I quickly became much more involved once I joined the Backrooms Discord Server where I spend my time critiquing, green-lighting, discussing ideas, and hosting Jackbox games! I'd highly recommend joining in on the fun if you're up for it!

Of course, despite all that I still have time to write! Check out some of my works (and ideas for future works):

You're still here? Well, I'm glad you chose to stay this long! It really means a lot to me…so much so that I may as well share some trade secrets of mine! Eh, if you call writing tips and tricks a trade secret, that is.

Tips and Tricks!
Knowledge is power!
  • When critiquing a writer I always tell them to read what they've written out loud. I do this for my own work as well and I'd be willing to bet you'd be surprised at the number of things you missed!
  • To back up my "read out loud" tip, pay attention to your breathing and reading speed. Chances are if you're rushing at the end of a sentence or end up gasping for air…you probably have a run-on sentence that needs to be cut down pronto!
  • Be consistent! If you decide to capitalize the word "Level" for the first half of your page but then have every other instance of "level" in lowercase…it's going to throw your reader off. And worse yet…me!
  • The "Enter" key on your keyboard is a beautiful thing. Please use it somewhat often to break up massive walls of text into lovely paragraphs. (ah…my poor eyes…)
  • Woah! Did you see a cool thing an author did in one of their entries and you really, really want to find out how they did it? Scroll down to the bottom of the page and select "+ Options" then select "Page Source". BOOM. There's how they did the thing! Just don't plagiarize, pretty please.
  • Worried that your totally original and awesome idea may have already been done by someone else? (Great minds think alike, after all!) Use this Wiki's search bar to search up some keywords that may match with what you plan on writing.
  • Still stuck on something despite looking at every guide you could find? Ask your fellow site members! Most of us would be more than happy to help you out! Including me! ^_^
